# Terminology

> **Out-of-character notice:** This is a reference glossary of terms used on CHOMPstation and in the broader SS13 community.

---

**Admin** -- Server administrator. Red-orange OOC text. Enforces rules and runs events. Moderators perform a similar function with limited admin powers and pale blue OOC text.

**Adminhelp** -- Command (`F1`) for sending a private message to admins. Use for reporting rule violations. All adminhelp messages are logged even when no admins are online. Do not use OOC for this.

**AI** -- The station Artificial Intelligence. Controls station machinery from the AI Core. A playable role with its own law constraints. See [AI Overview](../Systems/Science/AIOverview.md).

**Analyzer** -- Most commonly a health analyzer used in medical triage. Can also refer to atmospheric analyzers, botany plant analyzers, or the R&D destructive analyzer.

**Atmos / Atmos Tech** -- The Atmospherics area of Engineering, or an Atmospheric Technician working in it.

**Beepsky / Officer Beepsky** -- A securitron stationed in Security. Chases, stuns, and handcuffs anyone flagged for arrest in the security records.

**Blob** -- A spreading organism that consumes oxygen, causes structural damage, and attacks crew. Can appear as a major random event. Fire is the primary countermeasure.

**Bolt / Bolted** -- Airlock bolts lock a door in its current position (open or closed). Bolts can be lifted by the AI or by hacking the airlock wiring.

**Bridge** -- The command center of the station.

**Brig** -- Security holding area with individual cells. See [Security Operations](../Systems/Security/SecurityOperations.md).

**BYOND** -- The game engine SS13 runs on. Required to play.

**CentCom / Central Command** -- NanoTrasen's administrative authority over the station. Sends periodic announcements. Also refers to the destination of the emergency escape shuttle.

**CO2** -- Carbon dioxide. Invisible gas in black canisters. Suffocating at elevated concentrations. A small percentage occurs naturally in breathable air.

**Cryo** -- Cryogenic tubes in Medbay. Used to stabilize patients, treat genetic damage, or complete cloning procedures.

**Deadchat / Ghost Chat** -- The chat channel accessible to dead players via the `say` command. Only visible to other dead players and observers.

**Disk** -- The Nuclear Authentication Disk. A critical item in nuclear operation events.

**Emag** -- Slang for the Cryptographic Sequencer, a traitor item that forces airlocks open and enables other unauthorized operations.

**Engine** -- The main power generation area, typically housing the supermatter crystal. Located in the aft section of the station.

**ERP** -- Erotic roleplay. Permitted on CHOMPstation within server rules.

**EVA** -- Extra-Vehicular Activity. Also refers to the EVA Equipment storage room where space suits and breach repair supplies are kept. See [EVA](../Systems/Engineering/EVA.md).

**Ghost** -- The observer state entered after death. Ghosts can move freely, see all areas, and communicate on deadchat, but cannot interact with the world. See [Ghost](Ghost.md).

**Gib** -- The remains left when a living being is violently destroyed. Results from certain explosions or extreme damage.

**Gimmick** -- A player or round built around a specific theme or premise. Entertaining occasionally; repetitive gimmicks are considered poor form.

**Griefing / Griefer** -- Deliberately ruining the round for other players without roleplay justification. Examples: killing without in-world cause, station sabotage, arson, persistent harassment. A bannable offense.

**Hacking** -- Using wirecutters or a multitool to bypass security on airlocks, APCs, and other machinery. See [Wiring Reference](../Systems/Engineering/Hacking.md).

**HoP** -- Head of Personnel. Manages crew assignments and station access.

**HoS** -- Head of Security. Commands the security department.

**Hulk** -- The genetic superpower that turns a character green, grants immunity to stuns, and allows punching through walls. Rarely appears without admin involvement.

**IC** -- In Character. Information and actions that exist within the fiction of the setting. See also: OOC.

**IC in OOC (Icky Ocky)** -- Using the OOC chat channel to communicate information about in-progress round events. A rules violation. Use adminhelp to report incidents; keep round information out of OOC.

**Internals** -- A gas mask and air or oxygen tank worn together to breathe in vacuum or contaminated atmospheres. See [The Basics](TheBasics.md) for setup.

**Ion Storm** -- A moderate random event that alters the AI's law interpretation. Command should monitor AI behavior during storms.

**Lag** -- Server or client delay causing noticeable gaps between input and result. Atmos simulation during fires or breaches is a common cause of server-side lag.

**Laws** -- The rules the AI and Cyborgs operate under. Can be modified via the AI Upload Terminal. See [NanoTrasen Standard AI Law Set](../Systems/Science/AILaws.md) and [AI Law Sets Catalogue](../Systems/Science/AILawSets.md).

**Lockdown** -- Individual fire alarms or shutters securing an area, or the AI bolting all doors. Can also refer to AI's `Lockdown` verb triggering all fire alerts simultaneously.

**Mass Driver** -- A powered catapult mechanism. Found in Toxins (launches test bombs into the range) and the Chapel (ejects items into space).

**Metagaming** -- Using out-of-character knowledge to influence in-character decisions. A bannable offense. See [A Crash Course in Roleplaying](CrashCourseRP.md).

**N2** -- Nitrogen. Invisible inert gas in red canisters. A standard component of breathable air.

**N2O** -- Nitrous oxide. White gas in red-striped canisters. Causes unconsciousness and suffocation at sufficient concentrations.

**NIF** -- Nanite Implant Framework. An implant system that provides crew abilities via purchasable software. See [Nanite Implant Framework](../Systems/Science/NIF.md).

**Nuke** -- The Nuclear Fission Explosive used in nuclear operation events. Also refers to the event itself. Events of this type occur only under admin direction.

**O2** -- Oxygen. Invisible gas in blue and white canisters. Necessary for respiration; can cause damage at very high concentrations.

**OOC** -- Out of Character. Information or communication that exists outside the in-world fiction. The `ooc` command broadcasts to all players. See also: IC.

**OOC in IC (Ocky Icky)** -- The inverse of IC in OOC. Referencing game mechanics, meta-features, or OOC conversations in character speech or emotes. Breaks immersion and should be avoided.

**PDA** -- Personal Data Assistant. A handheld device acting as pager, flashlight, and tool depending on installed cartridge.

**Permadeath / PD** -- Permanent retirement of a character. Applied as an OOC administrative consequence or as an IC outcome in antagonist events. See [Permanent Death](PermanentDeath.md).

**Phoron** -- Purple gas in orange canisters. Toxic and highly flammable. Used in the supermatter engine and research. Also called biotoxin in some contexts.

**Pray** -- The in-game `pray` command. Sends an IC-framed message to admins. May receive an in-character divine response if admins are available.

**RCD** -- Rapid Construction Device. Quickly builds and demolishes floors, walls, and airlocks. Cannot affect reinforced walls.

**RD** -- Research Director. Head of the Science department.

**Records** -- Three types: Employment, Medical, and Security. Accessible via station terminals. Set during character creation and visible to appropriate department heads in-game.

**Robust / Robusting** -- The in-game combat system. To "robust" someone is to fight and defeat them. Robusting without IC cause is considered griefing.

**Roleplaying** -- Acting as your character within the fiction of the setting. The opposite of metagaming. See [A Crash Course in Roleplaying](CrashCourseRP.md).

**Shuttle** -- Most often refers to the Emergency Escape Shuttle or Transfer Shuttle. Can also mean the Arrivals Shuttle, Supply Shuttle, or the mining and research shuttles.

**Signal / Default Signal** -- A Remote Signaling Device broadcast. Default signal is frequency 147.9, code 30.

**Soulcatcher** -- An NIF software module that preserves crew minds for backup and recall. See [Soulcatcher](Soulcatcher.md).

**SS13** -- Space Station 13. The game.

**Syndicate** -- In mechanical terms, the faction behind traitor and nuclear event antagonists. In-world, there is no single organization called "the Syndicate" -- it represents a coalition of rival corporations and criminal groups. Characters would not use this term to describe what they have witnessed. See [The Syndicate](../Organizations/Syndicate.md).

**Tabling** -- Using the grab mechanic to place a person onto a table, briefly incapacitating them. Also used to move someone onto a surgical table.

**Tele** -- Usually the hand teleporter (traitor item). Can also refer to the full-size teleporter in the Teleporter room.

**Thermals** -- Optical Thermal Scanners, a traitor item allowing vision through walls but not floors.

**Traitor** -- A player role assigned as an antagonist in certain event rounds. The traitor works against station interests through theft, sabotage, or assassination. Do not assume someone is a traitor based on witnessing a single suspicious act -- your character does not have that framing.

**Vore** -- Vorarephilia. The server's primary focus. Common terms: **OV** (oral vore), **AV** (anal vore), **BV** (breast vore), **CV** (cock vore), **TV** (tail vore), **UB** (unbirthing), **Endo** (non-digestion release after a period inside), **Soft vore** (swallowed whole, no damage, may or may not include digestion), **Hard vore** (prey sustains damage or mutilation), **SV** (soul vore -- soul consumed or transferred). **Pred** and **prey** refer to the consuming and consumed parties respectively. See [Vore Overview](VoreOverview.md).

**Wormhole** -- A random-teleportation event or feature. Contact sends the victim to a random location on or off the station.

**You Hear a Voice in Your Head...** -- A message from an admin delivered through the in-game prayer system. Treat it as in-character divine communication.

**Wizard** -- An antagonist game mode or event in which a player commands powerful spells. Rare; appears only under admin direction.

**Z-Level** -- A discrete area of the game world. The station, telecommunications satellite, mining site, CentCom, and derelict all occupy separate Z-levels. Falling off the edge of one transitions to another, usually randomly.

**ZAS** -- Zone Atmospherics System. The atmospheric simulation engine inherited from Baystation12.
