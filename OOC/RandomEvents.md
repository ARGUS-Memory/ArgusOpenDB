# Random Events

Random events occur throughout a shift to introduce unpredictable conditions. They are categorised into three independent tiers -- Mundane, Moderate, and Major -- each running on its own timer. An event in one tier does not reset the timers for the other two.

---

## Mundane Events

Low-impact events. Most are easily resolved or self-resolving.

**PDA Spam** -- Commercial messaging broadcasts to crew PDAs. Disabled by default.

**Lottery Victory** -- A crew member has won the lottery. Results are posted to the nearest newscaster. NanoTrasen retains the funds for security reasons.

**Account Hacker** -- A brute-force intrusion against a station account is detected. The account must be suspended before funds are lost.

**Mundane News** -- Distant news items of little operational relevance populate station newscasters.

**Trivial News** -- Vague headline bulletins with minimal information content.

**Economic News** -- Supply market fluctuations reported by the Nyx Daily. Affects supply crate prices and mineral sale values.

**Lost Carp** -- One to six space carp separated from their group enter the station's proximity.

**Brand Intelligence** -- A viral advertising program infects vending machines, causing them to throw products at passersby. Spreads if not fixed.

**Vermin Infestation** -- Spiderlings, mice, or lizards have bred somewhere on the station.

**Wallrot** -- Fungal growth spreads across interior walls, degrading them. Engineering should address it before structural integrity is affected.

**Space Dust** -- A minor cloud of space dust impacts exterior windows, causing cosmetic damage.

**Camera and APC Damage** -- Extended operational wear causes cameras or APCs to fail and require engineering repair.

---

## Moderate Events

More significant events requiring a departmental response.

**Carp School** -- Twelve to sixteen space carp approach the station in small groups.

**Rogue Drone** -- A perimeter drone is malfunctioning and attacking crew. Requires security intervention.

**Meteor Shower** -- A meteor field intersects the station's orbital path. Crew should move to central areas. The meteor shield, if installed, mitigates damage.

**Communication Blackout** -- Station communication relays go offline. Crew must rely on local radio ranges until restored.

**Prison Break** -- Brig door mechanisms fail, releasing prisoners into the corridor. Security must contain the breakout.

**Grid Check** -- NanoTrasen conducts a power grid audit, temporarily interrupting station power.

**Electrical Storm** -- An electrical event shatters light fixtures across the station, causing widespread darkness.

**Radiation Storm** -- The station passes through a radiation zone. Crew should shelter in maintenance shafts or dormitories until the storm passes.

**Appendicitis** -- A crew member's appendix has become dangerously inflamed. Medical must perform surgery before rupture.

**Viral Infection** -- A crew member has contracted a virus. Virology or medical staff should assess and contain the infection.

**Spider Infestation** -- Spiderlings from an earlier infestation have matured and are attacking crew. Security is required.

**Ion Storm** -- An ion storm alters the AI's law interpretation for the duration. Command should monitor AI behaviour closely. See [Standard Operating Procedure](../Systems/Command/StandardOperatingProcedure.md) for alert-level guidance.

**Space Dust (Severe)** -- A denser dust cloud causes significant hull damage to exterior surfaces.

---

## Major Events

Station-wide emergencies requiring coordinated response.

**Carp Invasion** -- A large carp group, including adults, invades the station. Numbers too high to count individually.

**Viral Outbreak** -- A particularly virulent and lethal pathogen spreads through the station. Quarantine protocols should be initiated.

**Blob** -- A blob organism has established itself on the station. It spreads rapidly and consumes oxygen. Fire is the primary countermeasure.

**Meteor Wave** -- A dense meteor belt impact. Crew should retreat to the station core. Explosion damage will be widespread.

**Space Vines** -- Radiation-mutated vines grow through the station, trapping crew and blocking movement. Scythes, goats, or fire will clear them.
