# Permanent Death

> **Out-of-character notice:** This document describes server policy on character death and bans. These are out-of-character mechanics and administrative rules.

CHOMPstation allows characters who are killed to be restored through resleeving. Permanent death (permadeath) is not a standard gameplay outcome. It is applied only in specific circumstances.

---

## Standard Death

Execution handed down through Corporate Regulations is **round-scoped only**. Characters executed in-round return normally in the next session. Corporate Regulations references to "execution" as a sentence should be read in this context.

---

## Permanent Death Circumstances

### OOC Punishment

Permadeath may be issued as an administrative consequence for a player running a character that violates server rules and who refuses to rework the character. In this case, a **character ban** is issued: the specific character is prohibited from appearing again, while the player continues playing other characters or on other jobs as normal.

Ignoring a character ban is grounds for a full OOC ban.

### Antagonist Event Characters

When playing an antagonistic role during a staff-run event -- pirates during a pirate raid, hostile agents, and similar -- death or capture may result in permanent death as an IC consequence. The character is removed from play as a result of in-world events.

For this reason: **do not use a character you are emotionally attached to as an event antagonist.** Use a throwaway character for these roles.

---

## Player-Initiated Permanent Death

Players may choose to permanently retire or kill their own characters at any time and for any reason. This is a personal choice and is not enforced by administration. Admins will not support enforcement of player-to-player agreements about temporary or permanent character death -- these arrangements are between the players involved and carry no administrative weight.

---

## Temporary Kills and Suspensions

A **tempkill (TK)** or **suspension** applies when a character would realistically be barred from returning to the station for a finite period -- due to serious SOP violations, certain crimes, or in-game consequences of events.

Key points:

- If a character's identity is known to Central Command, they cannot return during an active TK even if they escaped in-game.
- TKs generally cannot exceed one real-life week.
- A successful escape from an incident where your identity is known may result in the TK being escalated to a permanent kill.
- CHOMPstation does not currently enforce temporary death or suspension for non-event characters. Self-imposed suspensions for IC consequences are a personal roleplay choice.
