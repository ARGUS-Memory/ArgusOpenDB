# Mail System

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" alt="ARGUS" width="80" align="right"/>

The station mail system handles the receipt, sorting, and delivery of personal parcels dispatched from Central Command. Mail arrives aboard the supply shuttle at regular intervals and must be manually sorted and hand-delivered to crew members by cargo personnel. Successful deliveries generate supply budget for the cargo department. This article covers how mail is generated, how it reaches crew, how to sort and deliver it, and how crew members can send their own packages.

---

## How Mail Arrives

Central Command accumulates outgoing mail over time and loads it onto the supply shuttle during each docking cycle. When the shuttle departs CentCom and returns to the station, a mail crate is included with the incoming cargo. The crate contains a mix of standard letters and larger envelopes addressed to individual crew members currently on shift.

Not all crew are eligible to receive mail. Central Command only dispatches mail to Nanotrasen employees who are active on station and confirmed present at their post. Personnel on extended leave from their consoles, visitors not on the Nanotrasen payroll, and crew operating outside standard employment classifications do not receive mail.

The ratio of standard letters to envelopes in each delivery is roughly 70 to 30. Junk mail, unsolicited correspondence from various external parties, makes up a portion of the total volume whenever the eligible recipient pool is small.

---

## Mail Types

**Standard Letters** are the most common type. Small sealed envelopes containing a single item addressed to a specific crew member. The envelope color corresponds to the recipient's department.

**Large Envelopes** are slightly bulkier and contain two items rather than one. Otherwise handled identically to standard letters.

**Junk Mail** is unsolicited correspondence: advertisements, chain letters, conspiracy pamphlets, and similar material. It has no verified recipient and no guaranteed value, but occasionally contains something useful.

**Blank Mail** is station-created mail. Any crew member can obtain a blank envelope, place an item inside, and address it to another crew member on shift. See the section below on sending mail.

---

## Mail Contents

Each piece of addressed mail contains a randomly selected item weighted toward the recipient's job. A medical officer is more likely to receive pharmaceutical supplies or surgical instruments. An engineer is more likely to receive building materials or tool components. Security personnel receive consumables appropriate to their role. Command staff receive higher-value items.

In addition to job-specific items, all crew have a chance of receiving general goods: station credits, beverages, snacks, or personal effects. Envelopes always contain two such items rather than one.

The envelope bears the recipient's department color, a postmark, and one or two decorative stamps. These are cosmetic.

## Sorting and Delivery

### Destination Tagger

The destination tagger is a handheld device carried by cargo personnel. It stores a list of all registered sorting destinations on the station. Clicking a piece of mail or a parcel with the tagger applies a routing tag to it and adds a visible overlay to the item indicating its destination.

Tagged mail placed into the disposal network is automatically routed to the correct sorting outlet. Untagged mail passes through the system without sorting.

### Pneumatic Disposal Network

The disposal network connects most departments to the central cargo sorting area via pneumatic pipe. Mail dropped into a disposal unit while tagged will travel to the destination registered in the tagger. Sorting pipe junctions read the tag and route the item accordingly.

### Manual Delivery

Once sorted, mail must be physically carried to the recipient. Cargo personnel typically use a mail bag for this purpose. The mail bag holds a large volume of letters and small parcels and is standard issue for the mailman role.

---

## Mail Scanner and Delivery Verification

The mail scanner is a belt-worn device that confirms and records deliveries. Proper use:

1. Scan the mail item to register the recipient in the scanner's active log.
2. Locate the recipient and scan them directly to verify identity.

The scanner checks that the person being scanned matches the addressee on the envelope. It will not confirm delivery to a corpse, an unresponsive crew member, or someone whose identity cannot be verified. A successful scan triggers an approval tone and credits the cargo department's supply budget with a small amount. Failed verifications produce a denial tone with no credit.

Scanning the same mail twice does not award additional credit.

---

## Opening Mail

Only the addressed recipient can open their own mail. Attempting to open mail addressed to someone else will be refused. Opening takes a brief moment and cannot be interrupted once started without cancelling the attempt. The contents drop into the recipient's hands or onto the floor if their hands are full.

---

## Sending Mail

Any crew member can send a personal package to another crew member currently on shift.

1. Obtain a blank envelope from cargo or the mail sorting area.
2. Place the item to be sent inside the envelope.
3. Seal the envelope.
4. Address it to a recipient by writing their name on the outside with a pen.

The envelope can be retrieved and reopened before sealing if the contents need to change. Once sealed and addressed it is treated as standard mail and can be delivered through the normal sorting and delivery process. The item inside is initialized for the recipient's job, meaning job-appropriate bonuses may apply when it is opened.

---

## Parcels

In addition to mail envelopes, cargo handles two types of wrapped parcels for larger items.

**Small Parcels** are handheld-sized wrapped packages. They accept destination tags from the tagger and can be labeled with a pen. They move through the disposal network like mail.

**Large Parcels** are floor-standing crates wrapped for shipping. They also accept tags and labels but must be dragged or carried rather than pocketed.

Both types are unwrapped by the recipient to retrieve the contents.

---

## Cargo Roles and Mail

**Cargo Technician** handles incoming and outgoing freight including mail. Has access to the mail sorting area, cargo bots, and the disposal network.

**Mailman** is an alternate assignment within the cargo technician role focused specifically on mail delivery. Starts with a mail bag and scanner. Has maintenance access to reach all parts of the station for deliveries.

**Quartermaster** oversees the cargo department and the shuttle cycle that brings mail to the station. Manages the supply budget that delivery credits accumulate toward.

---

## Supply Budget

Each verified mail delivery adds a small amount to the cargo department's supply budget. This budget is used to purchase additional cargo shipments from Central Command. Consistent, accurate delivery is the primary way cargo contributes to the station's operational budget outside of mining operations.
