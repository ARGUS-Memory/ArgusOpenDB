# Particle Accelerator

The **particle accelerator** (PA) is a multi-component physics apparatus that fires charged particle beams. In the engineering context it is used to energise the supermatter crystal or the tesla containment orb. In the research context it is used to drive two specialist material synthesis processes: morphium production and supermatter synthesis.

For PA assembly, wiring, and operation as part of the Tesla Engine, see [Tesla Engine](../Engineering/TeslaEngine.md). For PA wiring reference, see [Wiring Reference](../Engineering/Hacking.md#particle-accelerator).

---

## Specialist Material Synthesis

The following synthesis procedures require a **modified gravitational singularity drive** (a standard singularity drive fitted with a superconductive transmission coil), a particle accelerator, and an emitter. All synthesis work should be conducted in a safe external environment away from populated station sections. The particle accelerator must be assembled, wired, and scanned for parts before use.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Radiation-proof equipment must be worn throughout both procedures. All synthesis involves ionising radiation at levels hazardous to unprotected crew.

---

## Morphium Synthesis

Morphium is a material required for advanced research fabrication.

### Materials

- Verdantium (loaded into the modified singularity drive)
- Alternatively: plasteel and alien salvage material

### Procedure

1. Construct the modified singularity drive and particle accelerator at a safe external site.
2. Load verdantium into the modified singularity drive.
3. Activate an emitter and direct its beam at the verdantium inside the drive.
4. Simultaneously activate the particle accelerator and direct the particle beam at the drive.
5. Monitor the material. Once the verdantium turns black, the conversion is complete.
6. Deactivate both the emitter and the particle accelerator.
7. Collect the resulting morphium.

The same result can be achieved using plasteel and alien salvage material in place of verdantium.

## Supermatter Synthesis

Supermatter is a rare material required for the highest tier of research fabrication. The synthesis process requires precise environmental control and carries a low success probability per attempt.

> [!CAUTION]
> Supermatter synthesis has an approximately 1% success rate per attempt. Multiple attempts should be expected before a successful yield is obtained.

### Materials

- Liquid phoron (minimum 10 units)
- Poly acid (minimum 10 units, equal volume to liquid phoron)
- Solid phoron
- Valhollide
- A second beaker containing 300 units of liquid phoron

### Procedure

1. Construct the modified singularity drive and particle accelerator in an isolated external environment.
2. Cool the environment around the singularity drive to between 1 and 100 kelvin.
3. Insert a beaker containing at least 10 units each of liquid phoron and poly acid in equal volumes into the drive.
4. Add solid phoron to the drive.
5. Activate the particle accelerator and fire at the drive until the phoron-poly acid reaction occurs.
6. Remove the beaker from the drive once the reaction completes.
7. Insert the valhollide into the drive.
8. Insert the second beaker containing 300 units of liquid phoron.
9. Raise the temperature of the environment around the drive to between 3,000 and 10,000 kelvin.
10. Fire the particle accelerator at the drive until supermatter is produced.

Each firing is an independent attempt at approximately 1% probability. The emitter and environment heating must be sustained between firing cycles.
