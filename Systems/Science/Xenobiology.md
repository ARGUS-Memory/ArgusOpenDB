# Xenobiology

The xenobiology laboratory studies extraterrestrial life forms, with particular focus on slimes: amorphous organisms of uncertain origin that feed by engulfing prey and exhibit remarkable genetic plasticity. Xenobiologists maintain slime populations, guide mutations to produce desired variants, harvest and process slime cores, and conduct research into the applications of extracted biological compounds.

The xenobiology laboratory is also the primary staging point for xenoarchaeological recovery operations, covered in the second section of this article.

---

## Slimes

### Basics

Slimes are motile, predatory organisms that feed on living matter. They track prey by proximity, engulf targets by anchoring and consuming them over time, and reproduce by splitting into offspring once they have accumulated sufficient nutrition. A newborn slime is small and weak; a well-fed adult is twice as large, twice as durable, and significantly more dangerous.

All slimes maintained in the xenobiology lab should be kept behind secured containment. Uncontained slimes will attack any non-allied organism, including station crew.

### Nutrition and Growth

A slime's hunger decreases continuously over time. If nutrition falls below zero the slime begins taking starvation damage. When nutrition exceeds its maximum, each additional feeding unit increments an internal growth counter. When the growth counter reaches 10, the slime is ready to reproduce.

Slimes can be fed monkey cubes, raw meat, or live prey. Grey slime cores injected with blood produce monkey cubes in quantity, providing a sustainable food supply.

### Reproduction and Mutation

An adult slime with sufficient accumulated feeding will split into five offspring, deleting the parent. If a xenobiologist is controlling a slime at the time of reproduction, their control transfers to one of the offspring.

Each reproduction event has roughly a one-in-three chance of producing a mutation. On a successful roll, the offspring may mutate into one of the variants listed in the slime's mutation table. Rarely, one in approximately thirty reproduction events produces a rainbow slime regardless of the parent's colour. On a failed roll, all offspring inherit the parent's colour.

Mutation chance is inherited by offspring and can be increased through the Red slime extract mutator compound.

## Xenoarchaeology

### Overview

Xenoarchaeological digs recover artefacts and remains from layered subsurface deposits. Recovery operations are conducted at generated dig sites using specialised excavation equipment. Each dig site is categorised by its apparent origin, which influences the type of artefacts recovered.

### Dig Site Categories

| Category | Character |
|---|---|
| Garden | Peaceful or cultivated origin |
| Midden | Refuse and waste deposits |
| House | Residential or domestic structures |
| Technical | Engineering or industrial facilities |
| Temple | Religious or ceremonial sites |
| War | Conflict and military deposits |

### Artefact Types

Artefacts span 41 recognised categories. Common finds include bowls, urns, statuettes, instruments, knives, coins, tools, crystals, pens, and metal sheet deposits. Less common finds include weapons (bladed, energy-based, and projectile), fossilised remains (humanoid, mechanical, and biological), and electronics components.

Rare and anomalous artefacts include soul stones, spellbooks, teleportation devices, alien spacecraft components, and advanced circuitry of unknown manufacture. Approximately 30 to 40 percent of statuettes, crystals, instruments, boxes, and stockpart caches carry anomalous properties.

### Artefact Effects

Anomalous artefacts carry one of 24 documented effect categories. Effects may trigger on proximity, contact, or pulse, and many require a specific activation stimulus (water, acid, volatile reagent, toxin, physical force, energy, heat, or cold). Documented effect categories include: animation of nearby objects, berserk induction, EMP generation, forcefield projection, gravity disruption, hallucination, localised plant growth, radiation emission, resurrection, sleep induction, stun, targeted teleportation, vampiric drain, and health or power regeneration.

Unidentified artefacts should be brought to the science lab for scanning before further handling. Contact with an unknown artefact without prior analysis carries significant risk.

### Recovery Equipment

Standard xenoarcheological recovery uses brush sets and chisels to carefully expose buried material. An archaeology scanner identifies the depth and composition of layers at a dig site. Sonic tools such as the jackhammer are capable of rapid excavation but destroy fragile artefacts on contact and should not be used at active dig sites.
