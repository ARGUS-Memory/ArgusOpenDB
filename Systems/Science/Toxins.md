# Toxins Research

The Toxins Research division occupies a dedicated section of the science department with a reinforced mixing chamber, gas storage, and a mass driver testing range. The division produces explosive ordnance through controlled phoron combustion, and provides the station with demolition capability, singularity containment failsafe munitions, and experimental pyrotechnic research.

---

## Principle of Operation

Phoron ignites at approximately 100 degrees Celsius (373 kelvin). When a heated phoron and oxygen mixture is combined in a tank transfer valve and the valve is triggered, the compressed gases react, producing an explosion. Explosion yield scales with gas temperature, pressure, and the ratio of the reactants. The reaction does not require extreme heat to produce a functional yield; a mixture at or just above the phoron flashpoint produces a controlled, testable explosion.

> [!CAUTION]
> If the gas temperature in the mixing chamber exceeds 5,000 kelvin, the reinforced chamber walls begin to degrade. This process cannot be stopped once started. Maintain temperatures well below this threshold during heating operations.

---

## Heating Phoron

1. Ensure the mixing chamber is completely empty before starting. Connect a portable air pump to the chamber's atmospheric port, set direction to inward, and run until all residual gas is evacuated. Close all valves and blast doors when done.
2. Bring 2 phoron canisters and 1 oxygen canister into the mixing chamber.
3. Connect the first phoron canister to the central floor port. Open the manual valve to allow gas to flow into the chamber. Fill the chamber with two full canisters of phoron. Close the valve and disconnect each canister once empty.
4. Connect the oxygen canister to the lower port. Open the valve briefly -- 1 to 2 seconds is sufficient. The goal is a very small oxygen fraction. Too much oxygen produces runaway heat; too little produces insufficient combustion. More oxygen can always be added, but excess cannot be removed without clearing the chamber.
5. Ignite the mixing chamber. The phoron ignites and burns until the oxygen is consumed.
6. While combustion is ongoing, connect an empty yellow transfer canister to the lower port.
7. Once the fire has burned out, pump a small quantity of the resulting gas mix into the transfer canister. Check the canister temperature before proceeding.
   - If temperature is at or above 200 degrees Celsius: the phoron is sufficiently heated. Proceed to assembly.
   - If temperature is below 200 degrees Celsius: pump the mix back into the chamber, add another small oxygen increment, and repeat the ignition cycle.
8. Close all transfer valves and disconnect all canisters when the target temperature is achieved.

The output pump's desirable pressure setting governs flow rate. Higher settings move gas faster and are useful when filling canisters from the chamber. If the mix stops flowing into a canister below the target pressure, increase the output pressure setting.

---

## Assembly

Once hot phoron is available in the transfer canister:

1. Obtain empty phoron tanks from the tank dispenser. Load each into the air scrubber adjacent to the dispenser to purge any residual phoron before use.
2. Connect the clean phoron tanks to the yellow transfer canister containing the heated mix. Set output pressure to the maximum (approximately 1,013 kPa) and fill each tank.
3. Obtain an equal number of oxygen tanks from the dispenser. Fill each from the oxygen canister at maximum output pressure (1,013.25 kPa).
4. Attach one phoron tank and one oxygen tank to each **tank transfer valve**. Each valve becomes an active explosive device.

---

## Triggering Mechanisms

Three trigger types are available for tank transfer valves:

**Timer:** The valve activates after a set countdown. Apply a screwdriver to the timer before attaching it to the valve.

**Proximity sensor:** Activates when a living being enters range. No preparation required before attachment.

**Remote signalling device:** Activates when the device receives a signal on its set frequency and code. Apply a screwdriver to the device before attaching it to the valve. After attachment, immediately change the frequency and code from the defaults to prevent accidental triggering by other signalers in the area.

---

## Testing

The testing range is accessed through the corridor to the right of the lab, past two sets of doors. The range contains a mass driver and observation screens.

1. Place the prepared explosive on the mass driver.
2. Arm the trigger as appropriate (set the timer, confirm signal frequency, etc.).
3. Return to the observation room and activate the mass driver.
4. Trigger the device via the observation screen's camera controls, a remote signaller, or by waiting for the timer.

The range is rated for a single detonation of moderate yield. Extremely high-yield devices may damage the test range structure.
