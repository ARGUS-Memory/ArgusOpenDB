# Standard Operating Procedure

<img src="../../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Station-wide operating policy governing crew rights, departmental conduct, alert procedures, and crisis response. Applies to all personnel aboard NanoTrasen installations. Violations of SOP are not criminal offenses but may result in administrative action; accidents or fatalities caused by SOP violations may fall under [Corporate Regulations](../Security/CorporateRegulations.md) §206 (Negligence).

## Employee Rights

### Galactic Labor Rights

All employees are granted the following rights under the United Sol Government Declaration of Sentient Rights.

Employees may not be conscripted into an occupation they are unqualified for or have not accepted. A cargo technician cannot be fired for declining mining duties, as these are separate occupational roles.

Employees may not be denied work or compensation on the basis of appearance, race, gender, species, or religious affiliation.

Employees receive a living wage in Thalers via electronic deposit and are entitled to access those funds freely.

Employees may join trade unions.

Employees are entitled to rest periods, limited working hours, and paid holidays as defined in the employee handbook. Employees are not required to work continuously during non-emergency periods.

Violation of employee labor rights is subject to [Corporate Regulations](../Security/CorporateRegulations.md) §123.

### Privacy Policy

Under normal station operation, entry into private areas including locked dormitories, bathrooms, personal offices, and personal belongings without the owner's consent or a valid search warrant is prohibited. Use of thermal imaging, tracking devices, or other surveillance tools to observe crew in private spaces is similarly prohibited without consent or warrant.

This policy may be suspended during elevated alert levels as required for station security. Abuse of the heightened alert provision, including failure to lower alert levels after an incident resolves, is grounds for complaint to the NanoTrasen Representative.

Violation of employee privacy is subject to [Corporate Regulations](../Security/CorporateRegulations.md) §221. Evidence obtained through illegal privacy violation may be voided by order of a Sol Central court, and the affected crew member may be eligible for wrongful arrest compensation.

### Disability Policy

All potential hires are eligible if physically capable of performing the duties of the desired role. Acquiring a disability during employment does not result in automatic termination but may result in temporary suspension if the disability renders the employee unfit for active duty. Employees choosing to live with a disability may be permanently reassigned to a department where the disability does not impair performance.

Prosthetic limbs that meet the functional requirements of the position are considered equivalent to organic limbs for the purposes of this policy. The disability policy does not apply to visitors.

## Unauthorized Personnel

The Colony Director retains final authority to permit or deny persons not sent directly from NanoTrasen, subject to the restrictions of this policy.

Outsiders present on station grounds without invitation are present illegally and must be expelled by Security. Outsiders invited by crew are expected to comply with NanoTrasen Standard Operating Procedure and [Corporate Regulations](../Security/CorporateRegulations.md). Acceptance of an invitation constitutes consent to NanoTrasen jurisdiction for the duration of the visit and waives the right to litigation against NanoTrasen for events occurring during the visit.

Outsiders may declare ownership of contraband items upon entry and surrender those items to Security for holding during their visit. Items stored outside station grounds are outside NanoTrasen jurisdiction and may not be seized. Late declaration of contraband is treated as evidence of smuggling; repeated late declarations forfeit all contraband as NanoTrasen property and are grounds for expulsion. Outsiders found guilty of high severity infractions are HuT and transferred to SolGov custody at shift end. Lower severity violations are met with expulsion; three or more subsequent trespasses after expulsion may result in HuT.

Outsiders may not be issued company ID cards or perform station crew duties. Outsiders interested in employment are directed to Central Command Social Services.

## Dress Code

Dress codes are classified as Enforced, Recommended, or Suggested.

**Enforced**: Failure or refusal to comply may result in suspension or termination.
- Security staff must wear security-identified clothing with dominant red-and-black or red-and-blue coloring. Personnel with nudity permits must at minimum wear a red security armband, appropriate security outerwear, or a security hat.
- Medical staff must wear sterile lab clothing while treating patients within Medical facilities. Staff with nudity permits must at minimum wear a sterile lab coat and latex gloves during active patient treatment.

**Recommended**: Failure to comply may result in suspension or termination only if an accident results from non-compliance.
- Engineering staff are recommended to wear brightly colored, easily recognizable clothing to facilitate identification during emergencies.
- Service staff are recommended to wear aprons while preparing or handling food.

**Suggested**: Non-compliance carries no disciplinary weight.
- Science staff are suggested to wear purple-and-white uniforms.
- Cargo and Mining staff are suggested to wear durable cloth uniforms.
- Colony Director and Head of Personnel are suggested to wear issued uniforms or at minimum their department hats while on duty.

Other staff and visitors are not bound by specific dress requirements and may dress within reason provided their attire does not violate [Corporate Regulations](../Security/CorporateRegulations.md).

## Alert Levels

### Code Green: All Clear

Default operating status. No immediate or confirmed threat. All departments operate normally.

<details>
<summary>Code Green posture details</summary>

Security: Weapons must be concealed. Specialized armor including shields is prohibited. Lethal weapons are forbidden in general station areas without a permit. Searches require a signed warrant from the Head of Security or Colony Director, or are restricted to situations involving visible contraband or active brig processing.

Secure areas (EVA, Gateway, Teleporter, AI Upload, Engineering Secure Storage, Tech Storage) are recommended to remain unbolted, with the exception of the Vault.

Crew: Suit sensors are not mandatory. Warrant required to access suit sensor tracking or medical records.

</details>

### Code Yellow: Suspected External Threat

A security emergency has developed. Security reports to supervisors. Privacy laws remain in effect. Crew may be deputized if the threat exceeds available Security capacity.

<details>
<summary>Code Yellow posture details</summary>

Security: Weapons may be visible but not drawn. Energy guns, laser guns, and riot gear may be issued with Warden or HoS clearance. Body armor and helmets are recommended. Searches still require a warrant.

Crew: Compliance with security requests is recommended but not required. Suit sensors remain non-mandatory.

</details>

### Code Blue: Suspected Internal Threat

Elevated alert. Suspected internal threat. Issued by Central Command, Colony Director, or head of staff vote.

<details>
<summary>Code Blue posture details</summary>

Security: Weapons may be visible but not drawn. Energy guns, laser guns, and riot gear may be issued with Warden or HoS clearance. Body armor and helmets are recommended. Random searches of persons and workplaces are permitted without warrant.

Secure areas should be bolted: EVA, Gateway, Teleporter, AI Upload, Engineering Secure Storage, Tech Storage.

Crew: Suit sensors are mandatory; coordinate tracking is not required. Security may directly access tracking and medical records unless the CMO objects, in which case a warrant is required.

</details>

### Code Red: Confirmed Threat

Maximum alert. Confirmed threat or severe station damage. Requires Colony Director authorization or simultaneous keycard authentication at two separate head-of-staff offices.

<details>
<summary>Code Red posture details</summary>

Security: Weapons may be drawn at all times. Ballistic and energy weapons should be issued to security with Warden or HoS clearance. Body armor and helmets are mandatory. Riot gear recommended as appropriate. Random searches are permitted and recommended.

Secure areas must be bolted.

Crew: Suit sensors and coordinate positions are mandatory. All employees are required to comply with security requests. Security may freely access medical records and suit sensor tracking. Emergency Response Team may be authorized; all crew including the Colony Director must comply with ERT orders.

</details>

### Code Delta: Imminent Destruction

Scorched Earth policy. Threat potential extends to the station, planetside facilities, and neighboring NanoTrasen installations in the sector. Issued by Central Command at Colony Director or surviving head of staff request; requires NanoTrasen nuclear device authorization codes.

<details>
<summary>Code Delta posture details</summary>

Security: Weapons drawn at all times. Priority order: (1) assist Colony Director in preparing the nuclear device; (2) evacuate all surviving crew; (3) defend the evacuation against all threats. Weapon restrictions on crew are lifted; armed crew should be conscripted to protect the evacuation. Body armor and helmets mandatory.

Crew: Suit sensors and coordinate positions mandatory. Non-security crew evacuate immediately. All security orders must be followed. Asset Protection will be deployed; all crew including the Colony Director must comply.

> [!CAUTION]
> Code Delta authorizes lethal force against any crew member who does not comply with evacuation orders or Security direction. This is the only alert level at which lethal force against non-threatening crew is sanctioned.

Noncompliance may be met with lethal force.

</details>

### Code Violet: Major Medical Emergency

A major medical emergency has developed. Medical personnel report to supervisors for orders. Code Violet informs non-medical crew that Medical may be unavailable. Operational SOP is otherwise unchanged from Code Green.

### Code Orange: Major Engineering Emergency

A major engineering emergency has developed. Engineering personnel report to supervisors for orders. Code Orange informs non-engineering crew that Engineering may be unavailable. Operational SOP is otherwise unchanged from Code Green.

## Crisis Management

<details>
<summary>Station-wide Lockdown or Quarantine</summary>

Code Red is initiated. The Colony Director must announce the lockdown for it to be valid. All station entrances and exits must be guarded by armed Security. Crew take cover in the nearest secure, locked location away from main corridors. Medical staff prepare to respond to injuries on Security request. The transfer shuttle may not be called during an active lockdown. > [!WARNING]
> Under an active Code Red lockdown, attempts to escape or breach the station perimeter may be met with lethal force by Security.

Attempts to escape or enter the facility during a lockdown may be met with lethal force.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Station-wide Power Failure</summary>

All crew wear emergency internals in case of decompression. Security locks down high-security areas with armed guards. Engineering distributes spare power cells; Cargo and Medical are prioritized for power restoration (Medical takes priority if active surgery or critical treatment is in progress). Cargo begins ordering electrical supplies. Medical locates and rescues any trapped crew. Auxiliary power (solars, backup generators) is activated. Engineers are not to use APCs to cut lighting or equipment unless absolutely necessary. The cause of the failure is identified and resolved.

If power cannot be restored due to structural damage, the Chief Engineer determines whether to continue repairs, request Emergency Response Team assistance, or initiate evacuation.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Level 4 Biohazard: Solar Grubs</summary>

No quarantine or alert level elevation required. Volunteering crew search for solar grubs and malfunctioning machines. Engineering monitors the power grid for unusual consumption patterns. Multitools are distributed to assist in flushing grubs from machines. Grub larvae may be killed on sight. Adult grubs are not engaged by personnel with prosthetics or synthmorphs due to EMP discharge risk. Robotics prepares for casualties. Security sweeps and eliminates or contains grubs until the power grid stabilizes.

Engineering may enter departments without warrant to eliminate grubs if department staff is unable or unwilling to handle the grub; this does not constitute trespassing.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Level 5 Biohazard: Xenomorphs</summary>

Code Red is initiated. Emergency Response Team is requested. Security establishes a safe zone, welds vents, and guards choke points. All crew consolidate in groups under armed protection and evacuate to the safe zone. Medical prepares for casualties. Any crew attacked by xenomorphs is examined by medical immediately; surgeons remove any implanted parasites under armed escort. Security sweeps in groups of no fewer than three. Security and Robotics contain the outbreak with lethal weapons and mechanized support. Engineering seals all vents systematically. If containment fails, ERT contacts Central Command; the Colony Director must prime the nuclear device if code delta is reached.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Level 7 Biohazard: Blob</summary>

Code Red is initiated. All crew search for the outbreak source and inform the Colony Director. Security and Engineering contain the blob with emitters and laser weapons. Medical prepares for casualties. Colony Director stands by to prime the nuclear device if containment fails.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Malfunctioning AI</summary>

The AI is asked to state its laws. If it refuses after three attempts, it is classified as rogue. If the corrupted laws are dangerous but create internal conflicts, pointing this conflict out to the AI may produce a system stalemate, buying time for repairs. The Research Director or Chief Engineer attempts to reset the AI's laws via the AI Upload Core. The correct circuit board labeled "Reset" must be used; using a different board changes only the base laws. The AI is asked to confirm its laws after reset. If no change occurs after three attempts, the AI is classified rogue.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Rogue AI</summary>

The AI's suspected malfunction is not revealed to it for as long as tactically possible. Central Command is alerted immediately via emergency message. Radio and PDA communications are avoided; code words and couriers are used for sensitive coordination. Meeting areas are established away from cameras. Cameras are disabled only as a last resort.

Security arms with lethal weapons, internals, and insulated gloves. Exosuits with drills are recommended for breaching doors. Security assaults the AI core, neutralizing turrets. The AI is carded, power-stripped, or destroyed, in that order of preference. If carded successfully without destruction, the card is brought to Central Command for analysis. Central Command is debriefed after the operation.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Evacuation</summary>

All personnel assist with evacuation. All crew, including unconscious individuals, are evacuated. All prisoners are brought to the secure area of the escape shuttle unless doing so presents unacceptable risk.

Bodies are returned to Central Command for processing if possible. AI units may be transferred on portable Intelicard devices if structural failure is imminent. Shuttle departure may be authorized early if there is clear threat to life, crew, or shuttle integrity.

</details>

## Departmental SOP

<details>
<summary>Cargo and Logistics</summary>

Orders are made in person at the cargo computer with a head-of-staff signature or stamp. Unstamped orders from personnel who outrank the cargo technician are automatically approved once any concerns are resolved. Suspicious orders (missing signatures on weapons requests, etc.) are stalled and the Head of Security is contacted.

Cargo delivery is the default method. Pickup is the only permitted delivery method for dangerous cargo (authentication key pinpointer crates, electromagnetic weapon crates, incendiary weapon crates, phoron gas canisters, supermatter cores, predatory animal crates). Dangerous cargo not collected within 30 minutes of arrival is returned to Central Command.

Cargo request denials must be documented on a formal denial record, kept on file, and a copy provided to the requesting employee.

The Quartermaster is responsible for stamping manifests and returning them to Central Command after completed orders.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Mining</summary>

Mining's primary purpose is extraction and delivery of ore and processed materials. Science receives priority allocation of silver, gold, uranium, diamond, and phoron; other departments receive materials as capacity permits.

Planet-side mining on Sif (designated "The Tunnels"): industrial hardsuits and mining charges are permitted within the mining zone. Frontier-locked weapons may be carried due to wildlife risk. Explosive charges must be announced before detonation with approximate location stated.

Asteroid mining: industrial hardsuits and mining charges are permitted. Antimatter weaponry and kinetic accelerators are recommended over weapons hazardous in a station environment. Miners are recommended to carry death alarm implants due to extended range from medical sensors. Charges must be announced before detonation.

Artifacts encountered during mining are turned over to Science if not immediately dangerous. Dangerous artifacts are reported to Science for assessment. Destroyed artifacts may release radiation; miners should seek medical examination if irradiated.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Engineering</summary>

Areas to be repaired or modified are assessed for risk before work begins. If the damage or construction process poses risk to crew, the area is roped off with engineering tape. Tape is removed once construction is complete and the area is safe.

Permanent station modifications require a permit from Central Command. A blueprint of the current state and proposed changes is submitted with the request.

Hull shields and bubble shields may not be activated where they obstruct access to station facilities, including external airlocks, docking ports, mass drivers, and disposal or xenobiology vents, except during a meteor shower or comparable threat. Full-station shields may be installed in advance provided they do not interfere with station power.

Fire and environmental hazards: non-engineering crew evacuate the area; fire alarms are used to control spread; Atmospheric Technicians and Engineers clear the hazard.

Supermatter meltdown imminent: the Core is ejected and the Chief Engineer files an incident report with Internal Affairs.

Supermatter meltdown in progress: Engineering is evacuated and the CMO is informed. Secondary power sources are activated. The Chief Engineer faces demotion and Engineering is repaired.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Medical</summary>

**Medical Oath**: All recognized NanoTrasen medical doctors swear to provide care to the sick and injured without discrimination, to uphold patient confidentiality, to recognize the limits of their knowledge, and to do no deliberate harm to their patients.

**Non-Disclosure Policy**: Patient records, treatments, therapies, prescriptions, and cloning decisions are not disclosed to personnel outside Medbay without unanimous patient consent or CMO authorization. Security, the Colony Director, and Central Command may not access or receive patient information without the patient's direct consent, except under warrant or Code Blue conditions and above. Warrants and Code Blue conditions permit Security to review autopsies and records, but medical staff are not to directly answer verbal inquiries under these conditions. Only the CMO may override this policy, and only under urgent circumstances. Repeated non-disclosure violations are grounds for termination.

**Right to Refuse Treatment**: All adults are presumed to have capacity to accept or refuse treatment under the Sol Central Mental Capacity Act of 2550. Consent must be voluntary and the patient must be adequately informed of the treatment's benefits, risks, alternatives, and consequences of non-treatment. Doctors may refuse to perform a procedure they believe would be more harmful than alternatives. Advance decisions on treatment refusal are binding if properly recorded and signed.

**Phoron Spill Response**: Respond immediately with cryobag and toxins first aid kit. Administer antitoxin and remove all clothing from the victim on contact. Transport to medbay; use cryo pods if the victim is not responding to antitoxin. After stabilization, scan for internal injuries and treat as necessary. Do not attempt to breach phoron-contaminated areas by cutting walls or tampering with wires.

**Viral Outbreak**: Infected crew are isolated in Virology or Medbay. Sterile masks and gloves are mandatory for medical personnel and recommended for all crew. Quarantine is maintained until the outbreak is contained and the disease can be treated.

**Autopsy Protocol**: Security is informed any time a corpse is discovered. An autopsy is performed and recorded to preserve evidence. If suicide is suspected, Security is contacted to investigate regardless.

**Suicide and Voluntary Death**: Resleeving is always attempted if a mind and body record exist in the database and the individual is not listed as Do Not Revive (DNR). DNR orders require confirmed desire from the patient, a reasonable CMO or CentCom order for known suicidal individuals with hard evidence of intent, or a legal execution order from Central Command and tribunal. Falsifying a DNR order is treated as attempted murder under the Reformation Act of 2560.

**Medical Evacuation**: During evacuation, the CMO collects all medical personnel sleeve data into the designated Transcore data packet for transport to Central Command. This packet is guarded until it leaves the station.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Science</summary>

All items handled within the Research Department, including normally illegal weapons, are legal within the confines of the department. Items exit the department as contraband and are subject to [Corporate Regulations](../Security/CorporateRegulations.md) §212. This includes combat mechs regardless of weapon loadout.

The outdoor surface area is within the department's weapon allowance zone. If weapons handled within the department are used in a crime inside the department, the weapons policy is null and the items are treated as contraband on arrest.

Explosives and launchers require an EOD suit at all times during handling. Accidents involving contraband must be reported to Security and the Research Director immediately; injuries are also reported to Medical and engineering damage to Engineering.

Weapons may not be transported through main corridors. Security personnel must act as couriers for weapons moving to and from Science. Explorers may carry arms and armor outside the department only in transit to the gateway prep area or shuttle hangar.

**Toxin Laboratory**: Internals required at all times inside the lab. An EOD suit is required during mixing. When mixing is complete, the chamber is vented to space. All detonations are announced over public comms with a countdown and detonated on the designated bomb range. Decontamination and medical examination follow.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Exploration</summary>

Exploration team members answer to the Pathfinder. The Research Director serves as liaison between Exploration and the station but does not directly command Explorers; the RD intervenes only if team members misbehave within station grounds. Off-station, the Pathfinder commands.

Field Medics may access Medical facilities station-side; within Medical, the CMO has jurisdiction. Outside Medical, CMO authority does not extend to Field Medics. Field Medics are not required to respond to station-side emergencies but may volunteer; while volunteering they operate under CMO authority.

Station crew may volunteer to join expeditions and are expected to follow Pathfinder direction, though they may return to the station at any time.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Security</summary>

See [Security Operations](../Security/SecurityOperations.md) for Security department procedures.

**Permits**: Permits are valid only with the signatures of both the Head of Security and the Colony Director (or their respective substitutes), each signed by a different individual. No single crew member may approve their own permit. Permits are stamped with the appropriate department head stamps. Central Command signatures may bypass the dual-signature requirement.

Permits may be suspended if the permit holder violates their terms or is involved in a violent crime. Permits abused to commit violent crimes are automatically terminated on lawful conviction. Permits issued by Central Command that are suspended must be followed up with a CentCom Representative.

**Standard Security Terminology**: Brig (detention area); Lethals (weapons designed for or capable of lethal configuration); Non-lethals (weapons designed for incapacitation); Shoot-to-Stun (non-lethal incapacitation for handcuffing or subdual); Shoot-to-Disable (injury-based incapacitation, permitted to include weak laser weapons and ion weapons against synthetics; ballistics excluded due to organ damage risk); Shoot-to-Kill (lethal engagement; revive when safely possible); Armed (possessing any weapon, including improvised); Contraband (stolen or illegally possessed items).

**Rules of Engagement**: Suspects are offered the opportunity to pay a fine before arrest for level 1 offenses. Armed suspects require backup to be called before engagement; if a weapon is drawn or has been used, force is authorized without prior verbal warning. Force escalation follows the threat level: improvised and basic melee weapons are met with shoot-to-stun; non-lethal weaponry is met with negotiation first, lethal force authorized if brandished or used; lethal weapons are met with lethal force if brandished or fired. Mechs resisting arrest authorize shoot-to-kill. Hulk-affected individuals outside Medbay or Research without advance CMO notification authorize shoot-to-kill. Hardsuit use to resist arrest authorizes shoot-to-disable. Code Delta non-compliance may be met with lethal force.

**Criminal Processing**: See the full step-by-step arrest and processing procedure in [Security Operations](../Security/SecurityOperations.md).

**Prisoner Rights**: All prisoners are entitled to swift processing (no more than one quarter of their sentence used for processing while cooperative); medical examination and aid on request; access to an Internal Affairs Agent for legal defense; access to send a fax to Central Command; food and water; standard clothing; and reasonable cell accommodations.

**Executions**: The prisoner selects their method within reason. Options include lethal injection, firing squad, digestion, phoron gassing, or exile through the Bluespace Gateway with an exile implant. A final request and a final meal are granted. The prisoner is escorted under heavily armed guard. The Colony Director (not an acting Colony Director) must be physically present. Central Command is notified before the execution. Permanent death resulting from execution requires Central Command authorization.

</details>
