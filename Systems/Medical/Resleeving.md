# Resleeving

Resleeving grows a new biological body for a deceased crew member and transfers their backed-up mind record into it. It is the primary resuscitation method when defibrillation is not viable: the original body is too damaged, too long deceased, or absent entirely.

Mind records are maintained by backup implants installed in reception. These implants update periodically and broadcast an alert over the medical frequency when they cease function, signaling that the crew member has died. One-time manual scans are also available using a SleeveMate for crew who decline implants.

> [!NOTE]
> Some crew members carry Do Not Resuscitate or Do Not Resleeve orders on record. Check patient records before beginning. The CMO or Central Command may also issue such orders; a CMO order requires reasonable grounds, and a Central Command order requires hard evidence of voluntary suicide.

---

## Resleeving Procedure

1. Confirm no viable defibrillation candidate and verify no DNR/DNR-sleeve orders are on file.
2. If the patient lacks a mind record and their body is still present, scan the cadaver with a SleeveMate and select **Mind-Scan (One Time)**. A mind record with their name will appear at the resleeving console shortly.
3. At the resleeving console, confirm the mind record is present before growing a body.
4. Begin growing the body. Tint the resleeving lab windows.
5. Once ejected from the growing tube, transfer the body to cryogenics and run until health reads 100%. An unupgraded growing tube ejects at approximately 20% health due to genetic damage; cryo corrects this. Mutations may also be present.
6. Administer 1u Ryetalyn if mutations are detected.
7. Eject from cryo. Dress the patient in a uniform from the spare clothing locker.
8. If a bag of personal effects was delivered with the patient, place it on their back slot without looking inside.
9. Transfer the dressed body to the resleeving tube and sleeve the mind record.
10. Eject and buckle the patient to a nearby chair. Remain present when they become conscious.

---

## Post-Resleeve Protocol

Newly resleeved patients are often disoriented. The following procedure protects their wellbeing and the department's professional standing.

1. Shake the patient awake if they are not already conscious.
2. Ask how they are feeling and where they believe they are.
3. Lead with: there is some difficult news. This prepares the patient before the disclosure.
4. Inform them directly that they have been resleeved. Keep the explanation clear and simple. Misinformation is harmful.
5. Allow time for the patient to process. Responses vary; some are calm, some are distressed.
6. Answer questions only with what is known. Do not speculate about the circumstances of their death.
7. Advise rest and inform them of common post-resleeve symptoms: fatigue and disorientation.

The patient is not a new person; they are the same individual in a new body. Treat them accordingly.

---

## Carding

If no body record exists and the original body is absent or not intact, the patient's mind record can be sleeved into a card as a temporary measure. A carded mind is fully aware of its surroundings and can speak, but cannot act physically. This state is used when the patient needs time to design a new body before a permanent sleeve is grown.

---

## Body Design

The body design console in reception allows crew to design a custom sleeve when no body record is on file. The console functions similarly to character creation. Once a body is designed, save the configuration to a disk from the supply on the adjacent table, then insert the disk into the resleeving console to grow from it.

---

## Upgrade Notes

Research can upgrade growing pod components. Upgraded pods eject bodies with less genetic damage and fewer mutations, reducing or eliminating the cryo correction step. The resleeving pod can also be upgraded to reduce post-resleeve disorientation and confusion in the patient.
