# Non-Contiguous Memory Disorder

**Non-Contiguous Memory Disorder** (NCMD, sometimes shortened to Non-Con) is an umbrella term for a range of psychological and memory-related conditions common to crew members who have undergone resleeving, cloning, or similar body-transfer procedures. The disorder is rooted in missing or falsified memories and transcription errors that arise during the transition between an old body and a new one.

---

## Mechanism

NCMD is partially neurological and partially psychological in origin. The neurological component is consistent: newly grown sleeves involve a degree of brain damage analogous to a moderate concussion, and regaining consciousness in a medical facility after resleeving is the standard patient experience. The psychological component varies significantly between individuals; the same objective procedure can produce vastly different symptom profiles depending on the patient's mental constitution and the circumstances of their death.

Mind-scan implants allow burst-transmission of memory data to a paired backup database, enabling recovery of memories recorded up to the point of last scan. These systems are imperfect. Memories recorded under physical or psychological stress are frequently poorly transcribed, and traumatic memories in particular may be partially or entirely absent from the recovered record -- or replaced with plausible false memories the patient may not recognise as inaccurate.

---

## Common Symptoms

The two most frequently observed symptoms following resleeving are:

**Immediate post-resleeve disorientation.** The patient may be confused about their location, the time elapsed, or events preceding their death. This is expected and typically resolves within minutes.

**Short-term memory loss.** Memory gaps typically extend from the moment of death back to the time of the last mind-scan, or to a period shortly before death. The extent of the gap varies by the scan-to-death interval and the nature of the death event.

---

## Less Common Symptoms

Some patients exhibit additional symptoms that may persist after the initial recovery period:

- Mood instability and irritability
- Dizziness and nausea
- Anxiety, nightmares, or intrusive memories
- Long-term memory disruption unrelated to the death event
- Post-traumatic stress responses

Patients presenting with any of these symptoms should be referred for psychiatric evaluation. Medical staff are advised to arrange evaluation discreetly where possible.

---

## Patient Communication

Patient reactions to learning that they have been resleeved vary widely. An outwardly calm or dismissive response does not indicate the absence of psychological distress. Medical staff should review the patient's records before discussing the circumstances of their resleeving.

Medical staff are expected to be honest with patients about their status. The circumstances of the death should not be concealed from the patient, but should not be forced on the patient if they indicate they do not wish to know. The patient's prior medical record may contain notes indicating communication preferences.

---

## Legal Status of NCMD Testimony

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Witness statements from a crew member with confirmed or suspected NCMD are inadmissible as evidence in criminal proceedings. Such statements may be used as probable cause to open an investigation, but corroborating evidence sufficient for prosecution must be established independently. Security personnel are advised to account for this limitation when processing resleeve-adjacent incidents.

---

## Notes for Treating Staff

Resleeved crew members should be treated with the same dignity and professional courtesy extended to any patient. Staff should not speculate aloud about what the patient does or does not remember in the patient's presence, and should not attempt to prompt or fill in gaps in the patient's recollection. A psychiatric follow-up should be offered and documented in the patient's record regardless of how the patient presents at the time of discharge.
