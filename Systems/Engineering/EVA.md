# EVA

Extravehicular activity (EVA) refers to operations conducted outside the pressurised hull of the station, in open vacuum, in transit between structures, or in any environment without breathable atmosphere. EVA capability depends on appropriate protective equipment matched to the hazard level of the environment.

---

## Protective Equipment

### Softsuits

**Softsuits** are the most basic form of pressure protection available on station. The suit and helmet are separate items and must both be worn to achieve a seal. Emergency softsuit sets are stored in the blue emergency oxygen closets distributed throughout the station.

Softsuits are fragile: they are susceptible to puncture and tearing, and damage to the fabric compromises the seal entirely. They are also bulky and brightly coloured, which makes movement slower and conspicuous.

### Voidsuits

**Voidsuits** provide more robust protection than softsuits and include a dedicated storage slot within the suit for an oxygen tank, removing the need to carry a separate tank on the back. Voidsuits can be disassembled into a compact form for transport and reassembled when needed.

Voidsuits are not armoured. Punctures must be repaired with a welding tool; scorch marks require steel sheets to repair.

Non-human crew members must have voidsuits refit in a [suit cycler](#suit-cycler) before the suit will seal correctly against their anatomy.

#### Assembly

A voidsuit is assembled by holding a component and pressing it against the main suit body. Components deploy automatically when the suit is worn, provided nothing blocks their slot. Components are removed by applying a screwdriver to the suit.

| Component | Slot |
|---|---|
| Voidsuit helmet | Helmet slot |
| Voidsuit | Exosuit slot |
| Magboots | Shoe slot (fits over existing footwear) |
| Oxygen tank | Suit storage slot |
| Suit cooler | Suit storage slot (alternative to oxygen tank; for synthetic crew) |

Only one item may occupy the suit storage slot. The oxygen tank or the suit cooler is carried there, not both.

### Hardsuits

**Hardsuits** are integrated EVA systems built around a back-mounted control module. The module deploys and retracts all suit components at command and conforms automatically to the user's anatomy on activation, making refitting unnecessary. Each hardsuit includes an integrated oxygen tank, power cell, jetpack, heatsink, and bone support structure.

Hardsuits provide more structural protection than voidsuits and carry modular equipment slots whose function depends on the control module type. The tradeoff is a speed reduction that varies by model.

A crew member can reduce the speed penalty by keeping the chestpiece undeployed until it is needed, though this sacrifices torso protection.

---

## Jetpacks

Without gravity, any crew member without magnetic boots or propulsion will drift in the direction of their last movement until acted on by another force. A **jetpack** provides controlled propulsion in vacuum.

To operate, the jetpack must be placed on the back. Activating it provides basic movement thrust. Activating the stabilisation mode enables more precise maneuvering at the cost of additional propellant.

Jetpacks with black paint must not be used as a personal internals supply: the propellant is not breathable gas. Non-black jetpacks carry breathable oxygen as propellant and can serve as an emergency internal supply, but this drains propellant faster.

A fire extinguisher can substitute as a propulsion device in emergencies by venting its contents in the opposite direction of desired travel.

## Suit Cycler

The **suit cycler** is a station-mounted machine that refits voidsuits to a crew member's species anatomy. Each departmental cycler is access-locked to prevent suit transfers between departments.

### Operation

1. Unlock the cycler with the appropriate access.
2. Place both the voidsuit and the helmet inside the cycler.
3. Select the suit type and the crew member's species from the interface.
4. Press **Begin Customization Routine** and wait approximately 10 seconds for the cycle to complete.
5. Remove the suit and helmet and confirm fit on the wearer.

The suit type selection changes only the suit's appearance; protection values remain those of the original suit.

## Airlock Operation

### Exiting the Station

Before cycling through an exterior airlock, crew should activate internals and, where applicable, engage magboots in case of accident during the cycle.

1. Confirm the airlock is cycled to the interior side. If the door is sealed against the user, press the button on the interior to initiate the cycle.
2. Access the control panel and select **Cycle to Exterior**.
3. Wait for the vents to shut off and the outer door to open.
4. Pass through the outer door into vacuum.

> [!CAUTION]
> Do not use emergency door-forcing controls on an airlock during an EVA cycle. Forcing the door depressurises the airlock room immediately and can propel crew members or objects into space without warning.

### Navigating in Vacuum

Without gravity and without a jetpack, crew members are limited to moving along the exterior hull using magnetic boots. A crew member who drifts free of the hull with no means of propulsion can throw an object in the direction of travel to generate a counter-impulse.

An unconscious crew member in open space must be pulled back toward the hull. Manual pulling releases automatically at range, so contact must be maintained continuously.

### Returning to the Station

1. Reach the exterior side of any pressurised airlock.
2. Access the control panel and select **Cycle to Interior**.
3. Wait for the airlock to pressurize and the inner door to open.
4. Pass through into the station. Internals and other vacuum equipment can be deactivated once clear of the airlock.
