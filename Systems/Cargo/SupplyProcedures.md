# Supply Procedures

The cargo department manages all inbound supply shipments and outbound exports for the station. Operations are coordinated through the Quartermaster and Cargo Technicians. The supply console in the cargo bay interfaces directly with Central Command logistics for ordering and the supply shuttle for physical transport.

---

## Supply Points

All orders are denominated in supply points. The station begins each shift with 50 points and accumulates additional points through passive income and active exports.

### Passive Income

The supply system accrues 1 point every 20 seconds, yielding approximately 3 points per minute during normal operations. This income runs continuously regardless of shuttle activity.

### Exporting Goods

The primary means of generating additional points is returning goods on the outbound supply shuttle. Items must be placed inside crates before loading onto the shuttle; loose items are flagged as improperly packaged and return no value. Exceptions are trolley tanks from the refinery, which may be shipped unboxed.

| Export type | Points earned |
|---|---|
| Empty crate returned | +5 per crate |
| Empty subcrate (small) returned | +1 per subcrate |
| Stamped manifest slip (in crate) | +2 per slip |
| Station credits (spacebucks) | 0.02 per credit (1 pt per $50) |
| Material stacks (metal, glass, etc.) | Varies by material type and quantity |
| Research samples (in sample container) | Varies by sample type |
| Vaccines (full vial, in freezer crate) | 5 per vial |
| Refined chemicals (trolley tank, single reagent, near-full) | Varies by reagent and volume |
| Salvage items | Varies by item |
| Intact organs (in freezer crate) | Varies by organ type |

> [!NOTE]
> Manifest slips must be stamped with an official stamp (any department or command stamp qualifies) and must not be a copy. The original stamped slip earns the +2 bonus; photocopies do not.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The following items are prohibited from the outbound supply shuttle and will be automatically flagged: living creatures, nuclear authentication disks, nuclear devices, radio beacons, translocator beacons, quantum pads, and fulton extraction beacons.

## Ordering Procedure

### Placing an Order

1. Access the supply console in the cargo bay.
2. Browse available packs by category (see [Supply Manifest](SupplyManifest.md) for a full listing).
3. Add the desired pack to the order queue.
4. Confirm the order. The pack will be dispatched on the next shuttle departure.

### Requisition Forms

For formal accountability, requests from other departments should be documented before approval:

1. The requesting crew member prints a requisition form from a request console or fills one out by hand (name, department, item requested, justification).
2. The form is stamped by the relevant Head of Staff.
3. The stamped form is retained by the Quartermaster. Including it in the outbound crate earns the +2 manifest bonus.

Requisition forms create a paper trail. They are not strictly required for routine orders but are advisable for high-value or security-relevant requests (weapons crates, specialized equipment).

## Shuttle Operations

The supply shuttle departs and returns on a fixed cycle. Travel time between the station and Central Command is approximately 20 minutes each way. While in transit, no new orders can be dispatched until the shuttle returns.

Crates arrive pre-labeled with their contents. Larger orders may span multiple crates. Upon arrival, crates are unloaded from the shuttle pad and distributed via MULEbot or disposals.

---

## Delivery Methods

### MULEbot

MULEbots are automated cargo transport units stationed in the cargo bay.

1. Drag the crate onto the MULE.
2. Set the destination using a PDA or the MULE's onboard control panel.
3. Send the MULE. It navigates autonomously to the destination and waits for unloading.

To recharge a MULEbot: open the maintenance panel with a screwdriver, remove the depleted power cell, insert a charged cell, and close the panel.

### Disposals Mailing

Disposals mailing is suitable for individual items and smaller crates when the pipe network is intact.

1. Wrap the item or crate with package wrapper.
2. Set the destination on a destination tagger (click the tagger in-hand to cycle destinations).
3. Apply the tagger to the wrapped parcel.
4. Place the parcel on the cargo conveyor or drag it into a disposals unit.

Large crates can be dragged into disposals units but this is only possible a limited number of times before the action becomes unavailable. For standard delivery of large items, MULEbot transport is preferred.

Gift wrapping is available for items and works as standard wrapping but cannot be tagged with a destination and must be delivered manually. Gift wrapping requires a sharp object (wirecutters) in the off-hand while clicking wrapping paper with the item in hand.

---

## Department Request Consoles

Request consoles are installed throughout the station in each department. Crew can use them to submit supply requests directly to the cargo bay without visiting in person. The QM receives the request via the supply console queue. PDA messages to Cargo Technicians or the Quartermaster serve the same purpose and typically receive faster acknowledgment.
