# Corporate Regulations

<img src="../../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

The binding legal code governing conduct aboard NanoTrasen installations. Defines offenses, sentencing guidelines, and tribunal procedure. Applies to all employees and visitors on station grounds. ## Interpretation

Corporate Regulations governs conduct aboard all registered NanoTrasen ships and stations. Regulations carry sentencing weight only against employees and registered visitors; unauthorized personnel are handled under the Unauthorized Personnel Policy in [Standard Operating Procedure](../Command/StandardOperatingProcedure.md).

For violent crimes (assault, manslaughter, attempted murder, murder) and theft (petty, pickpocketing, high value), only the most severe applicable charge applies. A single incident generates a single sentence. Multiple stolen items from one encounter constitute a single count of pickpocketing, not multiple separate charges. Where total accumulated sentence from a single holding exceeds 90 minutes, the prisoner is held pending tribunal judgment rather than served a fixed sentence.

The intent of the accused determines charge selection in ambiguous cases. Assault and attempted murder are mutually exclusive; the same act cannot carry both. Select the charge that most accurately reflects the demonstrated intent.

If the direct victim of a crime declines to press charges specific to themselves, Security may not pursue those charges regardless of officer preference. This exception applies only to crimes committed directly against the victim; it does not shield the accused from charges related to property damage, crimes against other persons, or any offense not directly involving the consenting victim. A deceased victim cannot exercise this option; murder charges proceed without consent.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> If the direct victim of a crime declines to press charges, Security may not pursue those specific charges. This applies only to offenses committed directly against that person. It does not block charges for property damage, offenses against third parties, or any offense not directly involving the consenting victim.

Aiding a criminal constitutes complicity; accomplices may be charged with the same offense as the principal.

The Colony Director is not exempt from Corporate Regulations and may be arrested for violations. Pardons require authorization from a NanoTrasen authority ranking above Colony Director; the Colony Director cannot self-issue a valid pardon.

> [!NOTE]
> The Colony Director is subject to Corporate Regulations and may be arrested. Pardons require authorization from a NanoTrasen authority ranking above Colony Director. The Colony Director cannot pardon themselves.

## Modifiers and Definitions

**Community Service**: For low-level offenses, at officer discretion, community service may be offered in place of standard penalties. The task should be proportionate; an officer who sets an unreasonably punitive service requirement should expect the prisoner to elect custodial sentence instead.

**Cyborgification**: Removal of a prisoner's brain for transplant into a cyborg chassis as an alternative to execution. Requires prisoner consent or request.

**Demotion**: Reduction of rank to and including Assistant. Requires approval of the prisoner's head of staff or the Colony Director. Includes removal of relevant equipment, uniform, and access.

**Dismissal**: ID title changed to "Dismissed," carrying zero access. Requires head of staff or Colony Director approval.

**Employee**: Defined as an NanoTrasen employee as recognized by the station's commanding officer. Non-employees are not protected by Corporate Regulations.

**Empowering**: Acquiring access through means other than the Head of Personnel or Colony Director.

**Execution**: Prisoner is killed by firing squad, lethal injection, digestion, or other approved method. The Colony Director must be physically present for an execution to proceed; an Acting Colony Director does not satisfy this requirement. The prisoner must be offered a choice of method and a final meal. Central Command must be informed of all executions. Permanent death resulting from execution requires Central Command authorization via fax prior to the event.

**Head of Staff**: Colony Director, Chief Engineer, Head of Personnel, Head of Security, Chief Medical Officer, Research Director.

**HuJ (Holding Until Judgment)**: Held in the brig until tribunal is concluded or shift ends, whichever comes first.

**HuT (Holding Until Transfer)**: Held in the brig until shift ends. Converts to HuJ when a tribunal is convened for the prisoner.

**Imminent Peril**: An apparent and immediate threat of serious injury or death from which escape is not possible. Justifies lethal force when no other option remains.

**Severe Injury**: A state in which the victim is in critical condition and struggling to maintain consciousness.

Sentences longer than 90 minutes accumulated from a single holding convert to HuJ. Prisoners serving more than 20 minutes have access to the communal jail area rather than solitary cell confinement, except in cases of suicide attempts or where the cell is the interior of an arresting officer. If additional crimes are committed during imprisonment, the prisoner moves to solitary and additional time is added to their sentence.

## Tribunal Procedure

Tribunals are the primary mechanism for decisions requiring more than one individual or for crimes without a fixed sentence. Tribunals do not require a jury, a lawyer, or active defendant participation.

Three to six heads of staff must discuss the matter and vote on the outcome. The Colony Director or Acting Colony Director convenes the tribunal, either formally in a meeting room or informally over radio. The AI may substitute as a head of staff if insufficient numbers are available.

A majority vote (over 50%) determines the outcome. In a tie, the Colony Director's vote prevails. If the vote passes, the outcome is enacted, typically by Security. The decision may be appealed once by either the defendant or the Colony Director.

Tribunals are not required for sentences under 10 minutes.

## Unauthorized Personnel

Unauthorized personnel (outsiders, non-crew) present on station grounds without invitation are subject to expulsion by Security. Those invited must comply with Corporate Regulations and Standard Operating Procedure; failure to comply results in expulsion and a ban from station grounds for the duration of the current shift.

Outsiders found guilty of medium or low level infractions are expelled from station grounds. Outsiders found guilty of high severity infractions are HuT and transferred to SolGov custody at shift end. Vessels docked at station and land beyond outpost perimeter are outside NanoTrasen jurisdiction and are not subject to this policy.

See also [Standard Operating Procedure](../Command/StandardOperatingProcedure.md): NanoTrasen Unauthorized Personnel Policy.
