## Low Level Infractions (§101-§125)

Suggested sentences up to approximately 10 minutes, set at officer discretion. Fines may be paid in lieu of brig time; if paid, no detention is required. Community service may be offered at officer discretion for low-level offenses. Additional penalties require authorization from the Colony Director, Head of Security, or equivalent.

<details>
<summary>Full descriptions: §101-§125</summary>

---

### §101 Trespassing

**Description:** To be in an area which a person does not have access to, without permission from those who do have access. Public spaces under departmental control are subject to this if the department finds the individual has no reason to be present or is disrupting regular service, and asks them to leave. This includes the bar, library, and chapel.

**Notes:** Confirm whether the individual was let in by someone with access, or was granted access on their ID, before charging. Trespassing and petty theft are frequently committed together; both charges apply where applicable. Severity increases if the individual refuses to leave peacefully or attempts to use department equipment.

**Suggested sentence:** 3 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** 200 credits.

---

### §102 Petty Theft

**Description:** To take items from areas one does not have access to, or to take items belonging to others or the station.

**Notes:** Access to an area does not authorize taking items from it. A department member who removes all tools and conceals them commits theft regardless of their job access. Keeping items from areas where they are in short supply is the key concern. Items range from toolboxes and materials to specialized equipment. Stolen items are confiscated and returned to their origin.

**Suggested sentence:** 3 minutes. Confiscation and return of stolen items. Immediate search.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** 150 credits. Confiscation and return of stolen items. Immediate search.

---

### §103 Minor Assault

**Description:** To use, or threaten, physical force against someone, without intent to kill or seriously injure.

**Notes:** If the damage caused is minor and easily treatable, this charge applies. Starting fights, punching crew members, or seriously threatening physical harm without intent to kill all fall under this. For injuries beyond minor and easily treatable, see Assault (§208).

**Suggested sentence:** 4 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** 400 credits.

---

### §104 Battery

**Description:** To have unwanted physical contact with someone, including contact that is not violent.

**Notes:** Incidental contact in transit does not constitute battery. Deliberate physical contact after the individual has explicitly requested it not occur does.

**Suggested sentence:** 2 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 8 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** 200 credits.

---

### §105 Disturbing the Peace

**Description:** Intentionally and publicly engaging in erotic acts; yelling at people without cause; throwing objects where they may strike others; harassing a person or department without provocation; refusing to leave after being asked; publicly denigrating NanoTrasen.

**Notes:** Operating publicly while nude without a nudity permit, openly engaging in sexual conduct in public areas while ignoring requests to relocate, or similar disruptive conduct falls under this. Intoxicated crew are charged only when actively causing a nuisance. Brief detention until sobriety is permitted if necessary.

**Suggested sentence:** 5 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 8 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** 250 credits.

---

### §106 Suspicious Conduct

**Description:** To possess a suspiciously wide skill set not indicated in employee records; to wield dangerous weapons near other staff without authorization; to extensively inquire about critical areas; to attempt to conceal identity; to attempt to impersonate other staff; to stalk other employees.

**Notes:** Wearing face-obscuring gear without operational need, performing duties outside one's job assignment without the relevant department head's knowledge, or behaving in a threatening or irrational manner all qualify. An individual operating outside their job title or wearing the wrong department's uniform without authorization may have ulterior motives and warrants investigation.

**Suggested sentence:** 2 minutes. Immediate search.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes, or indefinite detainment for the duration of an ongoing investigation. Forced psychiatric examination. Tracking implant.
**Fine:** 200 credits. Immediate search.

---

### §107 Misuse of Public Radio Channels

**Description:** To continually broadcast unimportant, false, or insignificant messages on the public radio frequency.

**Notes:** Applies to persistent spamming of radio channels. Screaming false emergency messages such as a security alert when none exists also qualifies. Severe false alarms that divert personnel from their duties may additionally constitute Obstruction of Duty (§120).

**Suggested sentence:** A warning.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 5 minutes or fine. Forced psychiatric examination. Injunction banning use of radio equipment.
**Fine:** 150 credits.

---

### §108 Violation of Injunction

**Description:** To violate the terms of an injunction made by Security or other legal personnel.

**Notes:** Injunctions may be issued for a range of purposes, including weapons bans and radio channel restrictions. They are applied by the Head of Security, Colony Director, or equivalent. If the individual commits a further offense while violating an injunction, that charge is added separately.

**Suggested sentence:** 5 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes.
**Fine:** None.

---

### §109 Insulting an Officer on Duty

**Description:** To directly insult a head of staff or member of Security without valid complaint.

**Notes:** Most commonly occurs during an arrest or termination. This charge applies when behavior is persistently rude and disruptive, not merely disagreeable. Insults and slander are distinct and should be charged separately depending on which is applicable. Confirm with superiors before charging to avoid an Internal Affairs review.

**Suggested sentence:** A warning.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 5 minutes.
**Fine:** 50 credits per applicable minute.

---

### §110 Slander

**Description:** To spread false claims in order to damage someone's reputation.

**Notes:** False statements made deliberately to damage another's standing. Requires proof of falsity; this charge is typically established through tribunal or officer judgment. Distinct from insults (§109).

**Suggested sentence:** A warning.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 5 minutes.
**Fine:** 50 credits per applicable minute.

---

### §111 Failure to Execute an Order

**Description:** To ignore or disregard a superior's valid orders.

**Notes:** Orders that are unlawful or require the individual to break regulations may be ignored without charge, and a formal complaint is appropriate in those cases. Lawful, achievable orders within the individual's job description must be followed. If failure to execute an order causes serious damage, severe injury, or death, see Failure to Execute an Order with Serious Consequences (§201).

**Suggested sentence:** 5 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** 500 credits.

---

### §112 Sexual Harassment

**Description:** To make unwanted sexual advances or obscene remarks toward another employee.

**Notes:** Applies to minor incidents that the recipient finds offensive. For more serious incidents, see Sexual Assault (§308).

**Suggested sentence:** 5 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 15 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** None.

---

### §113 Animal Cruelty

**Description:** To inflict unnecessary suffering or harm upon animals with malicious intent.

**Notes:** Experimental or research use of animals through established procedures does not qualify. Deliberate harm with no operational purpose does. The use of animals as food is a recognized grey area; standard livestock are generally acceptable, but keeping animals as crew pets occupies a different category.

**Suggested sentence:** 5 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 15 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** None.

---

### §114 Vandalism

**Description:** To deliberately damage or deface the station without malicious intent. Damaging synthetics counts toward this.

**Notes:** Ranges from minor hull damage to defacing surfaces. Sentence may be adjusted proportionally to the extent of damage. For damage caused with malicious intent, see Sabotage (§213).

**Suggested sentence:** 5 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** 450 credits.

---

### §115 Threat of Murder or Serious Injury

**Description:** To threaten to kill or seriously injure an employee.

**Notes:** The threat must be substantive. Heated radio arguments may warrant only a warning. A credible threat accompanied by physical pursuit or a weapon constitutes a more valid charge.

**Suggested sentence:** 2 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes. Psychiatric evaluation or tracking implant.
**Fine:** 500 credits.

---

### §116 Disrespect to the Dead

**Description:** To abuse the bodies of deceased or formerly deceased employees.

**Notes:** Covers use of morgue bodies as food, physical abuse of a prisoner's body, unauthorized experimental surgery on the deceased, and actions that prevent a body from being cloned or transferred to a synthetic chassis.

**Suggested sentence:** 5 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes.
**Fine:** 400 credits.

---

### §117 Excessive Use of Force in Detainment

**Description:** To use more force than is required to subdue a suspect.

**Notes:** Repeated striking of a compliant, restrained prisoner; use of force against an unarmed and cooperative suspect; and use of lethal weapons without imminent peril all qualify. Investigations into allegations of excessive force may be initiated by any head of staff.

**Suggested sentence:** 5 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 20 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** 350 credits.

---

### §118 Minor Contraband

**Description:** To be in possession of contraband, including drugs, that is not dangerous to other crew members.

**Notes:** Includes non-lethal weapons held without a valid permit, violations of existing permits (such as a bartender carrying their authorized weapon outside the permitted area), and chameleon items due to their impersonation potential. Drug possession under this charge applies where distribution intent is absent and no prescription from the Chief Medical Officer exists. Contraband intended to be dangerous falls under Major Contraband (§212).

**Suggested sentence:** 5 minutes. Confiscation of contraband items. Immediate search.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** 200 credits. Immediate search.

---

### §119 Illegal Parking

**Description:** To park a vehicle in a manner that obstructs crew movement.

**Notes:** Vehicles and mechs left in the center of rooms or blocking hallways qualify. Vehicles parked to one side or at room edges generally do not. If a hallway or doorway is completely blocked, see Illegal Blocking of Areas (§217).

**Suggested sentence:** Vehicle impoundment until fine is paid.
**Additional penalties:** Destruction of vehicle.
**Fine:** 200 credits.

---

### §120 Obstruction of Duty

**Description:** To negatively interfere with the duties of crew.

**Notes:** Pushing an officer attempting an arrest, ignoring or removing caution tape, opening fire shutters into a hazard area, moving injured personnel away from treating medical staff, and false emergency calls that divert personnel from their duties all qualify. If the obstruction results in injury, see Assault (§208). If serious damage occurs, see Sabotage (§213). If death results, see Manslaughter (§302).

**Suggested sentence:** 5 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Detainment without fine payment option until duties are completed. Demotion.
**Fine:** 500 credits.

---

### §121 Rubbernecking

**Description:** To arrive at the scene of an emergency with no purpose other than observation.

**Notes:** Applies when staff with no operational role at a crisis scene gather to observe and ignore requests to clear the area. This charge applies only after the individual has been asked to leave and refused. If the resulting obstruction blocks a corridor or doorway entirely, see Illegal Blocking of Areas (§217). If death results, see Manslaughter (§302).

**Suggested sentence:** Forceful removal from area.
**Additional penalties:** Detainment until crisis resolved. Demotion.
**Fine:** None.

---

### §122 Operating Under the Influence

**Description:** To operate heavy machinery or handle sensitive or dangerous equipment while under the effects of drugs or alcohol.

**Notes:** Applies to piloting mechs, handling weapons, administering medicine, managing the supermatter engine, and similar high-consequence tasks performed while noticeably impaired.

**Suggested sentence:** Detainment until sober.
**Additional penalties:** Demotion.
**Fine:** None.

---

### §123 Violation of Employee Rights

**Description:** To deliberately deny an employee their rights as defined in Standard Operating Procedure.

**Notes:** This is one of the few cases where Security may act on a Standard Operating Procedure violation. Charges under this section require an Internal Affairs Agent to initiate them. If an Internal Affairs Agent determines rights were violated, such as denying an employee rest periods or subjecting them to discriminatory practices, they may request Security enforcement. The specific penalty is at the discretion of the Internal Affairs Agent pursuing the charges.

**Suggested sentence:** Warning.
**Additional penalties:** Demotion.
**Fine:** 1,000 credits.

---

### §124 Minor Negligence

**Description:** To engage in negligent behavior leading to minor injury or damage, without deliberate intent.

**Notes:** Applies where damage or hazard caused is minor and easily treatable. A station worker failing to place wet floor indicators resulting in crew injury, or a service worker failing to remove hazardous ingredients from a preparation, are examples. For deliberate behavior, assault or vandalism charges are more appropriate.

**Suggested sentence:** Warning.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** 100 credits if a warning was already issued.

---

### §125 Forgery

**Description:** To falsify official documents or orders.

**Notes:** Claiming a superior issued orders they did not, fabricating faxes, or issuing medical prescriptions without proper signature and department stamp. All important documents should be verified for valid signatures and stamps.

**Suggested sentence:** 5 minutes. Immediate search.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 10 minutes. Demotion.
**Fine:** 200 credits. Immediate search.

</details>

## Medium Level Infractions (§201-§221)

Suggested sentences up to approximately 30 minutes, typically 10-15 minutes, set at officer discretion. Additional penalties require authorization from the Head of Security, Colony Director, or equivalent.

<details>
<summary>Full descriptions: §201-§221</summary>

---

### §201 Failure to Execute an Order with Serious Consequences

**Description:** To ignore or disregard a superior's valid orders, which then causes serious damage to property or loss of life.

**Notes:** As with the low-level equivalent (§111), but where the failure caused serious station damage or severe injury. Medical staff ignoring the Chief Medical Officer while patients accumulate, or engineering staff ignoring the Chief Engineer resulting in a major system failure, are examples. If the failure results in death, see Manslaughter (§302).

**Suggested sentence:** 15 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Demotion. Holding until transfer.

---

### §202 Resisting Arrest or Sparking a Manhunt

**Description:** To not cooperate with an officer conducting a proper arrest, or to cause a manhunt by evading Security.

**Notes:** Refusing restraints alone does not constitute resisting arrest. Refusing restraints and refusing to proceed to the brig does. Physically pushing or fleeing from an arresting officer also qualifies. Simply not appearing at the brig when requested does not qualify; active concealment or flight does. Context is relevant: an engineer managing an active engineering emergency may have legitimate cause to delay compliance with a minor arrest. If Security personnel threatened the individual with harm rather than lawful arrest, some justification for flight may exist.

**Suggested sentence:** 10 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 20 minutes.

---

### §203 Suicide Attempt

**Description:** To attempt or threaten to commit suicide.

**Notes:** Covers any crew member who threatens or attempts self-termination. Threatening self-harm as leverage in a situation qualifies.

**Suggested sentence:** Compulsory psychiatric examination. Holding until examined on repeat attempt.
**Additional penalties:** Demotion. Isolation in holding facility. Holding until transfer.

---

### §204 Abuse of Confiscated Equipment

**Description:** To take and use equipment that has been confiscated as evidence.

**Notes:** Evidence is not to be used operationally under any circumstances, including emergencies. The prohibition is absolute.

**Suggested sentence:** 10 minutes and re-confiscation of equipment.
**Additional penalties:** Demotion.

---

### §205 Illegal Detention, Arrest, or Holding

**Description:** To arrest, brig, or penalize an employee without proper cause or reason, or in violation of due process rights.

**Notes:** Applies primarily to Security personnel who act beyond their legal authority. Also covers kidnapping. Excessive time spent processing or questioning a suspect detained for a minor offense may fall under this charge.

**Suggested sentence:** 15 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Demotion.

---

### §206 Negligence

**Description:** To fail to perform a job to a satisfactory standard, or to create a dangerous situation without taking proper precautions.

**Notes:** Applies where an individual ignored Standard Operating Procedure and an accident resulted. Exposing areas to vacuum without posting hazard warnings, routing dangerous power levels into shared infrastructure and treating resulting crew harm as their own fault, and similar failures of professional duty qualify. If death results, Manslaughter (§302) is added to the charges.

**Suggested sentence:** 10 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Demotion.

---

### §207 Infiltration

**Description:** To attempt, or succeed, in entering a high-security area without authorization.

**Notes:** Designated high-security areas include the AI core and upload chamber, Armory, Bridge, EVA storage, Bluespace Gateway, head of staff offices, Research and Development server room, Telecommunications satellite, Teleporter room, Toxins storage, Vault, and Virology. Requesting an AI or synthetic to grant entry does not constitute authorization.

**Suggested sentence:** 15 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 30 minutes, or holding until transfer.

---

### §208 Assault

**Description:** To cause severe injury to another employee, or to brandish a deadly weapon with intent to cause or threaten such injury.

**Notes:** Distinguishable from Minor Assault (§103) in that the injury is severe enough that basic field treatment is insufficient. Severity may be reflected in the sentence. Also covers poisoning with drugs or hallucinogens. Weapons that appear functional to the victim, including chameleon, replica, and unloaded weapons, qualify as brandishing even if they would cause no actual harm. If intent to kill is evident, see Attempted Murder (§301).

**Suggested sentence:** 20 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 40 minutes. Demotion.

---

### §209 Escaping From Confinement

**Description:** To escape from brig confinement while serving a non-life sentence or awaiting judgment.

**Notes:** Applies to individuals who were not serving a Holding Until Transfer sentence at the time of escape. For escape from a HuT sentence, see Escaping From HuT Sentence (§306).

**Suggested sentence:** 20 minutes added to original sentence, with original sentence reset. If this accumulates past 90 minutes total, convert to Holding Until Judgment.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 30 minutes. Demotion.

---

### §210 Unlawful Modification of AI/Cyborg Laws

**Description:** To modify the laws of a synthetic or artificial intelligence without need, proper access, or authority.

**Notes:** A law reset in response to obviously harmful uploaded laws is a recognized exception. Outside of this, only the Colony Director or two heads of staff acting jointly may authorize a law modification.

**Suggested sentence:** 20 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Demotion. Holding until transfer.

---

### §211 Sedition

**Description:** To incite rebellion or organize opposition against the established chain of command.

**Notes:** Includes attempts to declare independent jurisdiction over portions of the station or vessel, and general conspiring against the command structure. For open violent rebellion, see Mutiny (§303).

**Suggested sentence:** 15 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Demotion. Holding until transfer.

---

### §212 Major Contraband

**Description:** To possess, use, or distribute dangerous contraband items, including drugs.

**Notes:** The critical element is danger to persons other than the user, or purpose-built capability for unauthorized access. Firearms, explosives, drug distribution, and tools designed specifically for bypassing secure systems qualify. Tools with legitimate operational purposes (multitools, insulated gloves) do not qualify solely on the basis of potential misuse. Valid permits from the Head of Security, Colony Director, or Central Command override this charge. The Chief Medical Officer may authorize possession of otherwise restricted substances through prescriptions. Research personnel may hold any contraband in compliance with their departmental contraband policy.

**Suggested sentence:** 15 minutes. Confiscation of contraband items.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 20 minutes. Demotion.

---

### §213 Sabotage

**Description:** To hinder the efforts of crew or station with malicious intent.

**Notes:** Hull breaches, sabotage of atmospheric systems, removal of critical equipment, and similar acts with deliberate harmful intent qualify. Intent is the determining factor. If the act creates imminent peril for crew, see Terrorism (§304).

**Suggested sentence:** 20 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 40 minutes. Demotion.

---

### §214 Exceeding Official Powers

**Description:** To act beyond what is authorized by the chain of command.

**Notes:** Applies to heads of staff who abuse the authority of their position, such as a Head of Personnel assuming operational security duties without emergency justification, or a Colony Director acting as if exempt from Corporate Regulations. Heads of staff issuing orders to departments outside their authority, ignoring the Colony Director, and Security personnel directing non-Security crew without legal grounds all qualify. Also covers unauthorized self-empowerment through stolen or modified ID cards.

**Suggested sentence:** 15 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 30 minutes. Demotion.

---

### §215 Grand Theft

**Description:** To steal items that are dangerous, of high value, or of sensitive nature.

**Notes:** Includes weapons, explosives, and ammunition. Also includes all items classified as High Risk Items under the contraband classification framework. Security personnel taking weapons from the Armory without authorization is an example.

**Suggested sentence:** 15 minutes. Confiscation of stolen items.
**Additional penalties:** Demotion. Holding until transfer.

---

### §216 Organizing a Breakout

**Description:** To attempt, or succeed, in freeing prisoners from the brig or other holding area.

**Notes:** Breaching brig windows, hacking Security access systems, or otherwise facilitating a prisoner's escape all qualify.

**Suggested sentence:** 15 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 30 minutes. Demotion.

---

### §217 Illegal Blocking of Areas

**Description:** To make an area inaccessible to those with appropriate access.

**Notes:** Bolting doors in public hallways or in departments the individual does not control, excessive cordoning beyond the scope of necessity, and leaving areas sealed after the relevant need has passed all qualify, provided the blockage interferes with normal crew duties.

**Suggested sentence:** 10 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 25 minutes. Demotion.

---

### §218 Failure to Obey Safety Protocol

**Description:** To willfully ignore safety measures designed to keep crew out of a crisis zone.

**Notes:** Non-engineering staff removing fire shutters, civilian crew dismantling security barriers, and personnel disregarding hazard warnings without the qualifications to operate in the restricted area all qualify. If a person is trapped inside a dangerous area and attempting to escape, this charge does not apply to them. If death results, Manslaughter (§302) is added.

**Suggested sentence:** 10 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Detainment until crisis resolved. Demotion.

---

### §219 Severe Use of Excessive Force

**Description:** To critically injure someone in defense of self or others after they no longer pose a threat, or to seriously injure a suspect during a detainment attempt.

**Notes:** Severely injuring an assailant who is no longer an active threat, or causing near-fatal injury to a suspect during an arrest without justification, qualify. Distinguished from §117 by the severity of injury caused.

**Suggested sentence:** 10 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 30 minutes. Demotion.

---

### §220 Mistreatment of Prisoners

**Description:** To intentionally take or cause an action that endangers the wellbeing of a non-hostile prisoner.

**Notes:** Denying medical treatment, physical abuse of a prisoner, and removing or preventing access to a viable communication method all qualify. If a prisoner dies as a result of mistreatment, the charge upgrades to Murder (§301) or Manslaughter (§302) depending on intent.

**Suggested sentence:** 10 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 30 minutes. Demotion.

---

### §221 Violating Employee Privacy

**Description:** To conduct illegal searches and seizures, or to covertly surveil crew members by means other than station cameras. Includes breach of medical confidentiality and unauthorized release of personal information.

**Notes:** Security use of thermal imaging or hidden audio recording without a warrant are examples. Surveillance activities require a warrant under Code Green. The Security-specific elements of this charge are waived under Code Blue or higher. Medical confidentiality and prisoner personal information are protected at all alert levels.

**Suggested sentence:** 10 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** Up to 30 minutes. Demotion.

</details>

## High Severity Infractions (§301-§309)

These infractions generally require tribunal ruling. Accused individuals are held pending judgment. In severe cases, permanent removal from the station is applicable.

<details>
<summary>Full descriptions: §301-§309</summary>

---

### §301 Murder

**Description:** To kill someone, or attempt to kill someone, with premeditated malicious intent.

**Notes:** Covers both completed murder and attempted murder. Premeditation and malicious intent are required elements. Crimes of impulse or passion where intent is ambiguous may warrant consideration of Manslaughter (§302) instead.

**Sentence:** Holding Until Judgment.
**Judgments:** Holding Until Transfer. Execution (requires formal head of staff tribunal). Possible permanent removal from the station.

---

### §302 Manslaughter

**Description:** To kill someone other than oneself without malice or forethought.

**Notes:** Includes deaths resulting from negligence or dereliction of duty. May also apply as a sentencing outcome for excessive use of force in self-defense. The absence of malicious intent distinguishes this from Murder (§301).

**Sentence:** 20 minutes.
**Additional penalties:** 40 minutes. Dismissal.

---

### §303 Mutiny

**Description:** To openly rebel against, or attempt to remove, command staff through violent means.

**Notes:** Motive is the determining element. Killing a head of staff in a personal dispute is Murder; organized violent action to seize control of the station is Mutiny. For non-violent conspiratorial resistance, see Sedition (§211).

**Sentence:** Holding Until Judgment.
**Judgments:** Holding Until Transfer. Execution (requires formal head of staff tribunal). Possible permanent removal from the station.

---

### §304 Terrorism

**Description:** To engage in maliciously destructive actions that seriously threaten the crew or station.

**Notes:** Deliberate arson, hostage-taking, use of explosives, release of contained hazards, and similar acts with the intent or effect of seriously endangering the station or its crew qualify. Central Command must be notified in all cases of terrorism.

**Sentence:** Holding Until Judgment.
**Judgments:** Holding Until Transfer. Execution (requires formal head of staff tribunal). Possible permanent removal from the station.

---

### §305 Assaulting a Head of Staff

**Description:** To assault a head of staff, causing severe damage.

**Notes:** Substantively identical to Assault (§208), with the aggravating factor being the victim's position as a head of staff.

**Sentence:** 30 minutes. Tracking implant.
**Additional penalties:** Holding Until Transfer. Execution (requires formal head of staff tribunal).

---

### §306 Escaping From HuT Sentence

**Description:** To attempt, or succeed, in escaping from the brig or other holding area while serving a Holding Until Transfer sentence.

**Notes:** Requires that the individual was properly adjudicated to HuT by tribunal prior to the escape. This is one of the few cases where execution does not require a separate tribunal.

**Sentence:** Return to brig. Transfer to solitary confinement.
**Judgments:** Execution. No tribunal required. Possible permanent removal from the station.

---

### §307 Terrorist Collaboration

**Description:** To act as an agent of a terrorist organization or anti-corporate faction.

**Notes:** Covers espionage, disclosure of corporate secrets, and any assistance rendered to hostile external organizations or their agents operating against NanoTrasen. Suspected agents must be processed through a tribunal unless their level of resistance makes capture infeasible.

**Sentence:** Holding Until Judgment.
**Judgments:** Holding Until Transfer. Execution (requires formal head of staff tribunal). Possible permanent removal from the station.

---

### §308 Sexual Assault

**Description:** To assault, or attempt to assault, someone sexually, including through coerced contact.

**Notes:** If the victim dies as a direct result, see Murder (§301).

**Sentence:** 30 minutes. Tracking implant.
**Additional penalties:** Holding Until Transfer. Execution (requires formal head of staff tribunal). Possible permanent removal from the station.

---

### §309 Wrongful Execution

**Description:** To execute a crew member who was later determined to be innocent.

**Notes:** Applies when a tribunal was correctly convened and an execution order issued based on evidence that is subsequently proven false or insufficient. If tribunal procedure was bypassed or incorrectly conducted, Murder charges are added to the original responsible parties. The Colony Director faces demotion; individuals who deliberately fabricated evidence face Murder charges; those who provided incorrect evidence without intent face Manslaughter and Negligence charges. Central Command must be contacted. Central Command may suspend the Colony Director's command clearance for a period at their discretion.

**Sentence:** Immediate demotion of the Colony Director.
**Additional penalties:** Possible suspension from command positions.

</details>

## Special Infractions (§401-§402)

These infractions do not fit standard sentencing tiers. Sentences are determined case-by-case.

<details>
<summary>Full descriptions: §401-§402</summary>

---

### §401 Inducement

**Description:** To intentionally provoke, bribe, deceive, or incite a crew member to commit a crime they otherwise would not have committed, with the intent of having them arrested.

**Notes:** Examples include a Security officer behaving unprofessionally and then attempting to charge a crew member who responds angrily, bribing another employee to break into a location, manipulating a crew member into a confrontation to claim self-defense afterward, and Security officers threatening suspects with harm then charging them with resisting arrest when they flee. The operative standard is whether the suspect would have committed the offense absent the other party's intervention or escalation.

**Sentence:** Up to equal punishment with what the induced crime would have carried.

---

### §402 Piracy

**Description:** To rob a visiting vessel of goods or injure its crew while aboard that vessel.

**Notes:** NanoTrasen is required to enforce prohibitions on piracy in accordance with Admiralty Law in regions where it represents the primary governing authority. In addition to standard charges applicable to the criminal acts committed, a fax must be sent to Central Command, who will then contact SolGov for potential further action up to and including arrest by the USDF.

**Sentence:** Standard charges for the underlying criminal acts, plus mandatory Central Command notification.

</details>

## Sentence Modifiers

The following circumstances may reduce or increase a sentence. Multiple modifiers may apply to a single case.

<details>
<summary>Modifier table</summary>

| Modifier | Description | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Cooperation with Prosecution** | Providing information during questioning or naming accomplices. | Up to -25% reduction. Up to -50% for naming accomplices. |
| **Immediate Threat to Prisoner** | A significant hazard (detonation, breach, singularity event) endangers the holding area. | Officer must relocate prisoner to safety, or release immediately. HuJ and HuT sentences are reapplied after the danger passes. |
| **Medical Need** | Prisoner is sick or injured and requires medical attention. | Medical personnel are called, or the prisoner is escorted to Medbay. The sentence timer continues to run. |
| **Privacy Violated** | Evidence used against the defendant was gathered through illegal means, including unwarranted searches or unauthorized surveillance. | Relevant charges reduced or dropped. |
