# Contraband

Classification standards for contraband aboard NanoTrasen installations. Items are assessed by job authorization, permit status, list status, inherent danger, and intended use. Hardsuit access policies are documented by suit type.

## Classification Framework

When assessing whether an item constitutes contraband, the following criteria apply in order:

Is the person authorized to carry the item as part of their job? If yes, the item is not contraband.

Does the person hold a valid permit for the item? If yes, the item is not contraband. Permits must be the physical document; inability to produce the document means the item is treated as contraband per Corporate Regulations §118 or §212.

Is the item listed as contraband? If it is not on the contraband list, it is generally not contraband.

Is the item dangerous to the user or others? If yes, it is at minimum minor contraband.

Is the item intended to cause bodily harm to someone other than the user, or designed primarily for unauthorized access (such as an emag)? If yes, it is major contraband.

Is it a High Risk Item? High Risk Items are major contraband and additionally constitute grand theft under Corporate Regulations §215.

Items used in a crime may become contraband upon conviction regardless of their ordinary status.

## Generally Not Contraband

The following items are not considered contraband for any crew member under ordinary circumstances. If one of these items is used in a crime, it may be treated as contraband for that specific incident. Repeated possession after confiscation for criminal use may result in a contraband charge on the second offense.

Potential danger alone does not make an item contraband; the item's intended use is the determining factor.

---

## Potentially Contraband

These items are not always contraband but may be depending on context. Items carried within their appropriate department are generally not contraband. The same item paraded in an unrelated area by someone with no job requiring it is more likely to be contraband.

---

## Usually Contraband

These items are contraband for most crew in most circumstances. Standard exceptions apply: a non-lethal weapon is not contraband for Security officers, as it falls under job equipment. Standard Operating Procedure documents specific exceptions by role. See [Standard Operating Procedure](../Command/StandardOperatingProcedure.md) for permitted equipment by rank.

## Hardsuit Policy

Hardsuits are often armored and difficult to remove. Refusal to remove a hardsuit during an arrest constitutes Corporate Regulations §202 (Resisting Arrest). Fleeing an arrest while wearing a hardsuit authorizes shoot-to-disable.

Hardsuit permissions and contraband classifications vary by suit type.

<details>
<summary>Industrial Hardsuit (Mining)</summary>

Intended for mining operations. Permitted only in the Cargo and Mining department, planet-side mining areas, and off-planet expeditions. On-station outside these areas, the industrial hardsuit is Minor Contraband.

Charges should not be pursued if the miner is in transit delivering mining materials or traveling between the station and the planet. The preferred response is a reminder that the hardsuit is not permitted on station outside Cargo and Mining. A §118 Minor Contraband charge is appropriate only if the miner refuses to comply and return the hardsuit to the department.

</details>

<details>
<summary>AMI Hardsuit (Science)</summary>

Designed for protection from exotic energies during xenoarchaeological work. Permitted only in Science, planet-side, and off-planet expeditions. On-station outside these areas, the AMI hardsuit is Minor Contraband.

As with the industrial hardsuit, charges should not be pursued if the Scientist is in transit between the station and the planet. A §118 Minor Contraband charge applies only if the Scientist refuses to comply after a warning.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Rescue Hardsuit (Medical)</summary>

Intended for EMT personnel responding to crew trapped in hazardous conditions. May be worn on-station at all times with the helmet kept down when not actively responding to an emergency. This suit can override most airlocks but broadcasts a message over Security and Command radio, allowing misuse to be readily identified.

Wearing this hardsuit while not responding to an emergency (taking the suit to personal quarters, wearing it on break while not responding to emergency calls) may constitute §206 Negligence or §302 Manslaughter if a crew member's treatment is delayed as a result.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Advanced Hardsuit (Chief Engineer)</summary>

Designed for responding to supermatter delaminations. The only suit providing safe handling of active supermatter. The Chief Engineer may wear this suit at all times with the helmet down due to the need for rapid supermatter response.

Any crew member other than the Chief Engineer found in possession of this hardsuit may be charged with §215 Grand Theft.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Pursuit Hardsuit (Head of Security)</summary>

The Head of Security's personal hardsuit for emergency response and pursuing fleeing criminals. Classified as combat armor. Intended for Code Yellow and above or EVA operations. Casual use such as wearing the suit in the bar constitutes an SOP violation and may lead to demotion if repeated.

When not used as intended, this suit is Major Contraband. Any crew member other than the Head of Security in possession of this suit may be charged with §215 Grand Theft.

</details>
