# Grenades and Explosives

Grenades are throwable devices that trigger a timed or impact-activated effect. Security and command personnel may carry grenades for crowd control and area denial; engineering and science departments maintain explosive ordnance for demolition and containment. All grenades share a common arming and timer system.

---

## Timer System

Every standard grenade has an adjustable delay fuse. The timer is set by applying a screwdriver to the grenade's fuse cap before throwing.

| Timer setting | Delay |
|---|---|
| Instant | No delay; detonates on impact or immediately when activated |
| Short | ~1 second |
| Medium | ~3 seconds |
| Long | ~5 seconds |

The default setting when a grenade is found or issued is medium (~3 seconds). Instant-fuse grenades detonate the moment they land; they cannot be kicked or repositioned after throwing. Longer fuses allow for repositioning and cover.

## Fragmentation Grenades

### Frag Grenade

The standard fragmentation grenade produces a focused shrapnel burst over a moderate area.

- **Fragments:** 63
- **Fragment spread radius:** ~7 metres
- **Explosion size:** Moderate (wall-damaging at close range)

Fragments deal brute damage to anything in the spread radius. The grenade casing also produces a directional explosion at the detonation point. Effective for area denial, breaching, and anti-personnel use in open spaces.

### Mini Fragmentation Grenade

A scaled-down variant for confined spaces or precision use.

- **Fragments:** 20
- **Fragment spread radius:** ~3 metres
- **Explosion size:** Minor

Lower fragment count and tighter spread make this more controllable in corridors and small rooms. Still lethal to unarmored targets at close range.

## Flashbang

A non-lethal disorientation device producing a bright flash and loud concussive sound.

- **Flash effective radius:** ~7 metres
- **Close sound radius:** ~3 metres
- **Mid sound radius:** ~5 metres
- **Far sound radius:** ~7 metres

Within the flash radius, affected individuals experience temporary loss of vision. Sound effects scale with distance: within ~3 metres, the concussion causes additional ear damage. Photosensitive species and those without eye protection are more severely affected.

Flashbangs have no shrapnel and do not damage structures. They are the standard less-lethal option for room breaching and crowd control.

### Clusterbang

An enhanced flashbang that breaks into multiple submunitions on detonation. Spawns 4-8 miniature flashbangs that scatter and detonate independently over a wider area, making evasion difficult and covering multiple lines of sight simultaneously.

---

## Concussion Grenade

A pressure-wave device that knocks down and disorients targets without the blinding flash.

- **Blast radius:** ~5 metres

Targets within the blast radius are knocked prone, concussed, and may suffer ear damage. The concussion grenade does not flash, making it effective against parties with eye protection. No shrapnel; minimal structural damage.

---

## EMP Grenade

An electromagnetic pulse device designed to disable electronics and silicon entities.

### Standard EMP

| Zone | Radius |
|---|---|
| Heavy EMP | ~2 metres |
| Medium EMP | ~4 metres |
| Light EMP | ~7 metres |
| Long-range EMP | ~10 metres |

Each zone triggers a different level of disruption. Heavy EMP at close range can immediately disable or damage cyborg components and APCs. Medium EMP disrupts powered equipment and causes cell drain. Light and long-range EMP cause temporary interference and minor cell loss.

Effects on biological targets are minimal. The EMP grenade is the primary tool for disabling hostile cyborgs, emagged borgs, and electronic traps.

### Low-Yield EMP

A reduced-power variant with halved radii across all zones.

| Zone | Radius |
|---|---|
| Heavy EMP | ~1 metre |
| Medium EMP | ~2 metres |
| Light EMP | ~3 metres |
| Long-range EMP | ~4 metres |

Used when a full EMP pulse would cause collateral damage to nearby station equipment or allied cyborgs.

> [!CAUTION]
> EMP grenades will disable APC units, SMES interfaces, and powered doors within their heavy radius. Do not deploy in areas where power infrastructure loss would be immediately hazardous.

## Smoke Bomb

A non-explosive area-obscuring device.

- **Fuse:** ~2 seconds (fixed)
- **Smoke strength:** Dense

Produces a large opaque smoke cloud on detonation. The smoke blocks line of sight and persists for ~30-60 seconds depending on air circulation. Smoke color is configurable with a multitool before deployment.

Smoke bombs do not cause damage but can mask movement, obscure camera coverage, and create confusion. Smoke is affected by atmospheric pressure and vent flow; in areas with active ventilation, the cloud dissipates faster.

---

## Chemical Grenade (Base)

A throwable reagent dispersal device. The base chemical grenade casing is empty and must be loaded before use. Once loaded with reagents, it disperses its contents as a splash on detonation.

Used by medical and chemistry personnel for area-effect reagent delivery. The type and effect depends entirely on the loaded reagent. Security and engineering personnel may encounter chemically loaded grenades as improvised devices.
