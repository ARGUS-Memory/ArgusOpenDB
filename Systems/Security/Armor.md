# Armor and Equipment

Protection values for all armor types available to security and general crew, plus specifications for restraint devices and area-denial equipment. For weapons, see [Weapons](Weapons.md).

---

## Armor Protection Values

All values are expressed as percentage damage reduction per category. Higher is better.

## Armor Selection Guide

**Melee-heavy threat:** Riot armor and riot helmet (80% melee protection). Sacrifices bullet and laser protection.

**Ballistic threat:** Bullet-resistant vest and helmet (80% bullet protection). Weak against melee and energy.

**Energy/laser threat:** Ablative vest and helmet (80% laser, 50% energy). Weak against melee and ballistic.

**General security loadout:** Standard vest (40/30/30) with standard helmet (40/30/30). Balanced protection across all categories.

**High-risk operations:** SWAT suit with SWAT helmet. Best overall protection including biological and radiation, highest melee and bomb. Significant slowdown.

## Restraint Devices

| Device | Escape Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Handcuffs | 120 seconds | Applied in 3 seconds; requires aggressive grab; metal; most secure |
| Cable Restraints | 30 seconds | Flexible plastic; faster escape |
| Tape Restraints | 20 seconds | Improvised; weakest |
| Legcuffs | 30 seconds | Restricts movement to walking pace; does not prevent arm use |
| Bola | 3 seconds | Thrown projectile legcuff; very short hold; disintegrates if dropped |

Handcuffs are the standard security restraint for arrested personnel. Cable restraints are available as an improvised alternative but are significantly easier to escape from. Legcuffs restrict movement without binding the hands, suitable for compliant prisoners in transit.

## Area-Denial Equipment

### Flashbang

A thrown device that produces a blinding flash and concussive sound burst on detonation.

| Effect | Range | Magnitude |
|---|---|---|
| Full stun | 3 tiles | Complete incapacitation |
| Medium stun | 5 tiles | Significant impairment |
| Light stun | 7 tiles | Disorientation |
| Blindness | 7 tiles | Temporary visual loss |
| Maximum effect range | 10 tiles | Edge of effect |
| Sound damage | Up to 15 ear damage | Hearing impairment |

Personnel facing away from the detonation or with eye protection reduce flash exposure. The flashbang also causes confusion and weakness at close range.

### Clusterbang

A thrown device that disperses 4 to 8 secondary flashbang submunitions on detonation. Each submunition functions as a standard flashbang. Submunitions scatter 1 to 4 tiles from the impact point. High coverage of an area at the cost of controlled application. Significant collateral risk to non-hostile personnel in the area.
