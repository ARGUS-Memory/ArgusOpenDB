# Languages

Known space hosts a range of spoken, signed, and synthetic languages. What follows is a reference to the primary languages crew are likely to encounter aboard NanoTrasen facilities.

---

## Human Languages

**Galactic Common** is the engineered interspecies lingua franca used throughout known space. It was designed to accommodate the vocal and cognitive ranges of multiple species, and serves as the default language for most cross-species communication. Station operations, formal documentation, and official NanoTrasen communications are conducted in Galactic Common.

**Sol Common** is the informal lingua franca of the Sol system and inner colonies. A hybrid of Chinese, English, French, and several other Earth languages, it has diverged considerably from its source languages over generations. Most humans from core-world backgrounds speak it natively alongside Galactic Common.

**Tradeband** is maintained by trading cartels across major systems and used for commercial negotiation and bartering. It is a structured, formal language designed to minimize ambiguity in contract and transaction contexts, and is widely learned as a second language among merchants and traders.

**Gutterband** is a rapidly evolving colloquial dialect of Tradeband associated with the Free Trade Union and independent traders. It is highly idiomatic and difficult to formally translate; familiarity is typically built through immersion rather than instruction.

**Spacer** is a rough pidgin of Tradeband, Gutterband, and Sol Common, used by various spacefaring communities as an informal shared tongue. It is common among belters, long-haul crews, and those raised on stations rather than planets.

**Pan-Slavic** is the official language of the Independent Colonial Confederation of Gilgamesh, originating from the short-lived United Slavic Confederation on Earth in the 22nd century.

## Species Languages

**Sinta'unathi** is the common language of the Unathi Hegemony, composed of sibilant hisses and rattles. It is spoken natively by unathi and is the primary language in Hegemony-affiliated contexts.

**Siik** is the most prevalent language of Meralar and is native to the Tajaran. It is composed of expressive yowls and chirps. The related **Akhani** is the language of the sea-faring Njarir'Akhan Tajaran, structurally distinct from Siik though sharing some elements. **Siik Tajr** is a sign-based tail movement language developed and used during the Tajaran rebellion; it is not spoken aloud.

**Common Skrellian** is constructed from warbles and hums, with melodic and rhythmic components capable of conveying nuance of intent and emotion that many other languages lack. The related **High Skrellian** is the dominant language among the Skrellian Far Kingdoms and carries a higher concentration of phonemes outside the audible range of most non-skrellian species.

**Terminus** is a family of languages spoken by the Zorren, sharing enough mutual intelligibility to be grouped together. Evidence suggests it was once a single lingua franca that diverged through geographic isolation.

**Canilunzt** is the language of the Vulpkanin, composed of growls, barks, yaps, and heavy use of ear and tail movement.

**Sagaru** is the dominant language of Vilous and is native to the Sergal, consisting of aggressive low-pitched hissing and throaty growling.

**Schechi** is the trilling language of the Teshari.

**Vespinae** is the language developed and used by Vasilissans, designed for mouthparts, and has been adopted as a common language among several other arthropod species.

**Promethean Biolinguistics** is the complex communication system of Prometheans, composed of guttural vocalizations and bioluminescent signals.

**Birdsong** is primarily spoken by Narvians.

**Vedahq** is a harsh buzzing language created by the Zaddat following their homeworld exodus.

**Vox-Pidgin** is the common tongue of the various Vox ships composing the Shoal. It registers as chaotic shrieking to those without relevant vocal anatomy.

## Synthetic Languages

**Robot Talk** makes use of free-use communications protocols and routing hubs supported by most human stations. It provides a common channel for synthetic crew to communicate across hardware platforms.

**Encoded Audio Language** is an efficient communication system of encoded tones developed by positronics for high-bandwidth exchange. It is not audible to most organic crew as natural speech.

**Drone Talk** is a heavily encoded damage control coordination stream used by maintenance drones.

**Local Rootspeak** and **Global Rootspeak** are the communication methods of Dionaea, transmitted via modulated radio waves rather than acoustic speech. Local Rootspeak uses high-frequency waves for short-range communication; Global Rootspeak uses low-frequency waves for long-range transmission. Both are received rather than actively spoken by most other species.
