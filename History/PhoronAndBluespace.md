## Phoron

**Phoron** was discovered by humanity in 2411 (SolGov calendar) by researchers Alden and Sarapova in the plasma of the Gliese 229 system's red dwarf star. The original term "plasma" remained heavily in use in SolGov space following reclassification; "phoron" became the standard term primarily in frontier and non-human-dominated regions.

Phoron exists partially in realspace and partially in bluespace, with the proportion in flux at any given moment. This duality produces variable, sometimes contradictory properties that have resisted a unified physical explanation 151 years after discovery. Only phoron's effects are well-documented; the underlying mechanics remain unresolved.

Confirmed properties include: combustibility, function as a powerful reducing agent, toxicity to most known lifeforms, the ability to exist in solid, liquid, and gas phases under standard conditions as well as at least one exotic matter state, and high concentrations of Alden-Sarapova particles.

Phoron's three primary applications are **FTL travel** (powering on-ship FTL drives), **fuel** (combustion releases Alden-Sarapova energy at yields far above conventional fuels), and **chemistry** (liquid phoron functions as a potent catalyst and reagent in pharmaceutical synthesis). Phoron is one of the highest-value substances in known space and is the foundational resource of the interstellar economy. NanoTrasen holds primary phoron extraction rights above the phoron giant Erebus in the Gliese 229 system.

## Supermatter

**Supermatter** is a poorly understood exotic phase of phoron: a condensed crystalline form believed to possess a hyperdimensional structure existing largely in bluespace. Reliable study is difficult due to its properties. Supermatter emits heavy radiation, phoron gas, and oxygen when stimulated by a phoron laser or direct energy input. Overheating or exposure to vacuum causes catastrophic collapse, typically an implosion followed by a detonation. Direct contact with organic matter reduces the subject to ash rapidly. Energetic probing destroys the probing instrument.

Despite the risks, supermatter is used extensively in thermoelectric power generation. A supermatter crystal in a properly managed cooling loop provides a near-limitless energy output. See [Supermatter Engine](../Systems/Engineering/SupermatterEngine.md) for operational procedures.

## Bluespace

**Bluespace** is named for its visual appearance when traversed at low speeds relative to realspace. The term describes the dimensional medium exploited by phoron-powered FTL drives. No single theoretical model for its nature has achieved consensus among researchers. The four primary theoretical frameworks are as follows.

**Subspace theory** describes bluespace as the "space between space": a quantum effect that only manifests when observed, effectively existing nowhere until needed. FTL travel, under this model, constructs a three-dimensional bridge by stretching realspace and anchoring it to a distant point. Travel speed depends on the relative distance between realspace landmarks perceived during transit.

**Planar theory** describes bluespace as a separate plane or universe existing at a lower energy state than realspace. Contact with bluespace at full immersion is lethal to organic matter because it causes transition to this lower energy state. FTL travel involves entering the plane and building sufficient speed to break back into realspace at a strained point, typically a gravity well.

**Hyperspace theory** describes bluespace as a hyperdimensional extension of realspace: a fourth spatial axis. FTL travel under this model is movement along this axis, effectively going "around" three-dimensional realspace. The theory also posits that all matter consists of three-dimensional beings embedded in a four-dimensional universe.

**Entanglement theory** proposes that bluespace does not exist as a separate domain. Under this model, Alden-Sarapova particles are virtual exotic matter capable of instant information transfer. A bluespace particle can adopt the properties, including the "realness" variable, of a classical particle, effectively replacing it. FTL under this model is information transfer rather than physical transit.

## Redspace

**Redspace** is an informal scientific term rather than a defined phenomenon. The term originated as an in-joke based on an early misidentification of bluespace's color as blueshift-related, implying a "redshift" counterpart. It functions as a category label for bluespace phenomena that lack sufficient scientific understanding to classify rigorously.

In practice, "redspace" is applied to dangerous anomalies that appear more anomalous than physical, particularly those associated with artifacts of precursor civilizations or entities of partially non-realspace composition. Outside scientific circles, the term is used almost exclusively to describe hazardous and poorly understood phenomena.

The color of redspace, if it has one, remains undetermined.
