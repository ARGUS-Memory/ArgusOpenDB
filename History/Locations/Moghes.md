# Moghes

Moghes, in the Uueoa-Esa system, is the homeworld of the unathi and the capital of the Moghes Hegemony. The average surface temperature is approximately 23 degrees Celsius. The surface is predominantly land, interrupted by massive saltwater lakes and inland seas rather than true oceans; the planet is significantly less water-covered than Earth. The tropical band consists of hot rocky plains and stretches of desert; the temperate band transitions to swampland and savannas; the poles are dominated by swampland as well, with the northernmost polar region being a cold plain permanently oriented toward Moghes's moon Kahret.

Moghes was once rich in precious metals and minerals, but centuries of extraction have significantly depleted surface deposits. The unathi now depend on off-world mining and interplanetary extraction, a resource pressure that has historically driven territorial conflict.

---

## The Uueoa-Esa System

Uueoa-Esa translates roughly as "Burning Mother" and is a blue main-sequence star. The system contains three planets and an asteroid belt believed to be the remnant of up to two additional bodies.

Ouere is the innermost planet, hosting a solar power station on its surface that beams energy back to Moghes. Kahret, the only moon of Moghes, is tidally locked in orbit above the northernmost polar region. It possesses its own atmosphere and biosphere, though nothing classified as sapient has been found there; some unathi scientists theorize Moghes and Kahret were once a single larger body, split by an ancient impact.

Yeora is the system's only gas giant, orbited by dozens of moons, several of which host small unathi colonies or research facilities. The Yoos Asteroid Belt has been mined by the unathi since their early spacefaring period; its mineral richness supports ongoing debate about whether it represents the remains of one or multiple former planets.

---

## Settlement Patterns

Unathi inhabit massive industrial-residential complexes called City-Holds, each focused on specific industries or crafts. Outside the City-Holds, farmland and ranching operations dominate the landscape, connected by roads and rail. The carnivorous unathi diet requires substantially more agricultural land per capita than most other known species.

---

## Flora

The two most widely recognized plant species are the Strangler Vine and the Velo plant.

The Strangler Vine grows primarily in the southern swamps. It wraps trees and rock formations with flexible vines covered in stinging hair; when triggered, the vines constrict, injecting a sedative. The unconscious prey falls into the swamp below to decay and feed the plant.

The Velo plant is a short, thick starch plant with a white fibrous body. Its root is the primary food source of the stok, a farmed animal; its body is usable in fabric production, and the plant has spread to most unathi colonies as a result.

---

## Fauna

Hostile wildlife historically made surface life hazardous, though many predators have since been domesticated or brought under controlled ranching.

The Thresher is a large predatory quadruped approximately 1.4 meters at the shoulder, weighing around 72 kilograms, with large poisonous quills across much of its body. Threshers have pack instincts and are aggressive toward unathi by nature, though domestication is possible with significant effort and patience.

The Sissalik is a gecko-like creature nearly two meters tall at the shoulder, gentle in temperament but carnivorous. Historically used as cavalry mounts, Sissalik now serve primarily as beasts of burden and for herding other animals. They are also a source of meat.

The stok is a smaller reptile standing under a meter tall, with colorful scales and feathered head ornamentation. It stands on two legs with short arms and digitigrade feet, and spends most of its time eating and resting in the swampy tree canopy. It is the primary prey animal of the Velo root system and is farmed extensively.

The Moghes Duneworm is a critically endangered delicacy. Completely blind and omnivorous, it tracks prey by smell. Unathi legend records duneworms large enough to swallow an adult whole, but the species has been hunted nearly to extinction. It survives only in certified desert preserves and regulated ranches.
