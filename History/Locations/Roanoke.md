# Roanoke

Roanoke is the homeworld of the alraune species, a large high-pressure planet characterized by dense tropical marshland, aggressive megafauna, and an atmospheric environment hostile to standard humanoid biology. Formal contact with galactic society was preceded by two colonial incursions resulting in a decade of conflict before a ceasefire established the current relationship.

## Environment

Roanoke orbits a main-sequence B-type star with high ultraviolet and blue-light output. The elevated radiation environment is relevant to the evolution of alraune photosynthetic biology and their sensitivity to high-energy light.

Atmospheric pressure at the surface is approximately 2.7 standard atmospheres. The dominant atmospheric composition is nitrogen, CO2, and oxygen; the elevated CO2 concentration makes the atmosphere directly harmful to most humanoid species without internals or voidsuits. Surface temperature exceeds Earth-baseline due to the planet's proximity to the inner edge of its star's habitable zone.

Approximately 89% of the surface is water or tropical marshland. Landmass comprises roughly equal parts tropical rainforest and semi-arid and temperate zones. A small southern polar cap exists. The deeper landmass regions are densely forested and difficult to navigate without knowledge of the terrain.

Animal life on Roanoke includes megafauna, large insect species, and small lizard and mammal populations. The megafauna represent significant hazards to unprepared expeditions.

## First Contact and Conflict

The first human colonial group arrived on Roanoke in 2343 CE and settled in the low-altitude zone. The colony went dark within months of landing, with the last transmissions reporting hostile wildlife. The fauna reference almost certainly obscured the actual cause; contact analysis conducted later established that alraune were responsible for the colony's destruction, treating the settlers as prey entering their territory.

A second and substantially better-equipped colonial wave arrived in 2365 CE. Lessons from the first colony's loss led this wave to establish in the high-altitude mountain regions, where lower atmospheric pressure partially mitigated the CO2 hazard and reduced encounter frequency with the low-altitude alraune population. This positioning delayed direct contact long enough to establish a defensible settlement.

Sustained conflict followed when contact finally occurred. Alraune adapted rapidly during the engagement, incorporating captured equipment and organizational knowledge derived from observing human colonial structure. Both sides sustained heavy casualties. SolGov intervened following escalating military expenditure, and a ceasefire was negotiated in 2371 CE. The ceasefire terms required the release of all captives held by either side and mutual withdrawal from contested territorial zones.

Following the ceasefire, a subset of colonies established mutualistic relationships with neighboring alraune communities. These arrangements vary significantly by location and are not representative of alraune-human relations across Roanoke as a whole.

## Current Status

Roanoke itself maintains minimal contact with galactic society. The planet is not a formal member of any interstellar governance structure. Alraune who choose to leave the planet and operate in galactic space do so as individuals or in small associations; there is no unified alraune government representing the homeworld's interests in galactic forums.

Spacefaring alraune are present across frontier stations including those in the Vir system. Their reputation in mixed-species environments tends toward pragmatic and occasionally deceptive conduct, a pattern consistent with their cultural philosophy of self-interest as the primary operating principle.
