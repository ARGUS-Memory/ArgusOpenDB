# Xohox

Xohox is the origin world of the zaddat species and the site of one of the most severe industrial biosphere collapses in recorded galactic history. The planet remains marginally habitable but cannot support large populations. The majority of the zaddat now live aboard their Colony fleet rather than on the surface.

## Environment

Xohox is a high-pressure world. Atmospheric pressure substantially exceeds standard station baseline; unprotected humanoids operating on the surface require pressure suits. The dominant atmospheric composition includes compounds directly toxic to most galactic species at ambient concentrations.

The native biosphere was once complex enough to support the full zaddat lifecycle including the atmospheric polyp stage. Zaddat young begin as gas-filled polyp forms that float in the upper atmosphere, photosynthesizing as they develop before descending to the surface. This lifecycle required a stable upper atmosphere with consistent light availability and organic nutrient distribution. Industrial activity progressively degraded both conditions.

---

## The Collapse

Rampant industrialization rendered the Xohox atmosphere toxic and progressively less stable over several centuries. The event most associated with the beginning of the catastrophic period is the **Darkening of Xohox**, when atmospheric contamination became dense enough to significantly reduce and scatter incoming light, disrupting photosynthesis at all atmospheric levels including the polyp nursery zone. The zaddat symbiote, a light-sensitive organism cultivated as a pressurization aid, evolved heightened photosensitivity in response to the instability of this period.

Surface habitability declined in parallel with atmospheric degradation. As the capacity to maintain large populations on the surface collapsed, zaddat society transitioned to **Colonies**: originally converted Hegemony bulk freighters, eventually purpose-designed habitat-ships. The transition was not orderly; population loss during the collapse period is substantial and the figures disputed. The Hegemony's intervention in stabilizing the surviving population is credited by most zaddat as the decisive factor in species survival.

The near-extinction event has been interpreted within zaddat religious framework as a divine consequence of industrial overconsumption. The Uueoa-Esa sun-worship element of the Unity, which the zaddat adopted through centuries of Hegemony integration, merged with zaddat pre-collapse superstitions about light and the catastrophic effects of the Darkening into a coherent religious narrative. Many zaddat view the ongoing search for a replacement homeworld as a religious pilgrimage as much as a practical migration goal.

## Current Status

The Xohox surface retains a small zaddat population operating under life-support conditions. No large-scale resettlement is planned or considered viable under current atmospheric conditions. The planet functions primarily as a symbolic and spiritual site rather than a practical home.

The dominant political stance among spacefaring zaddat is that Xohox cannot be restored and that the species' future depends on securing a new homeworld. Terraforming the planet is not technically ruled out but is regarded as generationally impractical. Skrell terraforming activity on candidate worlds elsewhere is a significant source of zaddat resentment, as each terraformed planet reduces the pool of viable replacement sites.
