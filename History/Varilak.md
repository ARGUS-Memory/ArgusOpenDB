# Varilak

Varilak is the homeworld of the vasilissan species, a temperate jungle planet noted for exceptionally mineral-rich geology. Its unusually high density of gems and precious metals was the primary driver of NanoTrasen contact and remains the basis of its economic significance to the interstellar market.

## Environment

Varilak is a temperate world with dense forest cover across most of its landmass. Ocean surface accounts for approximately 60% of the planet. Atmospheric oxygen content is substantially higher than standard station baseline, a condition that supports the planet's large-bodied fauna. Insectoid, reptilian, and amphibian megafauna are present and represent the dominant animal life forms in the forested zones. The deeper ocean is largely unexplored.

The planetary crust contains gem and precious metal deposits at densities substantially above galactic norms. Current scientific consensus attributes this anomaly to an unidentified geological or astronomical event in the planet's history. The precise cause is unresolved and a subject of ongoing academic investigation.

## Vasilissan Civilization

Pre-contact vasilissan society was organized around small groups and queen-led settlements, in which queens directed both outward territorial expansion and vertical construction. Varilak's mineral wealth fed an architectural tradition of silk-and-gem towers reinforced with local metals; the tallest recorded structure reached approximately 300 meters before collapsing. Silk is the primary structural binding material, with gems and metal providing reinforcement and religious significance.

Resource allocation in traditional communities operated through queen orders and inter-settlement trade rather than currency. The abstract concept of currency was not intuitively familiar to vasilissans prior to corporate contact.

Gem-religion forms the central vasilissan spiritual framework. Gems are understood as conduits between the mortal plane and an energy plane that holds all past life. A gem's size and quality determines the quality of the transit it can facilitate. Varilak's own gems are considered insufficient to carry vasilissan souls properly; the belief that stars are incomparably large gems of ultimate spiritual power was the original motivation for the upward architectural ambition of the queen-led cities -- building tall enough to approach the stars.

## NanoTrasen Contact

NanoTrasen's interest in Varilak was motivated by the planet's exceptional gem and precious metal density as well as the vasilissan capability for architecture and weaving. The contact and subsequent labor arrangement significantly altered the traditional queen-settlement economic model by introducing external currency and demand for off-world exportable goods.

The relationship between vasilissan society and NanoTrasen remains primarily transactional. The homeworld continues to function under its traditional governance structures with corporate presence operating alongside rather than supplanting them. Vasilissans working in the broader galactic economy, including aboard NanoTrasen stations, interact primarily with humans and skrell.
