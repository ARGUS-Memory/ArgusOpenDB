# Ship Naming

All space-capable vessels registered in SolGov space are required to carry three forms of identification: a prefix designating the vessel's nature and ownership, a name identifying the specific craft, and a unique registration number used for administrative tracking.

---

## Prefix

A vessel's prefix consists of three parts read in sequence: owning body, purpose, and type classification.

### Owning Body

The first letter identifies the registering organization. The original system distinguished military government designations from a universal civilian prefix of "C". As trans-stellar corporations grew their private fleets, political pressure resulted in the ability for organizations to license their own prefix letters for a substantial fee. Most major TSCs with significant fleets have adopted proprietary prefix letters. The civilian "C" designation was consequently deprecated and replaced with "I" for Independent, as the original distinction became meaningless. Older vessels may still carry the "C" prefix.

### Purpose

The second letter designates the vessel's intended function. Official definitions are broad and overlap is common; exact designation is left to the registering party. Military vessels may use civilian purpose designations for traffic control interactions. Specific military purpose designations are primarily used within the fleet itself.

### Type

The third letter designates the class of craft: whether a shuttle, station, warship, or other configuration.

---

## Name

A ship's name is entirely at the owner's discretion and may be re-registered at any time. Individual TSCs maintain internal naming schemes; popular naming trends vary by system and era. Some regulations govern profanity and inflammatory designations, though enforcement is loose and provocative names exist in common use.

An unnamed vessel is referred to by its prefix designation and registration number alone.

---

## Registration Number

Upon registration with SolGov, a vessel is assigned a unique registration number that is never reused. The format consists of three letters representing the home system, three digits representing the year of registration, and a randomly assigned six-digit number. Display of the registration number is not required, though some operators with distinctive numbers choose to display them.

---

## Example

The NLS Southern Cross illustrates the prefix system:

- **N** stands for NanoTrasen, the owning corporation.
- **L** stands for Logistical, the vessel's purpose.
- **S** stands for Station, the vessel type.

From the prefix NLS alone, a reader can determine that the Southern Cross is a logistical station belonging to NanoTrasen.
