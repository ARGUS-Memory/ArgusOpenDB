# Bishop Cybernetics

Bishop Cybernetics is a boutique-model trans-stellar corporation specializing in high-quality cybernetic augmentations, prosthetics, synthetic organ replacements, and minor implanted consumer products. The company positions itself in the premium segment of the cybernetics market and is a favored supplier among transhumanist communities, though it draws criticism from bioconservative groups and the Positronic Rights Group alike.

---

## Operations

Bishop operates through smaller, purpose-built facilities rather than mass-market manufacturing lines, which keeps production volumes low and prices high. Products include full prosthetic limb replacements, brain augmentation hardware, synthetic organ substitutes, and minor consumer implants such as wrist-mounted displays. Bishop cyberwear is frequently compared to Vey-Med products in terms of cost: both occupy the premium end of the market.

The company's focus on human augmentation specifically, rather than on positronic or synthetic clients, places it in ideological but not directly economic competition with Morpheus Cyberkinetics. Bishop has received criticism from positronic advocacy organizations for this human-first orientation.

Each ship in Bishop's fleet is named after a notable mechanical engineer, consistent with the company's aesthetic of precision and pedigree.
