# Free Trade Union

The Free Trade Union is a large merchant coalition and the primary advocate for independent commerce in known space. Unlike other trans-stellar entities, it is not a single corporation but a federated network of independent traders, merchant fleets, and trade stations united under a common charter and governance structure. The FTU controls a substantial fleet of trading vessels and combat escorts, operates major trade stations in both human and non-human space, and is the originating body of Tradeband, the widely adopted interstellar commercial lingua franca.

---

## History

The FTU was founded in 2410 by Issac Adler, a merchant and economist who organized the fragmented "Free Merchants" into a formal lobbying and commercial body. The immediate impetus was the combination of high tariffs, corporate-controlled supply chains that excluded independent traders, and a lack of any collective bargaining power against the growing trans-stellar corporations.

From a modest start, the FTU grew into one of the largest commercial organizations in the galaxy by 2450, with the majority of independent merchants affiliated by 2500. The organization simultaneously promoted Tradeband as a universal trading language, which spread its adoption rapidly throughout non-aligned merchant communities.

The FTU has faced persistent accusations of price-fixing and cartel behavior, charges it denies. The legal tension between the FTU and the trans-stellar corporations it was formed to counterbalance remains ongoing.

---

## Operations

The FTU operates a decentralized fleet of autonomous trading vessels, with a separately commanded combat fleet that responds to central command for escort and protection duties. Its trade stations are found throughout known space, including in non-human regions, and feature duty-free commercial zones where products unavailable or heavily taxed elsewhere are sold at reduced prices. This makes FTU stations attractive stops for crew with personal commercial interests.

The FTU does not employ crew directly in the NanoTrasen sense: membership is a commercial relationship rather than employment. Independent traders who join access collective resources, legal representation, and protection in exchange for adherence to FTU trade rules.

---

## Station Relevance

Cargo departments occasionally interact with FTU-registered merchants when processing unusual procurement requests or dealing with independent supply chains. FTU contacts may be accessible through certain communication channels. FTU-flagged vessels in port are generally treated as neutral parties.
