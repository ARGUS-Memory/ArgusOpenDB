# Asset Protection

Asset Protection is a NanoTrasen special response division operating under Central Command authority. It deploys only in response to the most severe threat classifications: full crew mutiny, extreme hostile takeover, xenobiological contamination of the entire population, or hostage situations involving senior command personnel. Asset Protection teams are equipped and trained beyond Emergency Response Team standards and operate with near-absolute authority on any station they board.

---

## Deployment Criteria

Asset Protection is not dispatched for standard security failures or moderate emergencies. Deployment requires a Central Command determination that the situation has escalated beyond ERT capacity. Situations that have historically triggered Asset Protection deployment include:

- Active mutiny with command authority fully compromised
- Hostile alien lifeforms replacing or killing the majority of the crew
- Mercenary or paramilitary incursion that security forces cannot contain
- Senior command staff held hostage with no viable rescue option

---

## Personnel and Equipment

Asset Protection troopers are issued equipment well beyond standard NanoTrasen security issue. Standard loadout includes personal choice of energy and ballistic weapons up to heavy military hardware including pulse rifles and surplus USDF service rifles, melee weapons, energy shields, C4 demolition charges, a full grenade selection, and advanced Asset Protection hardsuits. All personnel carry first aid kits and optical thermal scanners.

In sufficiently severe scenarios, Asset Protection teams may arrive with military-grade exosuits including Seraphs, Marauders, and Gorilla-class mechs. These assets are reserved for situations where standard personnel face casualties from threats capable of killing equipped troopers in seconds.

---

## Operating Mandate

Asset Protection teams operate under orders issued by a Central Command Official or Admiral before deployment. On arrival, the team secures priority areas, eliminates confirmed threats, and patrols until cleared. Crew members who are not confirmed threats are expected to cooperate; crew who interfere with the team's stated objectives are treated as threats. The team extracts once objectives are confirmed complete.

In extreme cases, Asset Protection may be issued a nuclear device and authorization codes with orders to deny the station to hostile forces.

> [!CAUTION]
> Asset Protection arrival indicates that Central Command has determined the situation cannot be resolved by the crew or by standard emergency teams. Crew should identify themselves clearly, avoid appearing threatening, and comply with instructions immediately.
