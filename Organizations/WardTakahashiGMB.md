# Ward-Takahashi GMB

Ward-Takahashi GMB is one of the Big 7 trans-stellar corporations, producing consumer electronics, home appliances, small arms, and a broad range of finished manufactured goods. The company is best known for the near-ubiquity of its chips in consumer electronics and its Chevron line of sporting firearms.

---

## History

Ward-Takahashi was formed in 2512 from the merger of two established Earth corporations: Ward G.M.B. and Takahashi Electronics.

Ward G.M.B. originated as a military contractor before pivoting to the civilian arms market in the mid-2400s. Its Chevron range of sporting rifles, marketed on status and self-defense, became a commercial success that the company leveraged into sports equipment, precision tooling, and machined parts. By 2460, Ward held significant market share in weapons, sporting goods, and tools, along with a near-complete monopoly on high-quality machined parts in human space.

Takahashi Electronics was founded as a non-profit electronics design firm before shareholders forced its transformation into an aggressive licensing business. By 2450 the Takahashi name dominated consumer electronics and home appliances, with patents actively enforced through government-level relationships.

The 2512 merger was unexpected given the disparate industries, but the combination proved profitable as both companies rode the phoron boom, furnishing new mining colonies and arming their security forces simultaneously.

---

## Operations

Ward-Takahashi's chip fabrication is embedded in virtually all consumer electronics regardless of brand. The company maintains extensive licensing agreements and manufacturing facilities across human space. Their security force is large, well-trained, and occasionally contracted out; allegations of its use as a patent enforcement tool or as contracted muscle for other trans-stellars surface periodically.

Ward-Takahashi competes directly with Hephaestus Industries in small arms and with other electronics manufacturers in consumer goods, but its market position is secured by the depth of its supply chain integration.

---

## Station Relevance

PDAs, portable computers, standard-issue station equipment, and various small arms seen aboard NanoTrasen facilities are frequently Ward-Takahashi products or contain Ward-Takahashi components. The WT logo is a common sight on consumer hardware throughout the station.
