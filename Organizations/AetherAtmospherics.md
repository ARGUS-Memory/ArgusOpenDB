# Aether Atmospherics and Recycling

Aether Atmospherics and Recycling is the primary provider of atmospheric life support systems, gas distribution infrastructure, and recycling services across human space. Founded on environmental principles during the early colonial period, Aether has evolved from a sustainability-focused transport venture into the backbone of breathable atmosphere maintenance across Sol and beyond.

---

## History

Aether traces its origins to Arthur Greyson, CEO of Expansive Visions Transportation, whose cargo shuttles were repurposed during the early Lunar colony period to supply breathable air to struggling settlements. His team developed a superior atmospheric filtration and recycling system, and the technology was subsequently extended to Martian colonies after the formation of Sol Central. The company was renamed Aether Atmospherics and Recycling to reflect its new direction.

With the Sol colonies stabilized, Aether expanded into life support manufacturing, gas distribution contracting, and recycled goods resale. Environmental advocacy groups provided political backing and some funding, embedding Aether's identity in the green movement of the era. This alignment cemented Aether as the atmospheric systems provider of choice as Sol's colonies expanded, eventually earning the company trans-stellar status.

---

## Operations

Aether's primary business is the installation and maintenance of atmospheric systems across stations, ships, and colonies. Most of their revenue comes from long-term maintenance contracts rather than hardware sales. Gas byproducts filtered from maintained atmospheres are collected and resold as secondary commodities.

Aether maintains a large contracted workforce distributed throughout human space. Their presence is often invisible to crew -- the ventilation and life support systems aboard any well-run station are almost certainly running on Aether-designed infrastructure, with Aether technicians on retainer for servicing.

---

## Station Relevance

Aether-designed atmospheric components are embedded in station infrastructure throughout the NanoTrasen network. Atmospheric technicians and engineers should be aware that Aether holds installation documentation for most life support systems. In practice, crew working with atmospheric machinery will often find Aether service markings on older components.
