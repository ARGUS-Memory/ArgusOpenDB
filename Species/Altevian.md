# Altevian

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Nomadic rodent-featured humanoids with no homeworld, altevians live permanently aboard massive colony-class vessels they call their home fleet, sustaining a spacefaring civilization that has adapted entirely to long-duration transit as a way of life.

## Biology

Altevians are tall bipedal humanoids with pronounced rodent features: prominent front teeth, large mobile ears, long bare tails, and fine body fur. Physical proportions are on the tall end for sapient species, contrasting with the small size of their Earth-rodent analogues.

Altevians have a notably low caloric intake requirement relative to their body mass. Their metabolic rate is substantially lower than comparative-size species, allowing them to maintain health on rations that would be insufficient for most other sapients. This trait is well-adapted to long-duration spaceflight where food stores are a limiting resource.

Altevians are naturally capable of controlled movement in zero-gravity environments without external assistance, a trait that developed through generations of life aboard ships with imperfect or absent gravity plating. Their zero-G mobility is not exceptional, but it is reliable where other species would flounder.

## The Fleet

The altevian fleet consists of massive super-capital class colony ships organized around a flagship that serves as the cultural and political center of the civilization. Individual ships within the fleet are large enough to house the equivalent of small cities. No altevian homeworld exists; the fleet has been the altevian home for long enough that planetary living is culturally associated with other species rather than themselves.

The fleet's structure is hierarchical, with the flagship holding the highest status and ship captains holding significant social authority over their populations. Movement between ships is permitted but involves formal social protocols. Individuals who leave the fleet for NanoTrasen or other external employment are not unusual; they maintain fleet affiliation as a cultural identity regardless of current location.

---

## Culture

Altevian cultural values center on the fleet as the fundamental social unit. Loyalty to fleet hierarchy is considered a baseline expectation rather than an optional choice. The fleet's survival is collectively prioritized over individual preference.

Practical competence is respected and rewarded. Altevians who demonstrate genuine expertise in any field useful to the fleet earn standing. The nomadic necessity of maintaining all fleet functions continuously means that idle or non-contributing individuals are poorly regarded.

Altevians operating on NanoTrasen installations typically adapt well to the structured hierarchy of a station environment, finding parallels with fleet command structure familiar. Individual altevians may maintain a pragmatic and transactional orientation toward employment, viewing their station posting as an extension of the fleet's economic activity rather than a long-term commitment.

## Language

**Tavan** is the altevian native language, described as a language native to the altevians that has been adopted by other rodent-adjacent spacefaring species over time, suggesting extensive historical contact between altevians and related populations. Station communications systems include Tavan language support accessible by prefixing speech with `#E`.

Galactic Common is widely spoken by altevians who have had off-fleet commercial contact. Accent in Galactic Common reflects the altevian vocal structure, with characteristic rodent-like tonal qualities.

## Medical and Operational Notes

Altevians are vulnerable to burn and fire damage (burn_mod=1.15). Their fur creates combustion risk in fire environments and thermal injuries progress more quickly than in non-furred species.

Caloric requirements are low; altevian crew members are not at risk of undernourishment on standard station food provisions and can tolerate reduced rations without adverse health effects for extended periods. Medical personnel assessing altevian nutritional status should not compare intake to standard-species baselines.

Zero-gravity environments do not cause the disorientation and physical distress that many other species experience. Altevian crew are appropriate for assignments involving extended EVA or zero-gravity operational contexts.

Standard damage assessment and surgical procedures apply. No specific medical abnormalities beyond the above have been documented.
