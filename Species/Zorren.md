# Zorren

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Small humanoids native to Virgo Prime, zorren are the remnant population of a once-interplanetary civilization that collapsed following a planetary cataclysm, currently at a late-medieval technological level and navigating contact with NanoTrasen operations in the Virgo-Erigone system.

## Biology

Zorren are bipedal humanoids sharing the standard organ system organization found across most galactic sapients, with minor physiological differences.

The most notable distinction is their circulatory fluid. Zorren blood contains no red blood cells. Oxygen is transported by free-floating hemocyanin proteins, which use copper rather than iron as a binding element. The result is blood that is light and nearly transparent when deoxygenated but turns deep blue upon oxygen exposure, whether through normal respiration or external wound exposure.

Average height is 132 cm, though this figure is suppressed by chronic nutritional shortfall on the homeworld. Genetic analysis of well-nourished individuals suggests a natural height ceiling closer to 145 cm, with a recorded maximum of 168 cm. Sexual dimorphism is subtle; females have wider hips and smaller shoulders. Body fat distribution differences between individuals at similar nutrition levels are more strongly related to genetics than sex.

Reproduction is sexual with internal fertilization. Gestation lasts 32 to 34 weeks, typically producing litters of one or two. Childbirth carries elevated maternal mortality without adequate medical support.

Children are born blind and dependent. Sexual maturity is reached at 15, considered adult by most zorren cultures. Full physical maturity is reached at 20. Without access to modern medical care, life expectancy averages 50 years. Genetic profiling indicates the species is capable of reaching 90 years under adequate conditions.

## Highland Zorren

The Highland designation traces to early NT contact, when zorren noble ambassadors described themselves as of higher standing. Highland Zorren are sedentary and organized under a rigid hereditary caste system with minimal upward mobility.

### Castes

**Bound to Land (73% of Highland Zorren):** Agricultural serfs. Cannot move from their birthplace without lord permission, cannot marry or change occupation without permission, and are required to provide approximately 20 days of labor per year to the feudal lord's lands. Do not bear arms except under levy in wartime. A serf can achieve freedom by demonstrating exceptional military valor during levy service, becoming an Armsbounder, or by purchasing freedom for themselves and all future offspring, an extremely rare event that typically marks the completion of generational land reclamation.

**Bound to Arms (26% of Highland Zorren, 4% of these wealthy):** Free persons with no restriction on residence or occupation, in exchange for military service under the Fyrdlevy when called. Must supply their own weapons and equipment; readiness and composition vary accordingly. Wealthy Armsbounders can purchase exemption from military service. Promotion to Bloodbounder requires extraordinary service or a life-saving act for a Bloodbounder.

**Bound to Blood (1% of Highland Zorren):** Noble ruling class. Literate, educated, and in control of all government, military command, and founding of new settlements. Inheritance follows cognatic primogeniture; the firstborn regardless of sex inherits all titles. Noble titles include Trelite (rural, lowest), Proproctor (mid-tier), and Proctor (highest encountered). Former Prelate suzerains existed in recent history but were eliminated through internal conflict.

### The Ardent Flame

The Ardent Flame is the religious tradition of the Highland Zorren, led by the noble caste. The faith holds that the fall of the zorren's precursor civilization was a consequence of excess and waste, and that the present generation are custodians tasked with restoring order to Virgo Prime. The Ardent Flame serves as both a source of legitimacy for Bloodbounder rule and an anchor for the lower castes in difficult material conditions. The faith is also used to argue against structural reform of the caste system.

## Flatlander Zorren

Flatlander Zorren are predominantly nomadic, occupying the wastelands and frontier territories of Virgo Prime. The "Flatfooter" designation was applied by Highland Zorren ambassadors as a dismissal; the label stuck in NT records.

Flatlander society is organized around bannerlords: charismatic leaders whose authority derives from demonstrated competence and personal following rather than hereditary title. Bannerlord groups are mobile, frequently reorganizing as individuals leave or join based on the bannerlord's fortunes. A bannerlord's standing depends on continued success in providing for and protecting followers.

Social mobility is substantially higher than in Highland society. Identity and obligation are tied to the current bannerlord rather than to a permanent feudal structure. Trade, raiding, and salvage of precursor technology are common activities.

### The Broken Cycle

The religion of the Flatlander Zorren holds that the great cataclysm ended the natural cycle of zorren civilization, and that the cycle must be deliberately broken and remade rather than merely restored. The faith encourages engagement with alien technology and knowledge as tools toward this remade future, in contrast to the Ardent Flame's emphasis on restoration through order and hierarchy.

## Interspecies Relations

**NanoTrasen:** NT's interest in Virgo Prime is primarily resource-driven, centered on phoron extraction. Zorren interaction with NT is ongoing but secondary to NT's operational priorities. Some zorren have integrated into NT operations directly, most commonly in lower and generalist roles given their low-technology homeworld background. Medical and scientific positions are uncommon due to the educational gap.

## Medical and Operational Notes

**Blood color:** Zorren blood is blue when oxygenated. Standard wound assessment protocols are applicable; the color difference does not indicate altered chemistry requiring modified treatment.

**Height:** Zorren are significantly shorter than most station-standard species. Equipment ergonomics should be adjusted accordingly. Standard-size work surfaces, consoles, and protective gear may require modification.

**Dietary:** No species-wide dietary restriction. Nutritional requirements match the general omnivore standard. Zorren from Virgo Prime may arrive with deficiencies from low-availability homeworld diets; nutritional screening is advisable.

**Technology background:** Zorren from Virgo Prime generally lack formal training in advanced technology. Role assignments should reflect this. Technical roles require additional assessment before placement.
