# Teshari

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Small, cold-adapted omnivores uplifted by the skrell, teshari are a pack-organized species with exceptionally acute hearing, a highly communal social structure, and an increasingly independent presence on the galactic stage.

## Biology

Teshari are small bipeds, rarely exceeding 1.0 to 1.2 meters in height and seldom reaching 45 kg in body mass. Sexual dimorphism between males and females is not readily apparent. Natural coloration runs to earthen tones; richer individuals sometimes dye feathers and skin.

The species evolved in polar and sub-polar environments and retains strong physiological adaptations for cold. Their skin functions as an effective thermal insulator. Metabolic processes are not optimized for energy efficiency; high ambient temperatures produce significant physiological stress. Teshari cannot cope well with heat and require environmental controls on warm postings.

Hearing is exceptionally sensitive. Teshari can detect movement through solid partitions under normal station conditions. This sensitivity comes at a corresponding cost: sonic weapons and high-intensity acoustic events cause disproportionate harm. Standard flashbang exposure is significantly more dangerous to teshari than to most crew species.

Teshari are omnivorous with a preference for meat where available.

Reproduction involves egg-laying. A breeding pair, typically supported by their pack, tends a clutch until hatching. Pack members share collective responsibility for offspring, with biological parentage carrying less formal weight than the pack relationship as a whole.

## Culture and Society

Teshari culture is highly communal. Privacy carries little value in traditional teshari social life; communal eating, grooming, and shared sleeping spaces are normal. Packs typically share sleeping quarters, a practice originating in heat conservation that has been retained as a social signal of trust. Teshari more integrated with skrell or other spacefaring cultures develop greater awareness of outside species' privacy norms, though full adoption is uncommon.

Some skrell cities with teshari populations maintain dedicated residential districts. These quarters tend toward open construction heavily distinct from standard skrell architecture, functioning practically as a single extended dwelling.

The central teshari government is a grand parliament drawing representatives from major teshari populations across known space. A skrellian representative position was originally included in the parliamentary structure as an advisory role; this position has gradually become ceremonial rather than active as teshari political confidence has grown.

The teshari military is minimal. The Teshari Auxiliary Spacefleet handles logistical and support functions with a small number of warships, including the TAS Raptora Victore (destroyer, fleet flagship) and the TAS Curiosity's Endeavor (corvette, experimental weapons platform). Both are teshari-built. Most of the fleet consists of donated and retrofitted skrellian vessels.

Teshari technology derives primarily from skrellian knowledge bases, though indigenous teshari production is increasing.

## Known Packs

**Eshi:** Large, politically neutral, and heavily involved in efforts to place teshari aboard spacecraft. The most common pack encountered outside skrellian colonial space and likely the most numerous teshari pack beyond the homeworld of Sirisai.

**Nasemari:** A small pack of approximately thirty individuals, dedicated to acquiring technical and engineering skills for transfer back to Sirisai. Despite size, the pack owns and operates a nuclear power plant.

**Schasaraca:** Among the most skrell-integrated packs. Works primarily as scientists and field researchers on skrellian projects, mainly in biology and technical research. Carries a reputation among other teshari as informants for skrellian governments.

**Ceea:** An isolationist pack of approximately sixty from the northern tundra of Sirisai. Culture organized around cultural study, anthropology, and archaeology, originally to recover history suppressed or lost under skrellian contact. Rarely encountered off-world.

**Resca:** A pack that exchanged its native territory for access to spacecraft. Strong musical tradition. Tends toward medical professions.

## Interspecies Relations

**Skrell:** The teshari are a formal skrellian protectorate. The relationship is close and carries significant weight in teshari cultural identity, though some packs maintain skepticism toward skrellian intentions. At first contact when the skrell introduced the teshari to human diplomats, the teshari representative was mistaken for an exotic pet. Relations with humanity subsequently normalized.

**Humanity:** Functional. Teshari hold a negligible position in human astropolitical calculations; most humans adopt the skrellian framing of the teshari relationship without significant independent assessment.

## Medical and Operational Notes

**Heat vulnerability:** Teshari are physiologically adapted for cold. High ambient temperatures produce disproportionate stress. Environmental postings should account for this. Standard human-comfortable temperatures may be marginal for teshari on extended assignments.

**Sonic weapons:** Teshari hearing sensitivity is exceptionally acute by sapient standards, and acoustic trauma is documented as a significant vulnerability. Security staff should note this before deploying sonic incapacitation measures in areas with teshari crew.

**Size:** Standard equipment may not fit teshari without modification. Medical instruments calibrated to human or skrell dimensions should be adjusted for body size.

**Dietary notes:** Omnivorous. No species-wide dietary restriction. Preference for meat-inclusive diet; adjust nutritional provisioning accordingly.
