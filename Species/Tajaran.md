# Tajaran

<div style="height:3px;background:linear-gradient(90deg,#3F5974 60%,transparent);margin-bottom:20px"></div>

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Furred, digitigrade feline-analogue bipeds from the planet Meralar, tajaran are distinguished by diverse ethnic subgroups, multiple distinct languages, strong cold adaptation, and a complex relationship with humanity following their recent uplift into FTL capability.

## Biology

Tajaran are omnivorous bipedal mammal-analogues. The body is covered almost entirely in a dense fur coat, with bare skin only on the palms, soles, and directly around mucous membranes. Neck and head fur frequently forms a shaggy mane of coarser fiber. Fur color is typically solid with a darker mane; striped or patterned variants exist in some ethnicities.

Adult males range between 150 and 200 cm in height and 50 to 95 kg in mass. Females are typically slightly smaller. The body is generally lithe and highly flexible. Sexual dimorphism in body proportions is present but less pronounced than in humans, with degree varying by ethnicity and culture.

Eyes are large and well-developed, positioned above a slightly elongated muzzle. Pupils contract to a horizontal aperture in bright conditions; the species has substantially better low-light vision than humans. Ear structure on the top of the skull allows rotation up to 90 degrees and full rearward folding; hearing range and acuity exceed human baseline. Tajaran teeth are sharp at the front and thicken toward the rear; no significant grinding molar development is present.

Hands and feet have five digits with short claw-analogues. The stance is digitigrade; lower-body equipment designed for plantigrade species requires modification. A flexible, non-prehensile muscular tail extends from the base of the torso.

The standard waking period is 30 to 32 hours, followed by 11 to 13 hours of sleep, often with short naps during the waking period. Tajaran can maintain alertness for up to 75 hours but degrade with extended sleep avoidance.

The average lifespan is approximately 70 years, with statistical projections anticipating extension to 90 or 100 years as medical technology improves.

## Ethnicities

### Hharar

The most numerous tajaran ethnicity, most heavily represented in early human-tajaran diplomatic contact and therefore widely treated as the reference ethnicity by outsiders. Fur is typically silky, long, and thick, in shades from umber to pale tan.

**Siik:** Original settlers of the southern warm belts of Zarraya, with a history of kingdoms, empires, and city-states following the collapse of the Second Selem Empire. Speak Siik.

**Hakar:** Settlers of Hakarraya under the First Selem Empire, later organized under absolute God-King monarchies each claiming sole legitimate descent from the old imperial line. Speak Hakari.

**Selem:** Descendants of the ruling class of the First and Second Selem Empires. The Third Selem Empire under House Hadii is the current successor state, openly pursuing reunification of Zarrayan city-states. Speak Siikelem.

**Rhemazar:** Zhan-Khazan who settled in former Selem imperial territory following the fall of the Second Empire and gradually assimilated Siik cultural elements. Leaner than their Zhan-Khazan relatives due to interbreeding. Governed by a Noble Federation under a High Prince. Speak a Siik-Rrhangarr hybrid.

### Njarir'Akhan

Taller and more streamlined than the Hharar. Fur is thick, velvety, and water-resistant; the species migrated to archipelago and coastal environments thousands of years ago. Facial features are softer and more rounded; movement is characterized as more sinuous.

**Njarjirii:** Equatorial island-based maritime culture organized around house loyalty, individual heroism, and controlled feuding. Historically the primary tajaran long-distance traders; formed the first spacefaring tajaran crews. Speak Akhani.

**Nerahni:** A Njarjirii house originally hired for sailing expertise by the First Selem Empire. Culture organized around ship ownership across generations; ships serve as both home and historical record. Contributed extensively to early tajaran mathematical and navigational science. Speak a sailor-accented Akhani variant.

**Siirkhani:** Descendants of followers of the prophet Raniira Merar. Highly insular religious community practicing asceticism, historical preservation, and strict endogamy. Tolerated in most jurisdictions; suppressed in Hakar kingdoms.

### Zhan-Khazan

Generally taller and more heavily built than either Hharar or Njarir'Akhan. Average height 180 to 225 cm, average weight 70 to 110 kg. Eyes are proportionally smaller; jaw structure is heavier. Fur tends toward white or off-grey with heavy ear tufting.

**"Zhan-Khazan":** Northern nomadic raiders, relying on ice fishing and animal herding. Social organization is fluid and reorganizes around the rise and fall of Zhan-Rhezar leaders. Speak Rrhangarr.

**Shankamyan:** A branch that crossed to the eastern continent of Meralar. Extremely territorial and hostile to outside contact; diplomatic contact has been impossible. Do not venture off-world.

## Language

**Siik:** A complex trade language developed from the agricultural communities of Zarraya's temperate zone. Incorporates extensive pitch variation, yowls, and trills. Uses third-person self-reference as a marker of social distance; first-person marks trust or affection. Station communications systems include Siik language support accessible by prefixing speech with `#j`.

**Hakari:** A simplified and modified version of ancient Selem, developed as the holy language of the Hakar kingdoms. Simple grammar with many exceptions; also uses third-person self-reference.

**Siikelem:** A revived and modernized form of ancient Selem, merged with Siik grammar. The prestige language of the Third Selem Empire.

**Rrhangarr:** A simple, direct northern language with heavy emphasis on the R consonant and context-based grammar. Useful as a trade language on Meralar outside coastal areas.

**Akhani:** The language of the Njarir'Akhan, with formal and structured grammar. Also uses compound nouns extensively.

Tajaran speaking Galactic Common typically roll and extend R sounds. This can be consciously suppressed.

## Religion

**Tajr-kii Rarkajar** ("We who follow Rarkajar") is the predominant religious tradition of the Selem cultural sphere, with influence across much of tajaran society. Less common among Siik and Rhemazar; rare among Hakar, who center their theology on God-Emperor worship.

The belief system holds that after death, souls travel to the realm of Mephir (*The Bounty*), a wasteland where they are drained of energy and eventually destroyed. Rarkajar, a divine entity, actively searches for souls and extracts them to safety, allowing them to join a collective knowledge network. Souls who wish to leave may be reincarnated. Souls that have undergone many reincarnations are especially valuable and therefore especially targeted by Mephir, requiring greater intervention by Rarkajar.

Rarkajar is understood as unchanging and fundamentally stronger than Mephir in direct conflict, but unable to adapt to her indirect methods, making the contest ongoing.

## Interspecies Relations

**Humanity:** Humanity uplifted the tajaran into FTL capability, a fact neither species forgets easily. Human employers routinely exploit tajaran migrant labor. Tajaran hold a mixture of acknowledged debt and wounded pride toward humanity. The relationship is fundamentally unequal and a persistent source of tension.

**Other species:** Tajaran maintain generally aloof but cordial trading relationships across known space. Formal relations with most species remain within the diplomatic and commercial sphere.

## Medical and Operational Notes

**Cold resistance:** Tajaran fur provides meaningful insulation. Cold temperatures produce significantly less physiological harm than in most crew species.

**Heat vulnerability:** The same thermal insulation that helps in cold worsens heat stress. Elevated ambient temperatures produce disproportionate harm. Tajaran begin experiencing heat stress at lower temperatures than human-baseline crew. Environmental controls for areas with tajaran crew should account for this.

**Physical fragility:** Tajaran take disproportionate harm from blunt and physical trauma. Standard trauma assessment protocols apply, but injury severity will be higher than expected for equivalent impacts.

**Flash and high-intensity light:** Tajaran are more sensitive to intense light exposure than most species. Flash grenades and similar devices produce elevated disorientation and harm.

**Claws:** Unarmed strikes from tajaran produce scratch-type injuries rather than blunt trauma. Medical assessment should treat lacerations, not contusions, as the primary injury type from unarmed tajaran conflict.

**Sleep cycle:** Tajaran require approximately 11 to 13 hours of sleep following each 30 to 32 hour waking period. Scheduling systems that assume a standard human 8-hour sleep cycle may result in chronic fatigue in tajaran crew on extended rotation.

**Equipment:** Digitigrade lower body requires modification of most lower-limb equipment including voidsuits and footwear. Standard hardsuits and exosuits designed for plantigrade species are generally not compatible without tailoring.

**Dietary:** Omnivorous with strong preference for meat and fruit. Limited grinding molar development means fibrous plant matter is poorly processed.
