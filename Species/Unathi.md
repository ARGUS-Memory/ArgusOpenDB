# Unathi

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Large, cold-blooded reptilian bipeds from the planet Moghes, unathi are characterized by extreme physical durability, a continuous growth pattern throughout life, and a deeply clan-organized religious society currently navigating the aftermath of armed conflict with SolGov.

## Biology

Unathi are carnivorous reptilian bipeds. Back scales are hard and rigid; the scales on the inner legs, armpits, groin, and underbelly are softer but still substantial. Coloration tends toward high-contrast neutral patterns of grey, tan, or green. Albino individuals carry special cultural significance in several unathi traditions.

Adults range from 200 to 260 cm in height and 250 to 400 kg in mass. Females tend to be slightly larger than males of equivalent age. Unathi never stop growing; growth rate slows with age but does not stop. Exceptionally old individuals who adopt zero-gravity or aquatic lifestyles have been recorded living past three centuries. Most unathi die before 300 years from the progressive strain of gravity on increasing body mass.

The ribcage is especially dense and extends from the chest down to the pelvis, protecting all major abdominal organs. This produces a broad stature and near-universal bow-legged stance. The bone density slows movement on land but contributes to the species' durability. Non-vital body parts including limbs can be regenerated over a period of years if the individual can survive the interim.

Unathi have two hearts positioned across the chest, producing a double-beat sound audible during cardiac assessment. They lack kidneys; toxin filtration is handled by a single combined organ performing the functions of both liver and kidneys.

As a cold-blooded species, unathi are vulnerable to extreme cold, which can induce fatigue or short comas. They tolerate heat well.

Unathi have an ellipsoid skull, forward-facing slit-pupil eyes, two rows of regrowable serrated teeth, and a long forked tongue used for air-sampling. Some individuals develop head spines, frills, or horns based on ancestry. A line of dorsal spines may run from the base of the skull to the tail; these raise when the individual is agitated.

## Life Cycle

**Hatchling:** Females lay clutches of one to three eggs with a six-month gestation period. After two to three weeks in a warm, humid environment, hatchlings emerge. Newly hatched unathi are small, effectively non-sapient, and quadrupedal. They exhibit aggressive territorial behavior toward non-family. The clan structure evolved partly to protect hatchlings from predation and from each other during this stage.

**Infancy and Childhood:** At around three years the hatchling begins recognizing family members and transitions from territorial to pack-hunting behavior. Bipedal movement develops around age five. Rudimentary language comprehension begins around age five to six. Parents formally name children after language acquisition, around age nine.

**Adolescence:** Extended ribplate development begins at fifteen, a painful process comparable to teething. After this, a young unathi enters a Unity education program matched to early aptitude. By age 24, a primary vocational track is established and apprenticeship begins.

**Adulthood:** Physical maturity occurs around age 32. Formal adult induction into the clan occurs at age 40, or as early as 30 for exceptional individuals at clan elder discretion. Pair-bonding typically does not occur until after age 80. By approximately age 200 the individual reaches master-craft status in their chosen field.

## Cultural Backgrounds

**Unity Clans:** The dominant cultural grouping, organized under the Moghes Hegemony. Unity doctrine holds that all unathi actions should benefit the species as a whole in preparation for a final cosmic conflict. Clans are governed by a Circle of Elders; the Grand Elder position is elected approximately every ten years from this circle and holds final authority on tied votes. The Grand Elder is understood to serve the clan above family interest.

**Heretic Clans:** A catch-all term for clans that have departed from Unity dogma. Not a unified group; heretic clans have occasionally gained enough influence to reshape Unity doctrine rather than be suppressed. The most prominent are the Abel's Rest clans, which integrated with human colonists following the Human-Unathi Conflict and have developed a significant less-xenophobic political position with some traction in the Hegemony.

**Unbound:** Unathi who have surrendered or been stripped of clan ties through personal choice or punishment for serious crimes. Regarded as lacking legitimate clan standing. Gather in city slums or wilderness. Entry into the unathi afterlife is considered jeopardized without clan resources and preparation. Some Unbound adopt self-given titles. Unauthorized use of a former clan name is illegal and carries additional penalties for those outside clan membership.

**Redeemers:** A large organization drawing almost exclusively from Unbound who seek renewed purpose. Initiates begin stripped of all names, undertake years or centuries of ascetic labor and religious study, and may eventually be renamed as Redeemed by their sponsor. Full Redeemers who have themselves brought five Initiates to completion may be elevated to Redeemer status and a leadership role. During the Human-Unathi Conflict, Redeemer-led formations in breacher suits were among the most effective and feared unathi combat forces.

## Language

**Sinta'Unathi** is the primary unathi language, incorporating natural snarls and hissing. Regional grammar variation exists but is managed by Hegemony linguists. Sinta'Unathi can be accessed on station by the `#o` channel prefix.

Unathi address outsiders by clan or family name rather than personal name. Addressing a clan unathi by personal name without permission is considered a serious breach of etiquette.

Unathi speaking Galactic Common carry a hissing, guttural accent.

## Interspecies Relations

**Humanity and SolGov:** Relations remain strained following the Human-Unathi Conflict. SolGov achieved victory but continues to regard Moghes with significant concern. Anti-unathi sentiment persists widely across SolGov space. Waves of xenophobic violence recur despite official diplomatic efforts.

**NanoTrasen:** Anti-unathi employment discrimination is common within NT and affiliated organizations, particularly for supervisory, command, and security-adjacent roles. Unathi are more likely to find employment on border and outskirts installations where scrutiny is lower.

## Medical and Operational Notes

**Ribcage surgery:** The unathi ribcage extends to the pelvis. Surgical access to abdominal organs requires ribcage incision. Standard human abdominal surgical protocols are not directly applicable.

**Organ differences:** No kidneys. Toxin filtration is performed by a single combined liver-kidney analog. Organ damage assessment and chemical treatment must account for this difference.

**Cold vulnerability:** Severe cold causes fatigue and coma. Environmental controls for areas with unathi crew should prevent extended low-temperature exposure.

**Heat tolerance:** Unathi tolerate heat significantly better than most other crew species.

**Equipment:** Unathi cannot wear hardsuits not specifically tailored to their anatomy. Standard soft spacesuits, biosuits, and fire suits are compatible. Breacher suits are the standard unathi hardsuit form. Footwear and lower-body equipment requires modification to accommodate tail and claw structure.

**Regeneration:** Non-vital body parts including limbs regenerate over time if the individual survives the period without them. This process takes years. Standard prosthetics should be offered as functional interim solutions.
