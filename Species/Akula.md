# Akula

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Amphibious humanoids of shark-like appearance, akula are a skrell client race whose genome was modified to allow extended air-breathing, making them functional in standard station atmospheres while remaining dependent on high-humidity environments for sustained health.

## Biology

Akula are bipedal humanoids with physical features analogous to sharks: streamlined body proportions, a cartilage-dominant skeletal structure, and coloration patterns typical of large aquatic predators. They evolved as underwater tribal creatures and were discovered in that state by skrell survey teams, who initially assessed them as non-sapient fauna.

Akula proved to be rapid learners and were reclassified as sapients after early contact demonstrated both tool use and structured language within a short observation period. They are described as the first sapient life the skrell encountered other than themselves; this priority has historical significance in akula-skrell relations and forms part of the foundational narrative of their alliance.

Following uplift contact, skrell genetic engineering modified the akula respiratory and circulatory systems to enable extended survival in open air. Pre-modification, akula could only tolerate brief surface exposure. Post-modification, akula can operate indefinitely in standard atmosphere. However, the modification did not eliminate their biological water dependency. Without access to high-humidity environments, akula skin and gill structures begin to dry out within days, producing progressive physiological distress. Life on arid worlds like Virgo Prime is only sustainable for akula through technology that provides the necessary humidity.

Akula blood is a distinct blue color, resulting from a copper-based oxygen transport mechanism analogous to that of zorren, though the specific biochemical implementation differs.

## History

Akula civilization developed underwater in relative isolation over an extended period before skrell contact. The exact timeline of akula societal development prior to skrell discovery is not fully documented in materials available to NanoTrasen personnel. Following reclassification as sapients, akula entered into a formal alliance with the skrell Inner Kingdoms, receiving technological assistance that accelerated their development across multiple domains.

The genetic modification enabling air-breathing was performed with akula consent under the terms of the alliance and is regarded by akula as a foundational event in their modern identity. Most akula currently operating in SolGov space do so in connection with skrell diplomatic or commercial interests, though fully independent akula individuals are not uncommon on mixed-species installations.

## Language

The primary language used by akula encountered in SolGov space is **Spacer**, a pidgin developed from Tradeband, Gutter, and Sol Common used across various space-born human communities. Its adoption by akula reflects the practical reality of operating in predominantly human-influenced commercial environments. Station communications systems include Spacer language support accessible by prefixing speech with `#J`.

Akula in close contact with skrell also frequently speak Skrellian. Galactic Common fluency is standard among akula working on NanoTrasen installations.

## Medical and Operational Notes

The primary environmental requirement for akula is humidity. Standard station atmosphere is breathable but does not provide sufficient moisture for sustained skin and gill health. Medical personnel and facility operators should ensure access to humidified spaces or regular moisture treatment for akula crew serving extended assignments. Signs of desiccation include skin cracking, increased respiratory distress, and gill irritation.

Akula blood is blue, resulting from copper-based hemocyanin. Standard diagnostic equipment calibrated to red-blood species will require parameter adjustment. Blood reagents for akula are based on copper rather than iron.

Akula have a natural water-movement advantage and can navigate deep water environments with reduced resistance. This capability may be relevant in emergency scenarios involving flooding.

No specific damage vulnerability or resistance profile deviating from standard has been confirmed. Standard surgical and pharmaceutical procedures apply with normal considerations.
