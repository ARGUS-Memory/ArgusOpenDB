# Zaddat

<div style="height:3px;background:linear-gradient(90deg,#3F5974 60%,transparent);margin-bottom:20px"></div>

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Unathi client race from the high-pressure post-industrial world of Xohox, zaddat require specialized pressure suits to survive in standard planetary and station atmospheres, and have migrated in large numbers into SolGov space following the near-total collapse of their homeworld's biosphere.

## Biology

Zaddat physiology blends features resembling Earth insect and aquatic invertebrate body plans. A chitinous carapace covers a weak endoskeleton made of partially-calcified collagen. Facial mandibles retract into the skull when not in use and extend for eating. Their vocal cord analogues produce sound by friction rather than air passage, generating a distinct buzzing accent when speaking Galactic Common. Limbs are long and tapered, ending in three or four phalanges. Ancestral zaddat had eight limbs; the inner four have become vestigial in modern individuals and are only present in fertile females, externally distinguishing them from infertile females and males, who are otherwise identical in appearance.

Atop the carapace is a fibrous layer of soft tissue called the symbiote, a distinct organism cultivated by the zaddat during the period known as the Darkening of Xohox. The symbiote is extremely photosensitive and responds to bright light by reproducing haphazardly at a rate fast enough to kill the host within minutes. Suppression of this reaction requires a functional Shroud at all times in lit environments.

Standard station and planetary atmospheres present two survival problems. First, the partial pressure of nitrogen is directly toxic to zaddat physiology. Gas mask veil equipment addresses this. Second, ambient pressure is too low for zaddat joints, which evolved under Xohox's high-pressure atmosphere. The Shroud addresses this. Zaddat uncomfortable at standard pressure begin experiencing joint strain and related symptoms; exposure below hazard thresholds without suit support is quickly life-threatening.

Blood is a yellow-orange color. Yellow blood of similar composition has been reported from other insectoid species but the mechanism in zaddat is specific to their biochemistry.

### Lifecycle

Zaddat hatch in clutches of approximately seven eggs as nonsapient polyps: simple gas-filled bags that float in the upper atmosphere of Xohox, photosynthesizing in the early phase of development. Over several months, polyps develop neurodes and functioning limbs as they cannibalize their own gas reservoirs and photocytes, descending eventually to the surface. The reproductive system forms last and is skipped if conditions are unfavorable, meaning the majority of zaddat are infertile. Fertility rates have declined further since the introduction of the symbiote, which adds energy and weight burden during the polyp stage.

Natural lifespan is 100 to 110 years. Modern life expectancy averages approximately 90 years due to the accumulated physiological cost of living continuously within a Shroud.

## The Shroud

The Shroud is a personal life-support system that handles atmospheric pressure regulation, nitrogen-atmosphere protection, food intake, waste disposal, and protection from contagion in the cramped colony environments where most zaddat live. Because most zaddat spend the majority of their lives inside a Shroud, including within habitat-ships designed for their physiology, the suit has acquired deep cultural significance. It is considered an extension of the face and standard professional garb.

Shrouds are limited in design variety, which has led to an extensive culture of personal modification through patches, pins, colorful cloth additions, and custom fittings. Fashion guild competition drives rapid shifts in what modifications are considered tasteful versus vulgar in any given period. Shroud customization is one of the primary channels of individual self-expression in zaddat culture.

## The Colonies

Rampant industrialization on Xohox rendered its atmosphere unbreathable and its surface increasingly hostile. As the capacity to maintain large populations on the surface collapsed, zaddat transitioned to large habitat-ships called Colonies: originally converted Hegemony bulk freighters, now purpose-designed zaddat vessels. Colonies are not independent; they rely on other species for construction of new ships and for significant repairs. Most of the zaddat population is now transient, living permanently aboard Colonies and dependent on maintaining positive relations with host species for continued survival.

This structural dependency shapes zaddat diplomatic posture: pragmatic, relationship-oriented, and oriented toward avoiding actions that might jeopardize the goodwill of host nations. The Spacer zaddat, the largest community operating in SolGov space, maintain careful interspecies relations while remaining formally aligned with the Moghes Hegemony.

## Guilds

The most important social relationship in zaddat culture is that of guild master and apprentice. Access to food, water, and life support was historically restricted to guild members, embedding the guild relationship into the core of zaddat survival instinct. Young zaddat pass through a five-to-six year general education under the Noble Guild before pledging to a guild master for a ten-to-fifteen year apprenticeship. Completing an apprenticeship grants full political and economic autonomy; the guild master is regarded as a surrogate parent and the guild as surrogate family. Being guild-less is regarded as a severe social failure; a guild-less zaddat in the modern era is likely to be expelled from their Colony.

Guild leadership is based on seniority and demonstrated competence. Individual Colonies are governed by the interaction of competing guilds through contracts for every major function, from sewage processing to child-rearing to diplomatic representation.

**Spacers' Guild:** Navigation, asteroid extraction, and interspecies diplomacy. Originally a split from the Engineers, the Spacers have become the primary face of the zaddat exile community to outside species and currently hold dominant political power within the fleet.

**Xozi Engineers' Guild:** The largest engineering guild and parent organization of the Spacers. Maintains the Colonies physically. Widely regarded as the skilled backbone of zaddat civilization; many Engineers privately view non-engineers as beneficiaries of their labor.

**Noble Guild:** Controls polyp production, child-rearing, and general education. Nearly all polyp-producing females belong to this guild. All zaddat pass through Noble Guild education before taking on a permanent guild, giving the Noble Guild cultural influence over the entire population. Guild leadership styles itself using noble titles including queen.

**Droning Guild:** Formed from Engineers and Spacers during early SolGov residence following exposure to drone technology unavailable on Xohox. Advocates for automated labor as a solution to zaddat workforce limitations.

**Art Houses:** Multiple smaller guilds concerned with fashion, performance, and craftsmanship. Considered the most prestigious, they train few apprentices and maintain reputations for exceptional quality. They position themselves above guild politics and often provide moderating voices during internal disputes.

## The Unity

Zaddat entered unathi space during a period when the Unity served as the unathi Hegemony's organizing religious and social framework. Over two centuries of integration, zaddat indigenous religious practices were replaced by a Unity variant incorporating several zaddat-specific concepts. The historical disaster of Xohox's industrial collapse and the near-extinction of the zaddat population has been interpreted within this religious framework as a divine consequence, with the unathi Hegemony's intervention cast as a guided salvation. This interpretation is widely held and actively encouraged by both Hegemony authorities and Spacer guild leadership.

The sun-worship component of Unathi religion, Uueoa-Esa, merged with zaddat superstitions around light sensitivity developed during the Dark Sky period when Xohox's atmosphere became opaque. Many zaddat view their photosensitivity as a divine consequence of the species' industrial overconsumption. The search for a replacement homeworld is conducted with the emotional register of a religious pilgrimage.

## Interspecies Relations

**Unathi:** The Moghes Hegemony has ruled the zaddat for over two centuries and most zaddat remain genuinely loyal to it. Unity-following unathi are regarded as social superiors in the zaddat hierarchy. Heretic unathi are viewed with complicated deference; Unbound unathi are regarded with deep fear.

**Humans:** SolGov is valued as a host nation and generally viewed as more welcoming than the skrell. Humans are regarded by most zaddat as generous but naive; the primary concern in human relations is reliable access to labor contracts and economic opportunity.

**Skrell:** Zaddat hold a cultural hatred of the skrell, who are known to terraform moderately-habitable worlds into skrell-preferred conditions, reducing the probability that any candidate homeworld will remain available for zaddat resettlement.

**Positronics and Synthetics:** Positronics are regarded as competitors for the same labor contracts zaddat depend on. Tensions are common.

**Diona:** Several diona choruses have expressed interest in assisting zaddat homeworld terraforming efforts and some have taken up residence within Colony life-support systems. These relationships are viewed positively by zaddat.

**Tajaran:** Some tajaran have proposed an economic alliance to reduce both species' dependence on Solar technology. Zaddat leadership is wary of arrangements that would limit the Colonies' mobility.

**Teshari:** Privately, the zaddat extend sympathy to the teshari as another species that has lost control of its homeworld. Practical cooperation has not developed due to the limited resources available to either group.

**Vox:** Comparing the zaddat to the Vox is considered a severe insult within zaddat culture. The comparison is widely made by outsiders, which does not improve matters.

## Language

**Vedahq** is the common language of the Spacer zaddat fleet, developed to maintain communications between Colonies. It consists of buzzing and clicking sounds that most other species cannot produce accurately. It is a crisp, utilitarian language built around efficient information transfer.

Station communications systems include Vedahq language support accessible by prefixing speech with `#z`.

Most zaddat who travel extensively also speak Sinta'Unathi, acquired through centuries of Hegemony integration. Zaddat speech in Galactic Common carries a characteristic buzzing accent, which modern Shroud designs with cybernetic larynx modifications reduce but do not entirely eliminate. Zaddat who take pride in their language skills tend toward precise, formal register in Galactic Common, which can read as old-fashioned or overly formal to other species.

## Medical and Operational Notes

Zaddat are physically fragile by galactic standards. Both brute and burn damage are taken at elevated rates (brute_mod=1.15, burn_mod=1.15). Toxin exposure is handled reasonably well by their biology. Flash and bright-light exposure to the symbiote causes rapid severe burn damage (flash_mod=2); a standard flash grenade constitutes a serious medical emergency. Do not expose zaddat patients to bright light during treatment.

Zaddat require elevated atmospheric pressure to function safely. Safe pressure for a zaddat is approximately 400kPa. Warning thresholds begin around 300kPa; hazardous decompression begins at 220kPa. Standard station atmosphere of 101kPa is immediately hazardous without a functioning Shroud. Medical personnel responding to a zaddat in distress should verify Shroud integrity before other interventions.

Zaddat are also sensitive to nitrogen atmospheric content; unfiltered nitrogen-atmosphere exposure is toxic and treated as a poison. Oxygen supply must be maintained even in otherwise survivable pressure conditions.

Zaddat spawn with a pressure-resistant Shroud suit and gas mask veil. Loss or damage to either item is a medical emergency. Spare Shroud components should be stocked in medical facilities serving zaddat crew.

No appendix is present. All other standard internal organs are present.
