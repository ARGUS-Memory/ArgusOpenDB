## Biology

Humans (*Homo sapiens*) are bilaterally symmetrical, sexually dimorphic, plantigrade bipeds. The standard complement of organ systems includes the nervous, cardiovascular, circulatory, digestive, endocrine, immune, integumentary, lymphatic, musculoskeletal, reproductive, respiratory, and urinary systems.

Average height is approximately 172 cm for adult males and 158 cm for adult females. Body mass typically falls between 54 and 76 kg. Humans lack prominent external body hair relative to related apes despite having a comparable follicle count; individual hairs are shorter, finer, and less pigmented.

Humans are among the most capable endurance runners in the galactic animal kingdom. Reduced body hair and a high density of sweat glands across the body surface support extended exertion without heat failure.

Reproduction is internal. Gestation lasts approximately 38 weeks. Infants are dependent for an extended period relative to most species, reaching sexual maturity between 12 and 15 years of age. Physical development continues until roughly age 18 to 21. The maximum documented lifespan with modern medical care is approximately 110 years under normal conditions. Cases approaching 150 years have been recorded in exceptional circumstances.

Humans are omnivorous with broad dietary tolerance, though they share the cross-species vulnerability to a short list of highly toxic compounds including cyanide and heavy metals.

## Demi-Humans

A subset of the human population has undergone **splicing**: a gene-modification procedure that introduces animal DNA into the human genome, producing individuals with additional traits such as enhanced strength, extended lifespan, altered metabolism, or cosmetic features including non-standard ear or eye morphology.

The procedure was developed in the late 21st century and rapidly banned by the United Earth Government. Splicers and their descendants continued to exist in scattered communities throughout the interim period. In 2501 CE, the United Federation established diplomatic contact with SolGov and lobbied successfully for repeal of the anti-splicing legislation. Splicing is now legal across most of SolGov space, though the cost of qualified professional procedures remains prohibitive for most individuals; unlicensed genetic engineering fills the gap in many jurisdictions.

The preferred modern term is **demi-human**. The word "splicer" carries xenophobic connotations in most contexts and is considered impolite. The most notable demi-human population center is within the United Federation itself.

## Behavior and Psychology

Human behavioral norms vary substantially across populations and cannot be reduced to a single profile. Xenophobia toward non-human species correlates inversely with early exposure and positively with economic precarity. Individuals raised in diverse environments tend toward accommodation; those with no prior contact with alien species default toward suspicion and insularity.

Technophobic and isolationist tendencies are a common outside characterization of humanity, though human society is also among the most technologically ambitious in known space. The internal tension between expansionist ambition and conservative reaction to the pace of that expansion is a persistent feature of contemporary human political discourse.

Humanity's major technological development cycles have historically been driven by military application. The USDF is regarded as the most powerful single military force in known space, compensating for a technology lag behind some older species through strategic depth and asymmetric warfare doctrine. Active armed conflict between human factions continues to the present day.

## Interspecies Relations

**Skrell:** Oldest contact and closest alliance. Free migration between human and skrellian space. Cultural interchange is extensive but not without friction; both sides periodically accuse the other of attempting to influence their natural development.

**Unathi:** Relations remain strained following the Human-Unathi Conflict. SolGov claims victory but regards Moghes as a genuine threat. Waves of xenophobic violence on both sides recur periodically despite official diplomatic efforts.

**Tajaran:** Humanity uplifted the tajaran into the FTL age, a fact that shapes the relationship on both sides. Human employers routinely exploit tajaran migrant labor; tajaran carry a mix of acknowledged debt and wounded pride toward their uplift sponsors.

**Teshari:** A negligible presence on the astropolitical landscape from humanity's perspective. Most humans accept the skrellian paternalistic framing of the teshari as fact without independent assessment.

**Dionaea:** Generally regarded as curiosities. Human and diona interests overlap minimally.

**Sergals:** After a difficult initial conflict, the post-war Dominion reconstruction program on Tal established a working relationship. Sergals and nevareans carry a relationship to humanity broadly comparable to that of the tajaran.

**Vox:** SolGov maintains an official policy of preemptive force against vox raiders. NanoTrasen has quietly adopted a more permissive labor policy in practice on frontier installations.

## Medical and Operational Notes

**Diet:** No species-wide dietary restriction. Standard nutritional protocols apply.

**Equipment compatibility:** Human-standard equipment is the baseline for most station-issued gear. Other species requiring accommodation are measured against human dimensions.

**Surgical approach:** Standard organ-system organization. Conventional diagnostic and surgical protocols are applicable without modification.

**Temperature and pressure:** Human-baseline tolerances are the reference point for station environmental systems.

**Demi-human variation:** Spliced individuals may carry additional traits not present in baseline humans. Detailed trait information is recorded on individual crew files where applicable. Medical staff should review crew records before assuming baseline physiology.
