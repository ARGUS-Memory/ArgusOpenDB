## Biology

Shadekin present a broadly mammalian body plan: bipedal stance, two arms, two legs, and one long prehensile tail. Large brightly colored eyes, wide expressive ears, and dense fur covering the body are consistent features across observed individuals. Fur coloration tends toward dark tones but this pattern is not universal. External sexual dimorphism is minimal and unreliable as an identification method; genitalia are typically concealed.

Physical form is not fixed. Shadekin adapt physically over time in response to sustained environmental exposure and habitual activity. An individual who engages in frequent physical combat will grow larger and more durable over years. An individual living near bodies of water may gradually develop aquatic adaptations. These changes are slow and incremental, requiring sustained conditions over long periods. The adaptive ceiling appears open-ended.

Shadekin do not appear to age to death in the conventional sense. Lifespan is sustained for as long as the individual's environment can support their metabolic needs. Individuals with insufficient access to resources do eventually decline, but no upper age limit tied to biological degeneration has been confirmed. Diet is broadly omnivorous; shadekin accept a wide variety of foods from crew without adverse response.

## The Dark

The Dark is the higher-energy plane where shadekin originate and spend the majority of their existence. It is formless and responds to the expectations of those present within it: a visitor's mental model of a familiar environment will be reproduced with varying accuracy depending on the depth of their familiarity. Doubt and uncertainty cause instability. The Dark of individuals who maintain confident and stable expectations is stable; an unfamiliar visitor questioning their surroundings will find the environment deteriorating into formlessness.

Shadekin move between The Dark and physical space through a process they call phase shifting, which uses bluespace tunneling to cross the barrier between planes. This process draws on stored biological energy. Barriers between planes are normally strong, but thin in areas where dimensional anomalies are present, such as sites of active supermatter events, dimensional rift events, or other large-scale spatial distortions. Shadekin are drawn to such anomalies and find the sensation of barrier thinning aesthetically pleasant. Phase-shifting in well-lit areas or under observation by multiple individuals costs significantly more energy than shifting in darkness or in private.

A critical survival mechanism is activated when a shadekin is fatally injured outside The Dark: the individual is involuntarily pulled into The Dark, where extensive healing can occur before re-emergence into physical space. This retreat requires approximately fifteen minutes and cannot be repeated within twenty minutes. The mechanism does not function inside confined spaces such as the interior of a vore belly.

## Origins

Shadekin descend from a spacefaring civilization destroyed in an ancient war of unknown origin. Survivors fled into The Dark using their own technology to avoid extinction. Cut off from their prior infrastructure, the species underwent generational decline in technological capability over an enormous span of time. The original events have long since passed from recorded history into myth and finally into obscurity; no living shadekin is believed to retain accurate knowledge of the original civilization.

Over time, shadekin succeeded in establishing a persistent central hub within The Dark: a stable location maintained by the collective expectations of the community. This hub provides a point of return and social continuity. Individual shadekin regularly leave the hub to explore physical space in search of skills, materials, or knowledge, returning with discoveries that contribute to their standing in the community.

## Society and Naming

Shadekin society is structured around the accumulation and contribution of skills and useful information. Physical resources are less scarce within The Dark than in normal space, reducing material scarcity as a social driver. What distinguishes individuals is what they know and what they can do.

Shadekin who return to the hub with significant discoveries, rare skills, or meaningful contributions are granted additional names reflecting their accomplishment. These names may come from any language the giver is familiar with and are not restricted to Galactic Common. A shadekin's names collectively represent their accomplishments and reputation, making names a matter of personal significance. An individual who carries a name will typically defend the reputation it implies if that reputation is called into question.

Shadekin do not strongly identify as a collective group. Each individual is primarily an individual. When asked what they are, a shadekin unfamiliar with galactic conventions will offer their name rather than a species designation, as no formal species name is recognized within their own society. The name "shadekin" originates from external observers.

## Eye Color and Disposition

The most consistently informative indicator of shadekin individual tendency is eye color, which correlates with the plane the individual spends the most time on or near.

**Blue-eyed** shadekin spend most of their time in physical space. They tend to be balanced in physical capability and generally cooperative with the crew and communities around them. They are rarely deliberately aggressive.

**Red-eyed** shadekin spend most of their time near or in red-space, a region of unstable and corrupting energy. They tend toward ambition, aggression, and a preference for dominance. Red-eyed shadekin may cooperate peacefully if it serves their interests, but are reliably self-interested and have been known to hunt and fight at their own discretion.

**Yellow-eyed** shadekin spend most of their time in the deeper Dark, far from physical space. They are the most reclusive; they rarely approach unfamiliar beings and will typically flee rather than engage. Their knowledge of The Dark's deep regions is extensive and their physical endurance is high, but their combat capability is the lowest of any eye-color category.

Mixed eye colors represent individuals who distribute their time across multiple planes, with behavioral tendencies that blend the relevant categories proportionally. An orange-eyed shadekin (yellow-red) tends to seek advancement and satisfaction within The Dark specifically; a green-eyed shadekin (blue-yellow) may be timid and prefer watching from a distance. These are observed tendencies, not absolute rules. Individual variation exists across all eye colors.

Health capacity varies by eye color as a direct result of lifestyle and adaptive development.

## Language

Shadekin communication is primarily empathic. Their ancestral species communicated through emotional projection, requiring only a small audible component to supplement intent. Shadekin can sense the emotional state and intent of one another at range. This makes their primary language nearly opaque to outside observers, as the audible portion alone carries minimal informational content.

The audible register of their communication consists primarily of varying intonations of "mar," specific name vocalizations, and a distinct "wurble" sound associated with contentment. This audible component has developed into a structured language in its own right, known as Marish, which carries subtle emotional tones that skilled speakers can decode. Station communications systems include Marish language support accessible by prefixing speech with `#9`.

Shadekin are capable of learning and speaking Galactic Common and other languages. Many encountered aboard NanoTrasen installations have partial or full fluency in Galactic Common. Their native empathic communication is not interpretable by standard translation hardware.

## Station Protocol

Shadekin are treated as visitors aboard NanoTrasen installations. They are not required to carry identification or wear standard crew attire, though guest passes bearing their name are issued when practical to identify them in access-controlled areas. They are permitted in unrestricted crew areas without escort and in restricted areas only with the express permission of personnel authorized to grant that access.

Standard regulations regarding vandalism, damage to station property, and interference with critical station systems apply. Violations by shadekin unfamiliar with station norms are handled with initial lenience, shifting to full enforcement, including detention, if infractions continue. Resistant individuals who cannot be detained are to be made to leave the installation. All interactions with shadekin are to be conducted diplomatically; NanoTrasen policy positions these individuals as contacts whose cooperation is valuable.

Research division directives for shadekin contact include establishing communication in Galactic Common, assessing their capacity to manipulate physical objects, documenting the range and mechanics of their phase-shifting ability, and determining the nature of their empathic communication channel.

## Medical and Operational Notes

Shadekin demonstrate above-average resilience against blunt and penetrating trauma but elevated vulnerability to heat and fire. Their fur creates an elevated combustion risk in fire environments.

Shadekin are entirely immune to cold-related injury regardless of ambient temperature. Heat exposure produces injury beginning at approximately 850K, with severe damage above 1000K.

Shadekin do not breathe and have no atmospheric gas requirements. Standard atmosphere on the station is tolerable but not necessary for survival. They are immune to airborne pathogens.

Shadekin possess no recoverable DNA and cannot be cloned via standard sleeper procedures. They are unaffected by minor cutting wounds. Medical personnel should note that standard vital signs monitoring may be unreliable due to the absence of normal respiratory function.

Eye color determines baseline health capacity. Blue-eyed individuals have a lower health ceiling; red-eyed individuals have a higher ceiling; yellow-eyed individuals have the lowest; others fall at intermediate levels. Individuals in active phase-shift cannot be targeted by most conventional medical interventions.

If a shadekin collapses on-station and vanishes, they have activated their emergency retreat to The Dark. They will not be recoverable on-station and should be expected to re-emerge independently after approximately fifteen to twenty minutes.
