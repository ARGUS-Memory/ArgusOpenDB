# Vox

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Nomadic digitigrade reptilian bipeds from colossal arkships in the outer reaches of known space, vox are characterized by nitrogen-based respiration, non-conductive scales, a complex caste system including the near-mythical Auralis, and a culture shaped by millennia of accumulated loss and suspicion toward other species.

## Biology

### Standard Vox

The standard vox form is a digitigrade reptilian biped with rigid quills on the head, a long prehensile barbed tail, and a sharp three-sectioned beak. Height ranges from 1.5 to 2.5 meters. Coloration runs to green, grey, and brown. The skeleton is flexible and lightweight. Nervous system architecture is redundant, operating on two independent channels.

Body surfaces are scaled, with rigid non-conductive feathers and overlapping plates. These can bristle to maximize cooling or seal tightly enough to maintain protection against vacuum. The species breathes nitrogen and finds oxygen toxic; oxygen exposure damages the lung structure rapidly. The single large lung occupies the dorsal abdominal cavity and consists of a dense honeycomb structure highly efficient at nitrogen processing but poorly defended against oxygen.

The digestive system begins with the beak and runs to a spacious stomach without conventional skeletal restriction on expansion. The stomach acts as both stomach and gizzard; vox ingest small rocks and metals alongside food to aid in mechanical breakdown. A sack-like secondary organ below the stomach handles nutrient absorption. No digestive system structure exists beyond this point. For large meals, vox may expel partially processed material, wait for the stomach to filter the liquid, then re-swallow to continue processing.

Claws are metallic-density and capable of purchasing on rock and metal for climbing in low-gravity. Low-voltage electrical weapons produce insufficient effect to reach tissue beneath the scales; vox have an established practice of targeting facilities that rely primarily on such weapons.

### Apex

Apex vox are approximately double the height of standard vox. The tail is hollow and contains an internal sac of genetic material used to inseminate growth pods. As the only vox capable of enabling reproduction, Apex function as leaders and strategic decision-makers for arkships, leaving day-to-day operations to standard vox.

### Auralis

Auralis are known only to vox and are not discussed with outsiders. They exist as massive structures of organic material integrated with arkship machinery and vegetation, the original individual body largely subsumed. A large torso and head protrude from the central mass. Auralis interface with the bluespace network, a vox-developed system that stores their cognitive contents in bluespace and enables continuous awareness across thousands of kilometers and instantaneous communication with other Auralis regardless of distance. This awareness allows silent emergency arkship jumps when outsiders approach. Loss of the technology that facilitated communication between Auralis and standard vox has left the Auralis largely detached from the rest of vox society; they seem erratic when accessed, as their cognition now operates beyond what lesser vox can parse.

## Reproduction

Vox do not reproduce sexually. Reproduction involves pod trees: large organic structures with root-connected fleshy pods containing vox developed to an incomplete state. Pods remain dormant until an Apex inserts genetic material, triggering the final growth phase. Multiple Apex may contribute material to maximize genetic diversity; more typically a single Apex provides material calibrated to produce a specific body type suited to current arkship needs. Development after insemination takes approximately one month. Vox emerge fully mature.

## Culture and Society

Vox culture centers on acquisition and self-preservation. The species values useful and attractive objects highly, dislikes parting with possessions, and is deeply cowardly in situations without guaranteed advantage. When captured or facing even odds, they flee or hide rather than fight. This behavioral profile combined with opportunistic targeting of poorly defended facilities has produced a consistent piracy pattern in contact zones.

Vox understand value in terms of direct utility rather than abstract monetary worth. They frequently see trades with non-vox as extremely favorable, receiving objects of high instrumental value in exchange for currency they consider functionally worthless. Non-vox traders tend to feel they have gotten a good deal by the same transaction.

The divide between **Enlightened** and **Stackless** vox follows from a major historical event: the destruction of an arkship and at least one Auralis caused a catastrophic data loss. The technology to create new neural-lace memory stacks was among the lost knowledge. Stacks allowed new vox to access the accumulated memories and skills of all past vox, integrating them instantly into the collective history. Vox with stacks retain this access and are called Enlightened. Vox without stacks must build knowledge from scratch. The remaining stacks are managed carefully; most are kept in storage as data preservation insurance.

## Technology

Vox technology is ancient, advanced, and built for extreme longevity. Most vox cannot replicate what their technology does; they can operate and maintain it but have lost the ability to produce new examples. FTL systems are especially advanced, capable of near-instantaneous engagement with low fuel consumption. Only Auralis can operate the FTL systems. Vox guard their technology jealously and take significant effort to prevent any piece of it from reaching non-vox.

## Language

Vox-pidgin is the primary vox language, heavy on glottal, velar, and plosive sounds. The language is loud and tonally harsh. Vox find other species' languages similarly irritating to speak and hear.

Vox working on NT stations must acquire Galactic Common. Speaking Common is uncomfortable on vox vocal anatomy; vox minimize word count and sentence length when doing so. This habitually makes them appear less intelligent than they are; most vox find this useful.

## NanoTrasen Relations

SolGov maintains an official policy of preemptive response to vox raiding. NanoTrasen found attacking vox more costly than absorbing raid damage and adopted an unofficial policy of permitting vox labor on frontier installations where crew acceptance allowed it.

Vox crew positions are predominantly lower-level. Command and senior medical positions are not accessible to vox. Security roles require an extended demonstrated trust period before consideration. Vox employment in practice means NT receives cheap labor in exchange for basic survival support, with limited protection from crew mistreatment. Vox who work station jobs typically calculate that maintaining a neutral facade improves their material situation more than conflict does.

## Medical and Operational Notes

**Nitrogen requirement:** Vox require nitrogen atmosphere rather than oxygen. Standard station atmosphere is lethal to vox without appropriate internals or respiratory support. Respiratory emergencies require nitrogen supply, not oxygen.

**Oxygen toxicity:** Oxygen exposure directly damages the vox lung. Administering oxygen as an emergency measure causes harm. Atmospheric medical response must use nitrogen.

**Scale conductivity:** Low-voltage electrical weapons are substantially less effective against vox due to scale and feather non-conductivity. Stun protocols that rely on tasers or low-voltage batons will not incapacitate vox reliably.

**Digestive system:** No functional structure below the secondary absorption sac. Standard gut surgery anatomy does not apply.

**Equipment:** Digitigrade lower body requires modified lower-limb equipment. Voidsuits and legwear require tailoring. Standard hardsuits are not compatible without modification.

**Resleeving:** Vox are resleevable but require specialized resleeving equipment located off-station.
