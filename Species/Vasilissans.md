# Vasilissans

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Arachnoid bipeds from the mineral-rich jungle world of Varilak, vasilissans are cold-blooded, chitin-armored, and distinguished by four dorsal metalimbs, spinnerette silk production, and a matriarchal culture organized around gem-focused religious practice.

## Biology

Vasilissan skin is a thick chitin of variable color, iridescence, and texture depending on regional origin, ranging from bumpy and rigid to smooth and dense.

The face is largely flat and smooth, lacking a nose. Four eyes protrude outward rather than sitting recessed, providing a flush profile. Two mandibles extend from the sides of the mouth; the mouth itself contains multiple fanged teeth, a long bulbous tongue, and spinnerettes capable of producing silk strands on demand.

Cranial filaments function as modified sensory structures but closely resemble hair in casual observation. Filament color does not typically match the individual's chitin coloration.

Four metalimbs extend from the back. These normally face rearward but can extend forward to assist with tasks, including climbing vertical surfaces, stabilizing the body, subduing prey, or fine manipulation. Individuals with high metalimb dexterity can use them for precision work.

Males average 183 cm in height. Females are larger, averaging 213 cm, with a range of 183 to 244 cm for females and 183 to 213 cm for males. Females retain softer facial features and wider hips; males show rougher features and more slender secondary limbs. Vasilissans are non-mammalian; females do not have mammary structures.

The species lifespan is 120 to 130 years, with some individuals exceeding 150. Adulthood begins at 18.

## Reproduction

Vasilissans reproduce sexually. After mating, partners typically separate. Long-term bonding and formal union exist, influenced by exposure to human and other galactic customs, but are not the cultural default.

Female vasilissans possess a spermatheca that stores sperm after mating for later use. Eggs are laid via an ovipositor into a warm-blooded surrogate, which carries and eventually lays them after a gestation period of several weeks. Hatchlings emerge independently after laying. The surrogate role is sometimes filled by a third partner of a warm-blooded species, which in communities with human cultural influence has become a recognized social arrangement.

---

## Diet

Vasilissans are almost entirely carnivorous, though some individuals incorporate small supplemental amounts of plant matter. The digestive system can accommodate large whole meals. Standard food consumption behavior in public settings conforms to expected norms; exceptional intake volume is occasionally observed but not common in professional environments.

## Behavior and Psychology

Vasilissans are characteristically reclusive. Extended social interaction with individuals outside the personal circle is fatiguing for most. Unsolicited contact with personal space or possessions from non-vasilissans is poorly received. Females tend to seek and enjoy attention more actively than males; this difference contributes to the social structure in which female preference shapes group dynamics.

Vasilissans do not apply their personal-space norms consistently across species. Standards that apply to inter-vasilissan interaction may not extend to other species.

The species reports finding interactions with dionaea and prometheans less difficult than with most others.

## Culture and History

Pre-contact vasilissan society organized into small groups and queen-led settlements. Queens directed expansion both outward and upward, motivating the construction of extremely tall silk-and-gem structures. The tallest recorded structure on Varilak reached approximately 300 meters before collapsing. Structures are constructed with silk as the primary binding element, reinforced with gems and metals found in the planet's unusually mineral-dense crust.

Currency was an abstract concept in traditional communities; resource allocation operated through queen orders and inter-settlement trade. NanoTrasen contact was motivated by interest in Varilak's exceptional density of precious metals and gems, as well as vasilissan architectural and weaving capability.

Gem-religion is the central vasilissan spiritual framework. Gems are understood as conduits between the mortal plane and an energy plane that contains all past life. The size and quality of a gem determines the quality of transit it can provide. Varilak's gems are considered insufficient to carry vasilissan souls; the belief that stars are incomparably large gems of ultimate spiritual power originally motivated the upward architectural ambition of the queen-led cities.

## Homeworld

**Varilak** is a temperate jungle world with dense forest cover on most landmass, approximately 60% ocean surface, and an unusually high atmospheric oxygen content that supports large insectoid, reptilian, and amphibian megafauna. The deeper ocean is largely unexplored. The planetary crust contains gem and precious metal deposits at densities substantially above galactic norms, which current theory attributes to an unidentified anomalous event.

## Interspecies Relations

**Humanity:** The most frequent vasilissan contact, primarily through NanoTrasen corporate operations. Interaction is transactional rather than culturally deep.

**Skrell:** Contact occurs mainly through research and scientific contracting. The relationship is professional.

**Unathi:** Cold-blooded species are considered poor surrogate incubators.

**Prometheans:** An ongoing debate among vasilissans as to whether prometheans contain usable meat. All parties have confirmed that prometheans taste edible.

## Medical and Operational Notes

**Temperature extremes:** Vasilissans are cold-blooded. High heat will cause the chitin to begin failing before systemic failure from dehydration. Cold exposure produces a progressive shutdown: initial memory disruption and headaches, escalating to coma at or below 260 Kelvin. Unlike heat damage, cold-induced coma is reversible by removing the individual from the cold environment. Limb curling occurs in both thermal extremes.

**Cold coma threshold:** 260 Kelvin (approximately -13 Celsius). Environmental monitoring should flag any drop below this threshold in areas with vasilissan crew.

**Burn vulnerability:** Vasilissans take elevated damage from burns relative to most species.

**Glass and foot injury:** The chitin on vasilissan foot pads is resistant to glass puncture. Standard barefoot glass injury protocols do not apply.

**Metalimbs:** The four back metalimbs are functional tools and self-maintained structures. They are not prosthetics and do not require medical attachment or support systems. Physical trauma to the metalimbs should be assessed as limb injury, not equipment damage.
