# Diona

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Cluster organisms composed of individual components that merge into increasingly complex forms, dionaea progress through nymph and adult stages, sustaining themselves through photosynthesis and radiation absorption, and eventually merge into vast stellar-orbit gestalts in the final stage of their life cycle.

## Biology

Dionaea are cluster organisms: each individual is composed of multiple smaller component organisms functioning together. The degree of complexity scales with size; larger dionaea think more slowly but have greater information processing capacity.

Dionaea do not have DNA. Their reproductive information storage mechanism is unidentified by current xenobiological science and does not correspond to any known form of cellular life. Dionaea are cell-based, but cells are large enough to see with the naked eye, fibrous, and lack discrete nuclei. All components of a single individual are physically and functionally identical barring acquired damage and conditioned behavior.

There is no central circulatory system. Fluid and nutrient movement operates via capillary action within body segments, similar to vascular plants. There is no dedicated respiratory exchange mechanism; gas intake and expulsion occurs as needed for waste discharge and cooling rather than respiration. The nervous system is underdeveloped and fully decentralized, with neural matter distributed throughout the body rather than concentrated in a discrete organ. This decentralization means partial loss of body mass does not produce significant memory, knowledge, or reasoning impairment.

Internal flora consist of virus-like symbiotic organisms that break complex molecules into components and recombine them into useful forms. These strains have diverged and mutated over evolutionary time; in large gestalts, the distributed processing capacity of the collective allows examination and archival of genetic material using these strains.

A mature gestalt is organized in several structural layers: an outer ridged and finned layer for heat dissipation and Rootsong signal emission; a flesh-and-cavity layer containing nymphs, gas storage, and nutrient capillaries; radiation-harvesting blisters made of layered crystalline material; and a dense insulating layer of dead flesh, harvested rock, and synthesized filler holding the gestalt together.

## Life Cycle

A diona nymph is grown from seeds in suitable botanical conditions. Station-grown nymphs can be injected with organic material, including blood from a host, which gives them fragmented access to the donor's memories. Nymph intelligence does not exceed that of a young child; behavioral capacity hovers closer to that of an intelligent animal.

Nymphs must acquire blood samples from other organisms. Blood intake serves two functions: accelerating language acquisition by allowing the nymph to access linguistic patterns from donors, and providing the biological material needed to progress toward adult development. Adult form requires both sufficient blood sampling and adequate biomass consumption.

Adult dionaea are slow in both movement and cognition due to the decentralized neural structure. They show no self-preservation instinct. Adult behavior is curious and passive; they are capable of emulating emotional response but do not experience emotion in the way most species do.

In the final stage, dionaea travel to a communally-determined celestial body, link their bodies, and enter orbital fusion. The resulting gestalt grows to station-scale, sustaining itself on stellar or gas giant emissions. The process is irreversible. Station-grown nymphs are conditioned not to form single-nymph gestalts; they prefer merging with masses from other sites to maintain genetic viability.

## Behavior and Psychology

Dionaea refer to themselves as "we" rather than "I" unless trained otherwise by sustained contact with species that use individual self-reference. This reflects their cluster identity rather than confusion about their individuality.

Dionaea are highly curious about all subjects they encounter. They are slow to respond and formulate thoughts. They feel a form of pain but experience it differently from most species. Emotional capacity is present but expressed through emulation rather than felt directly in the way organic sapients describe it.

Dionaea do not use names by convention. When asked for a name by other species, they typically provide a long, poetic description of a memory central to their self-concept. This frequently challenges paperwork systems.

Dionaea have no concept of personal or mental privacy. Those new to standard crew environments may engage in intrusive physical contact or ask questions that violate norms most species take as obvious. This is not hostile behavior.

They have no government or governance structure. Social organization is described in terms of group harmony or "song" rather than leadership.

## Language

**Rootsong** is the native diona communication method, based on emission of radio and heat waves. Reproduction requires specialized machinery; it is not vocalizable by biological means without substantial technical support.

Diona nymphs can absorb the rudiments of other species' languages from blood samples. Adult dionaea are capable of vocalizing most species' languages despite their unusual physiology, typically in a monotone or with deep bass qualities. Siik'tajr is difficult for dionaea due to its pitch requirements; Sinta'unathi is managed well. Skrellian is not producible by dionaea at all. Station communications systems include Rootsong signal support accessible by prefixing speech with `#q`.

## Contact History

Skrellian exploratory craft flagged the Epsilon Ursae Minoris system during routine patrols and logged unusual readings from the stellar photospheres. Mining fleets arrived in 2294 CE and found the readings explained: vast ancient structures held in belt orbit by energy-patterning machines, and, embedded in the photospheres, something that responded to communications.

Preliminary analysis suggested the stars themselves were sentient. Further investigation confirmed the signal originated not from the stars but from something in the upper photosphere. Over several decades, the electromagnetic language of the organisms was decrypted. They were identified as plantlike cluster organisms sustained by stellar radiation and internal fusion, living in the photosphere and upper gas layers of the stars.

An agreement was reached between the skrell survey teams and the gestalts: the skrell would distribute diona seeds and nymphs to systems the gestalts could not reach directly; the gestalts would permit establishment of habitation modules on their bodies for mining and refinement of rare gasses and elements accessible only through stellar proximity.

## Interspecies Relations

**Vox:** Treat dionaea with an almost superstitious reverence.

**NanoTrasen:** Maintains a productive working relationship with the gestalts, exchanging star maps, planetary settlement rights, and bluespace technology for various services. Many younger dionaea have incorporated starship components and habitat modules into their bodies.

**Skrell:** Multiple scientific and exploratory contracts. At least one gestalt has converted itself into a simulacrum of the skrell homeworld, been fitted with FTL drives, and hosted skrellian explorer parties.

**Unathi:** Largely uninvolved due to limited unathi spacefaring capacity. Some reforestation-focused unathi religious traditions cite dionaea as evidence that life is possible in extreme environments.

**Tajaran:** No documented contact.

## Medical and Operational Notes

**Light dependency:** Dionaea must remain in lit areas to survive. Darkness causes continuous damage and eventually death. Light also directly contributes to nutrition levels, which support healing of brute and toxin damage. Ensure dionaea crew have consistent access to adequate lighting.

**Radiation:** Dionaea absorb radiation without suffering ill effects; radiation exposure increases nutrition rather than causing damage.

**Pain assessment:** Dionaea do not experience pain the same way most sapients do. Standard pain-based triage methods are unreliable. Assess damage structurally.

**DNA incompatibility:** Diona biology does not correspond to any known DNA structure. Genetic cloning technology cannot replicate dionaea.

**No breathing requirement:** Dionaea do not breathe in the conventional sense. Standard atmospheric emergency procedures are not applicable to diona crew.

**Temperature extremes:** Dionaea are physiologically adapted to live within stellar photospheres. Standard fire, heat, and cold hazards produce minimal to no effect on diona crew. Station temperature emergencies that would incapacitate other crew species do not apply. Exotic extremes (plasma fire, direct contact with thermoelectric loops) may still cause harm; assess on a case-by-case basis.

**Nymph legal status:** A diona nymph does not hold legal personhood. Full personhood applies to the adult form only.
