# Alraune

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Plant-derived omnivorous humanoids native to the high-pressure tropical world of Roanoke, the alraune are patient ambush predators and capable linguistic adapters who integrated gradually into galactic society following prolonged first-contact conflict with human settlers.

## Biology

Alraune bodies contain a network of hypha-like tendrils running the full length of the body. These tendrils serve dual purpose as both structural tissue and a distributed neural network, giving alraune heightened environmental awareness. Lower body segments may have reduced tendrils or protective filaments to shield the neural network.

Alraune skin is organized in three layers. The outermost is a thin, slightly waxy layer that prevents water loss. The middle is a porous layer enabling respiration. The innermost is the mesophyll, responsible for photosynthesis and the source of the characteristic green coloration. Deeper tissue is pale due to reduced light exposure.

Alraune possess two or more sensory organs capable of standard humanoid-range vision with elevated sensitivity to blue light. Eye-like structures vary in appearance from simple spots to iris-like formations to complex patterned structures; they are typically bright red. A vomeronasal organ processes airborne chemical signals. Hearing is accomplished through patches of stiff trichome fibers on the body surface, with some individuals developing ear-like structures. Two balance organs are located along the back of the neck. Alraune have no anatomical difficulty speaking Galactic Common.

Internal organs are few and relatively simple. The digestive system is a series of leaf-lined chambers that break down ingested material through pitcher-plant-type chemistry, storing processed energy as glucose and starch. Peripheral storage structures can take the form of berries, cereals, or other plant-adjacent edible matter. Removal of these peripheral structures is nearly painless; alraune occasionally harvest and consume them to recover stored nutrients.

## Body Variation

Lower body morphology varies significantly between individuals and correlates with native biome. Three primary forms exist: a large bud covered in large petals with prehensile vines for movement, a set of human-like legs, and winding tentacle or tail structures. Mountainous biomes produce a higher proportion of legged alraune; flat biomes produce bud-form alraune more frequently; aquatic and forested environments favor tentacled variants.

Upper body morphology above the waist has converged toward human-like forms following extended contact with human colonists, typically presenting in feminine proportion and taking on vivid coloration. External growths including flowers, fruits, and cereal structures vary between individuals based on genetics and individual choice, generally favoring colors that would stand out against the individual's native biome to attract prey.

The distributed neural network that runs through the body makes alraune somewhat slower and less balanced on their feet than humans.

## Diet

Alraune are omnivorous. Photosynthesis, especially under blue-spectrum light, is a supplement to diet rather than a primary source of energy. Exclusive reliance on photosynthesis produces torpor, cognitive degradation, and eventual metabolic failure. Active nutritional intake is required.

Dietary needs include carbohydrates, proteins not synthesizable from plant matter, and large quantities of electrolytes. The primary electrolyte source is high-sodium intake, typically from salt or salt lick compounds.

Water intake is critical and is absorbed most efficiently through root structures. Regular water immersion is highly beneficial.

Some alraune grow storage sections with hallucinogenic properties as a prey-luring mechanism. Alraune-produced edibles should be assessed before consumption by non-alraune crew.

Alraune evolved on a planet with approximately 2.7 atmospheres pressure and elevated CO2. The reduced atmospheric pressure and lower CO2 levels on standard space stations require dietary supplementation. Carbonated beverages are a common practical source of supplemental CO2. High-concentration CO2 exposure remains dangerous and can cause carbonic acid buildup.

## Reproduction

Alraune are fully hermaphroditic. Reproductive structures are typically internal and protected by modified leaf structures. "Female" seed structures are borne on motile tendrils and take the form of a softball-sized rind with a narrow central opening; coloration ranges from pale white-green to near-black aubergine. "Male" compartments are small motile pore structures that produce pollen.

After fertilization, the seed requires two to four days in moist conditions for the rind opening to close and harden. The fertilized seed is then viable for planting or cryogenic preservation. In root-accessible environments, the rind develops over several months into a structure the size of a large watermelon before spontaneously rupturing. Newly emerged alraune sever the root tendril attaching them to the rind; in communal settings this severing is a ritual act.

Early development requires external energy: parasitization of nearby plant root networks, intensive hydroponic support, decomposing organic matter, or a living host.

Stationary older alraune occasionally produce clones from removed branches. These clones do not retain the original's memories but typically share personality traits and disposition.

## Behavior and Psychology

Cognitive speed in alraune is somewhat slower than human-baseline for response and thought formulation. This is a result of the decentralized neuro-stroma, which has low regional specialization. The same architecture provides resilience: partial loss of the neural network does not produce significant memory, knowledge, or reasoning loss.

Typical alraune are even-tempered, patient, and slow to anger. They prefer precise and time-consuming work and are prone to focus narrowly on a task to the exclusion of their surroundings. Alraune are social and capable of empathy with most lifeforms, but frequently operate from a pragmatic moral framework that other species find unusual.

Alraune recognize themselves as individuals, unlike dionae, enabling more straightforward integration into crew structures.

Alraune lifespan reaches up to 800 years. In later life they gradually transition toward semi-dormancy, eventually becoming stationary. Stationary alraune continue to communicate with other alraune via extended branch and vine structures and historically served as communication relays between communities.

## History

The alraune evolved from carnivorous plant ancestors on Roanoke, developing intelligence as a hunting adaptation to pursue mobile prey. Hunter-gatherer communal organization centered on protecting and supplying stationary alraune.

First contact occurred in 2343 CE when a small settler group arrived on Roanoke. The colony went dark within months following reports of hostile wildlife. A second and better-equipped colonial wave arrived in 2365 CE, settling in the high mountains to avoid the low-altitude high-pressure environment. This delayed contact until the colony was established, converting initial ambushes into a large-scale conflict.

Alraune adapted rapidly during the conflict, using captured equipment and knowledge to organize beyond commune-scale for the first time. Both sides sustained heavy losses. SolGov intervened, and a ceasefire was negotiated in 2371 CE. Terms required release of all captives and mutual territorial withdrawal.

Following the ceasefire, some colonies established mutualistic relationships with nearby alraune communities. Roanoke itself continues to maintain only limited contact with galactic society. Spacefaring alraune have become increasingly present on frontier stations and are generally accepted as workers, though they carry a reputation for pragmatic and occasionally deceptive conduct.

## Homeworld

**Roanoke** is a large planet orbiting a main-sequence B-type star with high UV and blue-light output. Atmospheric composition is primarily nitrogen, CO2, and oxygen at approximately 2.7 atmospheres pressure. Surface temperature is above Earth-baseline due to proximity to the inner edge of the star's habitable zone. 89% of the surface is water or tropical marshland. Landmass is approximately half tropical rainforest and half semi-arid and temperate zones. A small southern polar cap exists.

Standard station atmosphere is not survivable on Roanoke's surface without voidsuits or internals due to pressure and CO2 concentration. Animal life on Roanoke includes megafauna, large insect species, and small lizard and mammal populations.

## Culture

Alraune names in Galactic Common are typically drawn from plant species, including Earth-native plants and species from other worlds. Surnames are culturally unfamiliar to alraune; individuals who adopt them typically choose a plant name or a concept of personal significance.

Alraune social philosophy varies between individuals. A significant portion of spacefaring alraune regard constructs such as morality, freedom, and governance as externally imposed distractions from their core operating principle of self-interest. These alraune form non-hierarchical associations called **garlands**: groups of self-interested individuals pursuing a common goal cooperatively without formal leadership.

Alraune understanding of property is grounded in the ability to exert force over an object or individual. Human legal frameworks for ownership are not intuitively understood by all alraune. Some alraune extend this framework to other beings, regarding those who accept their resources or direction as belonging to them.

## Medical and Operational Notes

**Atmospheric CO2:** Standard station air is CO2-deficient for alraune. Carbonated drinks provide accessible supplementation. Monitor for symptoms of CO2 deficiency on extended assignments. Avoid deliberate CO2 enrichment of shared atmospheric zones.

**Water:** Regular water immersion through root structures is required. Alraune should have access to a water source. Soil-contact accommodation significantly improves wellbeing.

**Peripheral growths:** Edible-appearing growths on an alraune's body may carry hallucinogenic compounds. All alraune-produced food items should be treated as potentially pharmacologically active and assessed before consumption by non-alraune crew.

**Distributed nervous system:** Minor trauma to peripheral body tissue produces little or no pain response. Standard pain assessment methods are unreliable. Damage should be assessed structurally rather than by pain report.

**Predatory behavior:** Alraune are ambush predators by evolutionary origin. Most spacefaring alraune have integrated socially and pose no concern. Behavioral changes toward crew, particularly targeting or luring behavior, should be reported to security for assessment.

**Language:** Alraune native language is verbal and visual-signal based and is not reproducible by most other species. Alraune speak Galactic Common without anatomical difficulty. Station communications support is not required.
