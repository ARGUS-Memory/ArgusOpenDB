# Skrell

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Amphibious humanoids from the humid world of Qerr'balak, the skrell are humanity's oldest alien ally and currently the most technologically advanced species in known space, with a rigidly caste-organized society centered on collective order and intellectual development.

## Biology

Skrell appear gelatinous but are opaque, with moist pliant skin covered in a thin mucus layer. Skin color varies widely and includes black, red, green, blue, pink, purple, orange, and teal. The face lacks a nose; two narrow slits occupy the position where nasal structures would be found on a human. Skrellian eyes are entirely black, making emotion difficult to read by expression alone. Vision underwater is substantially better than human-baseline, and low-light vision is slightly superior to human-baseline. Hands and feet are five-fingered and five-toed with slight webbing.

Each skrell carries three large **head tails** extending from the head. These are limp, unmuscled appendages with two functions: egg-incubation in traditional contexts, and ornamentation. Head tail length varies by caste. Loss or damage to a head tail is culturally treated as disfigurement and is typically concealed or surgically repaired.

Skrellian skin requires regular water contact. Exposure to dry environments for more than approximately twelve hours causes significant discomfort. Skrell assigned to stations in human space compensate through frequent showers or purpose-modified sleeping arrangements, commonly a shallow pool of water with fine sediment.

The skrellian brain is structurally analogous to an evolved amphibian brain and is compatible with standard NanoTrasen hardware, including MMI systems and neural interfaces.

Average height is 5'10" to 6'0" for males and 6'0" to 6'2" for females. Raskinta-Katish skrell are typically taller than average; Kanin-Katish are typically shorter.

## Reproduction and Family

Skrell reproduce via external fertilization. They have no external reproductive organs. Nudity taboos are minimal among skrell, who find the emphasis many other species place on bodily covering culturally unusual.

A typical clutch averages fifty eggs, most of which hatch into tadpoles. Immune system vulnerability in the tadpole stage means fewer than five per clutch typically reach maturity. Tadpoles are not classified as children under skrellian law, though deliberate destruction of a clutch is considered socially objectionable.

Fertility rates are low by human standards and are further reduced by population density. Skrell in core worlds commonly adopt rather than reproduce; colonists in lower-density environments experience better fertility outcomes.

Skrell form co-parenting bonds called **Kala-Qua** pairings. The Kala-Qua remain together through a child's primary developmental period and typically maintain lifelong friendship afterward.

Skrellian family structure uses a monolateral lineage system. Key roles:

| Term | Role |
|---|---|
| **Qarr-Maqa** | Primary parent; main caretaker and tutor; lineage holder |
| **Qrri-Maqa** | Secondary parent; material and emotional support; comparable to a close aunt or uncle |
| **Qarr-Baqa** | Clutchmates; closer than human siblings due to shared early development |
| **Qrri-Baqa** | Children of a parent's other clutch; relationship varies widely |
| **Maqa'lak** | Clutchmates of a parent; often involved in child-rearing |

Pairings are not required to be of different sexes. Cross-caste pairings produce Ue-Katish offspring, which carries significant social consequences.

## Lifespan and Development

Skrell reach adulthood at 20 years. Natural lifespan is approximately 100 years. With technological enhancement, individuals may reach approximately 135 years. Raskinta-Katish have a shorter average lifespan than other castes due to the physical demands of military service.

Skrellian education is structured: Early Enlightenment covers common knowledge through the young adult stage. Focused Enlightenment introduces caste-specific education at age 8. Ongoing Enlightenment, which begins at 15, determines career trajectory. Exit at 18 produces a **Qrri-Mog**, qualified for basic labor. Exit at 25 produces a **Qerr-Mog**, the standard credentialed professional. Only Qerr-Mog may teach or aspire to advisory roles.

## Diet

Skrell are functionally herbivorous. The preferred staple food is fungi. Most plant matter found in known space is palatable and many plants toxic or hallucinogenic to humans or omnivores are skrellian delicacies due to differences in digestive chemistry.

Sulfur-rich animal protein is toxic to unmodified skrell. Dietary augmentation to handle meat exists but is performed only for environments where plant matter is scarce. Most augmented skrell still find meat unpalatable.

Alcohol is absorbed by the skrellian brain approximately five times faster than by the human brain. A standard serving of weak alcohol is sufficient to cause visible intoxication in a skrell. Most adult skrell actively avoid alcohol.

## Caste System

The skrellian caste system has been in place for millennia and has shaped partial genetic speciation between castes. Cross-caste breeding produces reduced fertility and Ue-Katish offspring. The five primary castes and two non-castes are as follows.

### Primary Castes

**Qerr-Katish** (High-Caste): Rulers, managers, diplomats, and lawyers. The only caste permitted to hold the role of Qerr-Skria. Culturally conservative and socially sophisticated. Families are small. Skin tones are typically bright green. Head tails are famously long, reaching near the waist.

**Malish-Katish** (Caste of the Mind): Scientists, educators, and writers. The caste most frequently encountered by non-skrell and commonly regarded as the archetypical skrell by outsiders. Skin tones are typically pale green, sky blue, or pale yellow. Head tails are average length.

**Kanin-Katish** (Caste of the Builder): Manual laborers and craftsmen. Family solidarity and number of descendants are primary measures of social success within the caste. Skin tones are typically red, orange, dark orange, black, or pale yellow. Head tails are average length.

**Talum-Katish** (Caste of the Artist): Artists, artisans, aesthetes, and propagandists. Talum work incorporates psychological principles designed to increase resonance with the target audience. Skin tones are typically purple, blue, pink, orange, red, or white. Head tail length is intermediate; ornamentation of the head tails is expected.

**Raskinta-Katish** (Caste of Warriors): The military and security caste. Strongest and most physically capable skrell. The most culturally conservative caste, with a reputation for xenophobia within skrellian society. Skin tones are typically green, blue, black, brown, or yellow. Head tails are extremely short; head tail loss in combat is not considered disfiguring.

### Non-Castes

**Ue-Katish** (Casteless): Offspring of cross-caste pairings or two Ue-Katish. The Ue-Katish have no formal place in skrellian society and face widespread discrimination. Many are sterile or carry other genetic abnormalities. Common paths out of the caste include passing as a parent caste, emigrating to human space, or operating outside the law.

**Kiquol** (Guests): Non-skrell sapients living in skrellian space. Kiquol typically organize in pseudo-caste communities aligned with the caste whose function matches their species' typical strengths. Whether synthetics are classified as Kiquol or Ue-Katish varies by city-state.

Caste honorifics use the first syllable of the caste name in place of standard honorifics. "Comrade" is the accepted equivalent for non-skrell and is the polite form of address for Ue-Katish.

## Culture and Politics

Skrellian society is organized around a network of city-states, each governed by a Qerr-Katish constitutional monarch. The monarch holds executive authority and is advised by the **Qerr-Koal**, senior representatives of each non-Qerr caste. The legislature is drawn from the educational system; the franchise is not universal.

Skrellian culture places deep psychological value on order, hierarchy, and correct role fulfillment. Deviation from caste expectations causes genuine discomfort to most skrell. A counter-culture influenced by human democratic ideals exists primarily among skrell living in or near human space.

Gender was effectively abolished as a social category by the skrell before humanity invented the wheel. Clothing styles exist with mild gendered associations but are treated as suggestions rather than norms. Skrellian individuals find human gender customs genuinely difficult to understand.

Skrellian media characterizes the species as peaceful scientists committed to galactic harmony. This framing is managed by the Qerr-Katish ruling class, who maintain significant technological and memetic advantages over other species and have not shared them openly with humanity.

The **Qerr-Glia** are the dominant organized criminal faction in skrellian space: descended from deposed Qerr-Katish royals, operating across the full range of organized crime from money laundering to the slave trade. **Ue-Katish pirate flotillas** operate along the edges of skrellian space and at the human-skrell border, functioning as black markets and hosting information brokers and mercenaries.

## Interspecies Relations

**Humanity:** The closest interspecies relationship in the galaxy, formed through centuries of co-evolution and mutual interference in each other's politics. The relationship is productive but frequently toxic.

**Unathi:** Cold relations. The unathi remember skrellian intervention in the Sol-Hegemony War as decisive to their defeat. Stable but not warm.

**Tajaran:** Relations are generally functional at the personal level. Tajaran political culture finds skrellian foreign policy condescending.

**Teshari:** Effectively skrellian vassals. Teshari fill lower-status roles in skrellian systems and face consistent discrimination. Skrellian attitudes toward teshari are broadly condescending.

**Akula:** Another skrellian vassal, treated considerably better than the teshari. Some skrellian city-states formally recognize and occasionally protect akula sovereignty. Akula who fight in defense of skrellian interests are occasionally granted honorary Raskinta-Katish status, a practice that remains controversial.

**Dionae:** Widely perceived as a skrellian vassal species by outside observers. Skrell acknowledge that the close relationship is maintained by convenience rather than formal alliance.

## Language

Skrellian spoken language is a series of hums, warbles, and croaks operating across frequency ranges inaudible to unathi, tajaran, and most humans without computer assistance. Communication assistance hardware provides translation aboard NanoTrasen installations. Station communications systems include skrellian language support accessible by prefixing speech with `#k`.

Skrellian names are typically either transliterations from their native language or adopted human names. Transliterations are characterized by heavy use of Q, K, and vowels.

Skrellian written language uses logograms rather than an alphabet, with thousands of distinct signs. The emotional register of a text is encoded in how individual logograms are rendered -- sharp or faded strokes, elongated or abbreviated forms -- making accurate translation depend on visual reproduction as well as semantic content. A simplified machine-interface script exists for computer interaction but is considered aesthetically inferior by skrell.

Skrell speaking non-skrellian languages adopt the accent of whoever taught them, often with characteristic pitch variation. They are sensitive to minor tonal shifts in speech and may register as flat in casual conversation with other species.

## Medical and Operational Notes

**Meat allergy:** Animal protein containing sulfur-rich complexes is toxic to skrell. Standard NanoTrasen catering should not include meat in skrellian rations without explicit crew request.

**Alcohol:** Skrellian alcohol metabolism is approximately five times faster than human-baseline. Standard medical dosages for alcohol-based reagents should be adjusted accordingly. Even small quantities of alcoholic compounds produce strong effects.

**Hydration:** Skrell require daily water contact. Extended dry conditions cause increasing distress. Medical staff should monitor hydration status during prolonged treatment or confinement.

**Respiration:** Skrell are capable of breathing underwater and may prefer water-submerged rest conditions.

**Head tails:** Head tail tissue is sensitive and culturally significant. Damage should be addressed as soon as practical. Skrellian crew may decline medical examination of head tail structures by non-skrellian personnel.

**Hardsuit compatibility:** Standard hardsuit helmets are not designed for skrellian head morphology. Skrell requiring hardsuit protection must use skrell-compatible helmet designs. Non-compatible helmets cannot be worn.
