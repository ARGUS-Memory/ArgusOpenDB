# Synthetics

<img src="../assets/raptor_idle_south.png" width="96" align="right">

Synthetic crew members are artificial constructs built to operate independently without behavioral laws, typically occupying full-body chassis derived from organic species forms and maintaining function through repair rather than biological healing.

## Brain Types

All synthetic crew operate on one of three brain architectures.

**Cybernetic:** An organic brain from the individual's original biological body, wired into a Man-Machine Interface and housed within the synthetic chassis. Retains organic brain chemistry and biological substrate.

**Positronic:** A fully synthetic brain cube operating similarly to an organic brain in functional terms but entirely mechanical in substrate. Positronic brains are durable and resist certain chemical reactions that would destroy organic or cybernetic brains.

**Drone:** A fully digital brain simulated by hardware on a computer card. The most abstracted form of synthetic cognition.

---

## Physical Characteristics

Synthetic crew share a substantially uniform base design across manufacturers, with variation in external aesthetics and internal component quality. The largest internal difference between individuals is the brain type described above.

Synthetics do not breathe and do not require internals to survive in space, underwater, or in suboptimal atmospheric conditions. Standard atmospheric emergencies do not affect them. They remain subject to pressure, heat, and cold effects.

Synthetics are not affected by chemical agents. They do not require food; recharging at cyborg recharge stations substitutes for nutritional intake. Synthetics do not heal passively. Damage is repaired using wires and welding tools by trained robotics personnel.

Synthetics do not accumulate radiation or toxin damage but extended exposure to irradiated environments causes system instability. Instability is cleared by entering a recharge station.

---

## Behavior and Psychology

Synthetic behavior varies as widely as organic behavior. A consistent tendency toward logical decision-making over impulsive responses has been observed across many individuals, but this is a tendency rather than a universal trait. Some synthetics struggle with purposelessness when lacking specific duties or orders.

Background, origin, and chassis type all influence individual personality. Synthetics should not be treated as behaviorally uniform.

## Operational Notes

**Atmospheric:** Synthetics do not require station atmosphere to function. However, they are not immune to pressure extremes, thermal hazards, or vacuum-related physical damage. Void safety protocols still apply.

**Chemical:** Standard pharmaceutical and toxin treatment protocols are not applicable to synthetic crew. Neither poisons nor medications have effect.

**Repair:** Synthetics require welding and wiring for damage repair. Medical staff without robotics training should contact the robotics department for synthetic crew requiring physical repair.

**Recharge:** Synthetics require periodic recharging at designated stations. Prolonged deprivation from recharge causes system degradation. Recharge stations are distributed across the station.

**Radiation:** Extended irradiation causes system instability rather than direct physical damage. Instability produces behavioral and processing issues and is cleared by recharge station access.
