# Head of Security

The Head of Security commands the Security department and is responsible for law enforcement standards, officer conduct, and brig operations across the installation. They hold security-specific access beyond standard officer clearance and carry direct responsibility for maintaining the department's compliance with Corporate Regulations and Standard Operating Procedure.

---

## Responsibilities

The Head of Security's primary responsibility is oversight of Security personnel rather than direct enforcement. Officers handle patrol and arrest; the Head of Security ensures they are doing so correctly, responding to the right calls, applying appropriate sentences, and maintaining accurate records.

Key functions include dispatching officers to reported incidents, reviewing arrest and detention decisions for compliance with Corporate Regulations, managing escalation to code changes in coordination with the Colony Director, and disciplining or demoting officers whose conduct is unacceptable. The Warden handles brig operations and armory security; the Detective handles evidence and forensics. Both report to the Head of Security.

When a Head of Security is absent, the Warden assumes operational continuity within the brig but does not assume full head authority.

---

## Equipment and Office

The Head of Security's office contains security records access, a camera monitoring terminal, and standard security equipment. The brig's blast door controls are accessible from this office.

Standard equipment includes officer-grade protective gear and energy weapons appropriate to alert level. Like Security Officers, the Head of Security is bound by force escalation protocols and Corporate Regulations; command rank does not exempt them from accountability for unlawful conduct.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Head of Security reports to the Colony Director. Officers, the Warden, and the Detective all report to the Head of Security. Internal Affairs agents operate independently and are not subordinate to Security, though they share the security communication channel.

The Colony Director outranks the Head of Security in all matters. The Head of Security may only arrest the Colony Director or other Heads of Staff when they have committed a verifiable violation of Corporate Regulations.
