# Detective

The Detective is the Security department's forensic specialist. Their primary function is evidence collection, crime scene analysis, and identification of perpetrators through physical evidence. They are not a general patrol officer.

---

## Responsibilities

When a crime is reported and physical evidence may be present, the Detective responds to the scene, scans surfaces for fingerprints and biological material, collects evidence using evidence bags, and coordinates with Medical for autopsies on fatalities. Gloves must be worn at all times during active evidence work; contaminating a scene with the Detective's own prints compromises the entire investigation.

Back at the Detective's office, evidence and prints are loaded into the High-Resolution Forensic Scanning Computer, which consolidates partial prints and cross-references them against crew records. A complete or partial print match, combined with fiber analysis from clothing, builds the evidentiary dossier. This dossier serves as the formal evidence base for arrest, questioning, and any subsequent tribunal.

The Detective sets suspects to arrest in the security records system and informs Security via the security channel so officers can act. Reports should be communicated through Security, not over the general station channel, to avoid alerting suspects.

In the absence of active investigations, the Detective assists Security through camera monitoring, record updates, and patrol support. They report to the Head of Security.

---

## Equipment

The Detective's office contains a High-Resolution Forensic Scanning Computer, a camera terminal, and investigation supplies. Personal equipment includes a forensic scanner, detective's gloves, a security headset, a tape recorder, a hand labeler, a camera, and sunglasses. A Colt M1911 pistol may be carried with a permit from the Head of Security.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Detective reports to the Head of Security. They are firmly under Security command despite the independent nature of their investigations.
