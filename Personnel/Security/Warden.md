# Warden

The Warden manages the brig and armory. Their primary duties are overseeing the welfare and lawful treatment of prisoners, managing armory inventory and distribution, and coordinating Security Officer deployment from a fixed position within the brig.

---

## Responsibilities

The Warden is the brig's watchdog. They ensure that prisoners are held for appropriate durations under applicable Corporate Regulations, that sentences are not abusive or unlawfully extended, and that detainees are not mistreated. The Warden has authority to release prisoners being held unlawfully.

The Warden manages the Security armory. All armory withdrawals require documented authorization from the Head of Security or, in specific circumstances, the Colony Director. A running log of armory contents and withdrawals should be maintained throughout the shift. The Warden is responsible for ensuring equipment balance in the armory and for flagging any supply deficiencies to Central Command.

Between active prisoner management, the Warden coordinates Security Officers over the radio, monitors brig and station cameras for security events, and maintains accurate security records. The Warden should not routinely leave the brig; their role is to ensure that brig operations function without requiring the Head of Security's direct involvement.

Matters involving Sol Government offenses require the Warden to fax Sol Central with relevant paperwork. Charges that include holding until transfer are not fully processed unless SolGov is notified.

---

## Access

The Warden holds full brig access and armory access. No other personnel except the Head of Security and Colony Director are authorized in the armory without documented permission.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Warden reports to the Head of Security and assumes operational continuity of the brig when the Head of Security is unavailable. The Warden does not assume full head authority in the Head of Security's absence.
