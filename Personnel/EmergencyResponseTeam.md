# Emergency Response Team

Emergency Response Teams (ERTs) are Central Command rapid-response units deployed when a station's situation has escalated beyond the capacity of on-board Security and engineering. They operate with Central Command authority and are equipped above standard station-issue levels.

---

## Deployment

ERT deployment is authorized by Central Command in response to crisis situations that on-board personnel cannot resolve. Unlike Asset Protection, ERTs are the first escalation response rather than last resort. Typical deployment scenarios include significant hostile incursions, large-scale medical emergencies, major structural failures, and situations where command authority has been compromised.

ERT members assemble at the Central Command special operations area, receive mission briefing from the ERT Commander, and deploy to the affected station via shuttle.

---

## Specializations

ERT members are organized into specializations. Each carries a specialized hardsuit with role-appropriate integrated modules, plus additional gear for their discipline.

**ERT Commander** leads the team. Their hardsuit includes a datajack, mounted jetpack, and IIS module. The Commander coordinates the mission and holds authority over all ERT personnel during deployment.

**Security Response** carries the most advanced personal weapons available to NanoTrasen field teams. The Security hardsuit includes a mounted energy gun, mounted grenade launcher, mounted jetpack, and IIS module. Additional equipment includes webbing, night vision, handcuffs, stun baton, and a choice of firearms appropriate to the mission.

**Engineering Response** is equipped for rapid structural repair and infrastructure restoration. The Engineering hardsuit carries an IIS module, mounted jetpack, plasmacutter, and mounted RCD. Standard field load includes a full toolbelt, insulated gloves, optical meson scanner, wire, metal, glass, plasteel, and spare powercells.

**Medical Response** carries advanced medical equipment for mass-casualty stabilization. The Medical hardsuit includes an IIS module, mounted jetpack, inbuilt chemical dispenser, and health analyzer. Medical personnel supplement their hardsuit with medkits, surgical tools, and pharmaceutical reserves.

---

## Authority and Conduct

ERT members operate under the authority of their ERT Commander and by extension Central Command. On arrival at the assigned station, the ERT works toward their mission objectives as briefed. Station command personnel should be treated with professional courtesy; the ERT is not a replacement for station leadership but an augmentation to it.

For Asset Protection deployments, which represent a more extreme escalation level, see the separate Asset Protection record.
