# Quartermaster

The Quartermaster manages the station's supply chain. They oversee incoming and outgoing cargo, control the requisition process, maintain supply point balances, and direct Cargo Technician activity. Their position requires balancing departmental requests against available resources while keeping a verifiable record of all orders.

---

## Responsibilities

**Supply points** are the station's ordering currency. Each crate costs a set amount of points drawn from the shift-start pool. Points are replenished by returning empty crates on the supply shuttle (5 points per crate), including stamped manifests in returned crates (2 additional points per manifest), and sending refined materials back to Central Command (points proportional to material value). Managing the point balance is an ongoing duty throughout the shift.

**Requisition processing** follows a documented chain. When a department requests a crate, the standard procedure is to have the requester fill out a requisition form, obtain a stamp from an appropriate head of staff, and file the stamped form before placing the order. This creates an accountable paper trail; the Quartermaster's desk clipboard is used to collect and retain all forms. When the requested item is not in stock, the Autolathe may be able to produce it; if not, the requesting department must fill out both a requisition form and a crate order. Items available in the Cargo Bay inventory can be disbursed directly on documented request.

**Proactive ordering** is appropriate when station events make certain supply categories clearly necessary. The Quartermaster exercises judgment in anticipating departmental needs and ordering accordingly without requiring every request to come through formal channels.

**Disposals mailing** provides an alternative delivery channel when the pneumatic pipe network is intact. Items are wrapped with package wrapper, tagged with the destination tagger, and introduced to the disposals conveyor or a disposals unit. Large parcels are dragged directly into disposals units. Gift wrapping is available for small items but requires manual delivery.

**MULE bot deployment** handles heavy or bulky deliveries. A crate is loaded onto the MULE, a destination is set via PDA or the control panel, and the bot dispatches. Power cells are maintained via the maintenance panel on the MULE's exterior. The Cargo Technician typically handles MULE operation; the Quartermaster coordinates routing.

---

## Equipment

The Cargo Bay contains the supply shuttle console, the Autolathe, a MULE bot, empty crates, package wrapper, a destination tagger, a copy machine, and a cell charger. The Quartermaster's desk holds a clipboard for requisition records. The Autolathe produces a range of tools and components from raw materials without requiring a crate order.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Quartermaster heads the Cargo department and reports to the Colony Director. Cargo Technicians and Shaft Miners report to the Quartermaster.
