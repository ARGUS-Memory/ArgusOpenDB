# Station Engineer

Station Engineers are the primary technical workforce of the Engineering department. They are responsible for power generation, hull maintenance, construction, and response to infrastructure damage.

---

## Responsibilities

Core responsibilities at shift start are engine startup and solar array wiring. The supermatter engine is the station's primary power source; its startup procedure must be followed precisely. Solars provide supplementary or backup power and are typically assigned to one engineer while others work on the engine. Substations should be configured to prevent cascading power failures if the primary source becomes unstable.

Hull integrity monitoring is continuous. When a breach is reported, the Station Engineer responds with appropriate materials and sealing equipment, coordinates with Atmospheric Technicians on repressurization, and reports status to the Chief Engineer. Station damage can also result from internal events; engineers are expected to assess and repair any structural failure regardless of cause.

Power management via the Power Monitoring Computer allows remote APC control. During low-power situations, the engineer may be directed to shut down non-critical APC zones to preserve power for vital systems.

Shields, where present, protect the station from external hazards. Setting up and maintaining shield generators is an engineering responsibility.

---

## Equipment

Station Engineers start with a hardhat with headlamp, engineer's toolbelt (screwdriver, wrench, crowbar, welder, wire coil, multitool, T-ray scanner), an engineering headset, and an engineering PDA with atmospheric scanner.

Recommended additional gear includes insulated gloves, welding goggles or helmet, optical meson scanners, a Rapid Construction Device, a Rapid Piping Device, extra cable coils, a dufflebag, and a hazard vest (which allows carrying an emergency oxygen tank in the suit slot). Metal and glass for on-the-spot repairs should be kept accessible. All gear is available from the Engineering Tool Room autolathe or lockers; additional materials can be requisitioned from Cargo.

---

## Reporting Structure

Station Engineers report to the Chief Engineer. In the Chief Engineer's absence, the most senior engineer on shift assumes coordination responsibility.
