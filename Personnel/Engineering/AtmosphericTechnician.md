# Atmospheric Technician

Atmospheric Technicians maintain the station's life support systems. They manage gas distribution, pressure regulation, atmospheric filtration, and emergency response to fires, breaches, and toxic gas events.

---

## Responsibilities

The Atmospheric Technician operates out of Atmospherics, the central hub for the station's pipe networks, gas canisters, environmental controls, and monitoring equipment. Primary duties include ensuring all station sections maintain breathable, correctly pressurized atmosphere, and responding to events that compromise that standard.

**Repressurization** is the most common task. After a hull breach is sealed by engineering, the affected area must be refilled with breathable atmosphere. Air alarms and local vents handle this automatically over time; the Atmospheric Technician provides manual intervention via air canisters and portable air pumps when a critical area needs to be made habitable quickly.

**Filtering and gas removal** involves scrubbing toxic gases from atmosphere. Air alarm filter settings can be adjusted to target specific contaminants. Phoron gas in particular must be removed carefully; ignition sources must be eliminated before any phoron spill is addressed, and burning off phoron should only be attempted by experienced personnel as a last resort.

**Fire response** requires the technician to suit up and seal the affected area before using the air alarm's Panic Siphon or Replace Air function to suppress the fire. Areas that have been burning are lethally hot and must not be opened without first cooling them or establishing a containment barrier. The Fire Axe stored in the Atmospherics computer room is the technician's emergency tool, functioning as both a crowbar for powerless doors and a weapon of last resort; it should not be carried routinely.

---

## Equipment

Standard equipment includes an engineering headset, an engineering PDA with atmospheric scanner, and engineering tools. For fire and breach response, an atmospheric hardsuit or voidsuit with internals is required. Air canisters and portable air pumps are available in Atmospherics.

---

## Reporting Structure

Atmospheric Technicians report to the Chief Engineer and coordinate closely with Station Engineers on breach and pressure events.
