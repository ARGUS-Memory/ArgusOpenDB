# Central Command Officer

Central Command Officers are deployed directly by Central Command to NanoTrasen installations for specific administrative purposes. They hold the highest rank on any station they board and are outranked only by other CentCom personnel.

---

## Authority

A Central Command Officer outranks the Colony Director, Emergency Response Teams, and all departmental personnel. Their presence supersedes normal command hierarchy for the duration of their assignment. This authority is accompanied by a strict Non-Interference Policy: the officer is expected to pursue only the stated purpose of their visit and to avoid intervening in ongoing station operations unless those operations directly pertain to that purpose.

Unauthorized intervention shifts responsibility for outcomes from the station's command structure to the CentCom Officer, reduces accountability for those who should have managed the situation, and impedes the normal functioning of the chain of command. Officers who override station operations without cause undermine the purpose of deployment.

---

## Deployment Purposes

Central Command Officers are deployed for defined purposes, including: prisoner transfer to USDF custody, VIP escort and reception, crew instruction seminars, safety inspections, head-of-staff disciplinary proceedings (up to and including termination of employment), oversight of scheduled executions, and mediating disputes with foreign diplomatic representatives.

This role is not a general observer position. CentCom Officers have a mission objective and are expected to accomplish it with minimal disruption to normal operations.

---

## Equipment

A Central Command Officer carries a CentCom ID with full company-wide access, a CentCom headset with access to all company channels including ERT frequencies, a CentCom PDA, and appropriate dress uniform. Weapons for personal protection may be carried at the officer's discretion.

---

## Communication with Central Command

All personnel may contact Central Command by fax at any time. Command-tier personnel have access to an emergency transponder in the command console for live contact. The CentCom Officer is not the only channel for CentCom communication; station heads are expected to maintain direct fax contact with CentCom for official matters whether or not a CentCom Officer is present.
