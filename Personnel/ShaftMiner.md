# Shaft Miner

Shaft Miners extract raw ore from the asteroid for processing and distribution to station departments. They operate outside the main station in the asteroid caverns, communicating with Cargo and Science over the supply channel.

---

## Responsibilities

**Ore extraction** begins with suiting up. A mining voidsuit with a large oxygen tank in suit storage and active internals is required before cycling out the airlock. The standard tool is a handheld drill used directly on ore-bearing asteroid tiles; ore is collected into a mining satchel and transferred to a dragged ore box. For metallic hydrogen deposits, a fixed mining drill is required. Mining charges, when available from Science, provide the most efficient bulk extraction method.

**Ore processing** takes place at the smelter and processing machines near the airlock conveyor. Raw ore is dragged to the conveyor, fed through the smelt cycle, and stacked into refined sheets. Stacks hold up to fifty units. Processed materials are transferred to Cargo Technicians for distribution or delivered directly to requesting departments.

**Material distribution** follows departmental priorities. Science requests silver, gold, phoron, uranium, diamond, plasteel, and osmium for research advancement; five to ten sheets of each is a useful baseline delivery, with diamonds prioritized in higher volumes. Robotics needs metal, plasteel, diamond, uranium, silver, and gold for mech fabrication. Engineering requires metal and glass for structural and power work. Medical uses phoron for pharmaceutical synthesis. Departmental coordination via the supply channel ensures materials reach the right recipients.

Shaft Miners communicate with the Quartermaster and Cargo Technicians over the supply channel. A backup implant is strongly advised; the asteroid caverns are hazardous, and isolation from station rescue resources is a real possibility.

---

## Equipment

Standard equipment includes a mining voidsuit with internals, a handheld drill, a mining satchel, and an ore box. A belt miner crate from Cargo provides additional mining tools and supplies. More advanced tools, including the kinetic accelerator and resonator, are available through the mining equipment vendor. The Guide to Mining documents all tool types, ore yields, and alloy production procedures.

---

## Reporting Structure

Shaft Miners report to the Quartermaster and operate under the Cargo department hierarchy.
