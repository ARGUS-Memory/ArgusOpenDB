# Medical Doctor

Medical Doctors are the primary treatment staff of Medbay. Their responsibilities span diagnosis, wound treatment, surgery, and resleeving. They carry the authority of the Medical Oath and operate under confidentiality obligations that extend to all patient records.

---

## Responsibilities

The Medical Doctor diagnoses and treats the full range of injuries a crew member may sustain: brute trauma, burns, toxin exposure, suffocation, organ damage, and brain injury. A health analyzer provides basic damage readings; the body scanner in the Emergency Treatment Center yields deeper diagnostic information, including internal bleeding locations and organ status.

**Brute damage** encompasses cuts, bruising, and physical trauma. Minor brute damage responds to advanced trauma kits. Cuts that cause bleeding must be treated first to prevent blood loss. At high severity, Bicaridine is required. Internal bleeding does not respond to surface treatment and must be located via body scanner and repaired surgically, or treated with Myelamine.

**Burn damage** results from heat, extreme cold, electrical discharge, and fire. Advanced burn kits address mild burns and reduce infection risk. More severe burns require Kelotane or, where a Chemist is available, KeloDerm or Dermaline. Infection at burn sites must be monitored; untreated infections progress and become life-threatening.

**Toxin damage** follows poisoning, phoron contamination, radiation, critical blood loss, liver failure, or infection. Treatment begins by removing the source: sleeper dialysis for poisons and overdoses, full decontamination for phoron exposure, Hyronalin or Arithrazine for radiation. Dylovene is the baseline toxin treatment and does not carry meaningful overdose risk at therapeutic doses.

**Suffocation damage** results from oxygen deprivation, damaged lungs or heart, or critical blood loss. Inaprovaline stabilizes a suffocating patient and prevents deterioration. Dexalin or Dexalin Plus actively treats the damage. CPR provides emergency oxygenation when medications are unavailable.

**Blood loss** occurs when cuts, burns, internal injuries, or organ damage cause sustained bleeding. Symptoms include dizziness, fainting, pallor, oxygenation loss, and eventually brain damage. Stopping the source of bleeding is the first priority; IV drip with O-negative addresses critical blood volume.

**Organ damage** beyond a minor threshold requires Peridaxon or targeted medications such as Imidazoline for eyes and Alkysine for brain damage. Surgery bypasses medication limits for severe organ injuries. Brain damage above a critical threshold causes immediate death; patients with fatal brain damage require resleeving rather than on-site resuscitation.

**Medical states** follow a graduated severity model. Shock begins when sufficient damage accumulates, causing impaired movement and eventual collapse; treating the underlying damage resolves it. Soft critical occurs below zero health and sustains shock until the patient recovers. Hard critical occurs at negative fifty percent health: the patient falls unconscious, stops breathing, and takes suffocation damage. Inaprovaline halts deterioration; cryo tubes provide stable holding while treatment continues.

**Surgery** is performed in Operating Theatre 1 or 2, both equipped with full surgical instrument sets. OR1 supports two simultaneous operators on one patient. Surgery addresses internal bleeding, fractures, and high-severity organ damage. The body scanner identifies the location and type of injury requiring surgical intervention.

**Cryogenics** heals surface damage across all types provided tubes contain Cryoxadone or Clonexadone and are cooled to between 20K and 80K. The freezer controls are in Secondary Storage.

**Resleeving** is the station's primary death recovery mechanism. When a death alarm sounds or a request arrives, the Medical Doctor checks the resleeving console for a valid mind record. If the body is intact without a mind scan, a SleeveMate brain scan provides an alternative. A new sleeve is grown in the vat, then placed in cryo to resolve clone damage. The mind record is then sleeved into the body and the patient is briefed honestly on their situation. Patient records may indicate specific instructions for how to handle that briefing.

## Policies

**Medical confidentiality** applies to all patient records and treatment history. No individual outside medbay staff, including command, has the right to access patient information without the patient's own consent or a direct order from the Chief Medical Officer.

**The right to refuse treatment** is held by any patient who is fully conscious and capable of informed decision-making. Patients who are intoxicated or incapacitated may not invoke this right. A patient who refuses treatment exercises a recognized right; the Medical Doctor is not liable for outcomes that follow.

**The Medical Oath** establishes the professional standard for all Medbay personnel: honest and diligent treatment, patient advocacy in informed decision-making, non-discrimination, and continued learning.

---

## Equipment

Standard loadout includes a health analyzer and advanced medical kit. The Emergency Treatment Center stocks sleepers, a body scanner, cryo tubes, a defibrillator, roller beds, and IV drip equipment. Operating Theatres carry full surgical instrument sets, anesthetics, and patient monitoring consoles. The Resleeving Lab contains the console, growing vat, cryogenics tube, and resleeving unit. Chemistry supplies pharmaceuticals to Primary Storage via the SmartFridge connection. Medical belts, stasis bags, and additional advanced kits are available from Primary and Secondary Storage.

---

## Reporting Structure

Medical Doctors report to the Chief Medical Officer. Alternate role titles available to the role include Surgeon, Nurse, Emergency Physician, and Virologist.
