# Chief Medical Officer

The Chief Medical Officer leads the Medical department and is responsible for all patient care, staff assignments, pharmaceutical supply, outbreak response, and medical privacy compliance across the installation. They hold the highest medical authority on the station and are the final decision-maker for matters of medical ethics and patient treatment.

---

## Responsibilities

The CMO coordinates all Medical personnel: Medical Doctors, Paramedics, Chemists, Virologists, and the Psychologist. At shift start, the CMO should review available staff and assign roles. Recommended coverage includes at least one Emergency Physician for field response, a front-desk triage position, and a surgeon or cryo-capable doctor for critical cases. Chemistry should be directed to produce essential medicines immediately: clonexadone, hyronalin, tricordrazine, dexalin plus, spaceacillin, and ryetalyn are priority compounds.

The CMO is responsible for ensuring all crew are using suit sensors at appropriate alert levels. Genetic backup scans and death alarms should be offered to all personnel, particularly command staff, early in the shift.

Medical privacy is the CMO's primary legal obligation to the crew. Patient records, treatment details, and suit sensor data may not be shared with Security without the patient's consent except at Code Blue or above with a valid warrant. The CMO has authority to bar unauthorized personnel from Medical on privacy grounds. Consent to refuse treatment is a right; crew may decline treatment provided they understand the consequences. Mental incapacity to refuse treatment may only be invoked when the patient demonstrates clear evidence of disorientation or panic affecting their judgment.

During outbreaks, the CMO coordinates quarantine, directs the Virologist and Chemist, and has authority to detain infected personnel. The biohazard suit in the CMO's office is for direct outbreak response.

The CMO Transcore Data Packet, located in the CMO's locker, must be inserted into the resleever monitor and protected during evacuation. This packet contains the shift's resleeving documentation.

---

## Equipment

The CMO carries a Hypospray Mark II, a fast-injection device loaded with a 30-unit vial. It is not for indiscriminate use. Tricordrazine and inaprovaline are standard loads.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Chief Medical Officer reports to the Colony Director. All Medical department personnel report to the CMO. The CMO coordinates with other Heads of Staff on matters involving injured crew, outbreak risk, or decisions affecting station-wide welfare.
