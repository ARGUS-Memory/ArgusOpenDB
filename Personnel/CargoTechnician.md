# Cargo Technician

Cargo Technicians operate the station's Cargo Bay under the Quartermaster's direction. Their responsibilities center on processing incoming shipments, packaging and routing outbound deliveries, and maintaining the MULE bot and Autolathe.

---

## Responsibilities

**Cargo Bay operations** involve receiving crates from the supply shuttle, distributing their contents to the appropriate departments, and returning empty crates to accumulate supply points. Crates sent back with stamped manifests inside generate additional point bonuses. The Autolathe in the Cargo Bay produces tools and components from raw materials on demand, reducing dependency on ordered crates for common items.

**Disposals mailing** is the primary delivery method when the pneumatic pipe network is operational. Items are wrapped with package wrapper, tagged with the destination tagger set to the correct destination, and placed on the conveyor or into a disposals unit. Large parcels are dragged directly into disposals units. For small items, gift wrapping provides a presentational option but requires manual delivery since gift-wrapped parcels cannot accept destination tags.

**MULE bot operation** handles crate and heavy-item delivery. A crate is loaded onto the MULE, the destination is set via PDA or the onboard control panel, and the MULE dispatches. When the power cell is depleted, the maintenance panel on the exterior of the MULE is opened with a screwdriver (with controls unlocked), the cell is removed from the top slot, a charged cell is inserted, and the panel is closed.

Cargo Technicians support the Quartermaster in processing requisition forms, verifying orders before release, and maintaining the organizational records that give the department its accountability. When the Quartermaster is unavailable, senior Cargo Technicians coordinate department activity.

---

## Equipment

The Cargo Bay contains a MULE bot, an Autolathe, a copy machine, a cell charger, package wrapper, a destination tagger, a hand labeler, a multitool, and a basic medical kit.

---

## Reporting Structure

Cargo Technicians report to the Quartermaster.
