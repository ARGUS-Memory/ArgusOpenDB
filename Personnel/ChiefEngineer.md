# Chief Engineer

The Chief Engineer heads the Engineering department and is responsible for all power generation, atmospherics, structural maintenance, and construction operations on the installation. This includes direct oversight of Station Engineers, Atmospheric Technicians, and the supermatter engine.

---

## Responsibilities

The Chief Engineer's primary responsibility is ensuring the station maintains continuous power and breathable atmosphere. This requires working knowledge of SMES configuration, solar array wiring, supermatter engine operation, and basic atmospherics.

At shift start, the Chief Engineer should coordinate the department: assign personnel to engine startup and solar wiring, establish radio contact with the Atmospheric Technician team, and confirm who is responsible for each critical system. Proactive communication at the start of a shift significantly improves departmental response throughout.

Ongoing responsibilities include monitoring fuel levels in the supermatter's radiation collectors, supervising the engine during off-nominal operation, coordinating hull breach response with on-call engineers, and acting as the liaison to other departments for infrastructure needs. When Station Engineers require guidance on complex construction or repair, the Chief Engineer provides it.

The Chief Engineer has the authority to authorize autolathe access for engineering support tasks and may coordinate with Security to arrange camera coverage of damaged areas under investigation.

---

## Access and Equipment

The Chief Engineer holds full engineering and maintenance access. Standard equipment includes engineering-grade protection, tools appropriate for construction and electrical work, and access to the AI maintenance terminal located in the Chief Engineer's office, which can repair a damaged AI on an intelicard.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Chief Engineer reports to the Colony Director. Station Engineers and Atmospheric Technicians report to the Chief Engineer. Engineering communicates on the department channel and coordinates with other heads through the command channel.
