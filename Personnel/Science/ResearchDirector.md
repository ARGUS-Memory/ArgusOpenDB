# Research Director

The Research Director leads the Science department and is responsible for research operations, laboratory safety, anomalous phenomena assessment, and AI system maintenance. While the Science department is not essential to basic station operations, it contributes substantially to the capabilities of other departments and introduces the most concentrated source of uncontrolled hazard on any installation.

---

## Responsibilities

The Research Director's core responsibility is ensuring that experimental work is conducted safely. Toxins and Xenobiology are the highest-risk areas and require active monitoring. Containment failures in either area can directly threaten the rest of the station; the Research Director is expected to have sufficient working knowledge of these disciplines to recognize when something has gone wrong.

Research priorities include materials and technology that benefit other departments: upgraded fabricator stock, machine components, and advanced equipment. The Research Director coordinates with the Quartermaster to ensure incoming materials are routed to science, and with department heads to understand what improvements would be most useful.

The Research Director's office contains two critical maintenance tools: an AI repair terminal for restoring damaged AI systems loaded on intelicard, and an AI Core circuit board for constructing a secondary AI unit if required. Newly constructed AI units default to standard NanoTrasen law sets.

When anomalies, unknown substances, or unexplained phenomena appear on the station, the Research Director is the primary resource for identification and response guidance.

---

## Access

The Research Director holds access to all scientific areas including xenobiology, toxins, robotics, and the particle accelerator bay.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Research Director reports to the Colony Director. Scientists, Roboticists, and other Science personnel report to the Research Director. The department communicates on the science channel.
