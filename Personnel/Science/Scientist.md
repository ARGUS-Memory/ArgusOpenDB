# Scientist

Scientists conduct research across four laboratory disciplines: Research and Development, Toxins Research, Xenoarcheology, and Xenobiology. Each lab operates largely independently, and a Scientist may focus on one or distribute their time across several depending on station needs and staffing levels.

---

## Responsibilities

**Research and Development** is the station's primary technology advancement lab. Scientists collect items from around the station, including hand teleporters, stun batons, and analyzers, and use them to advance research technology levels. Higher tech levels unlock fabrication schematics for advanced devices: improved weapons, batteries, drills, and circuit boards. Mineral input from Shaft Miners is required to advance research beyond early tiers; coordination with Cargo on ore delivery is a recurring responsibility. The R&D console syncs fabrication servers and allows circuit boards to be produced for Robotics and Engineering.

**Toxins Research** investigates the properties of phoron gas, primarily through controlled explosive experiments conducted in the Toxins mixing chamber and testing range. Bomb construction follows a strict procedure using the mixing chamber: the chamber is evacuated, phoron canisters and a trace oxygen mix are introduced, ignited, and the heated product is collected at or above 200 degrees Celsius. Testing is conducted in the dedicated range, not in any occupied station area. Remote signaling devices must use unique frequencies to prevent accidental detonation. See the Toxins guide for full mixing and assembly procedures.

**Xenoarcheology** operates from the research shuttle and the asteroid dig sites. Artifacts recovered from dig site excavations are catalogued, analyzed, and where possible, identified for safe study. Anomalous artifacts may exhibit unexpected or hazardous properties and require careful handling. Unidentified items should not be activated or brought into occupied station areas until their effects are understood.

**Xenobiology** maintains and studies alien life forms, principally slimes. Slimes require regular feeding; overpopulation must be managed through culling to prevent containment failures. Experiments on slime extracts produce a range of outcomes depending on slime color and extract type. The xenobiology laboratory has containment airlocks; these must remain secured when slimes are present. See the Xenobiology guide for the full extract reaction reference and slime behavior documentation.

---

## Equipment

Scientists spawn in the Toxins Research area with standard Science department equipment. Lab-specific tools include signaling devices, mixing chamber controls, the R&D console and fabricators, and excavation tools accessible via the research shuttle. The Research Director's access covers all Science laboratory areas.

---

## Reporting Structure

Scientists report to the Research Director. In the Research Director's absence, senior Science personnel coordinate laboratory activity.
