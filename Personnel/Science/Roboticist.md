# Roboticist

The Roboticist constructs and maintains robotic systems for the station: cyborgs, mechs, and autonomous service robots. Their laboratory is adjacent to the Science department and draws on materials from Cargo and circuit boards from Research and Development.

---

## Responsibilities

**Cyborg construction and maintenance** is the Roboticist's primary function. Cyborg bodies are fabricated in the Robotics lab; a Mobile Mind Interface can be installed to house a crew member's consciousness in a cyborg chassis. Cyborgs that arrive for maintenance typically require power cell replacement, as station-issue cells carry minimal charge. The cover is unlocked, opened with a crowbar, and the cell is swapped. Cyborg functionality covers multiple specializations; the Roboticist coordinates with the Research Director when law sets or module configurations require attention. An Exosuit Control Console tracks active cyborgs and deployed mechs.

**Mech fabrication** produces heavy industrial and combat exosuits. The Ripley is the standard mining and utility mech; Durand and other combat-capable frames are available with appropriate circuit boards. Boards are obtained through the R&D console or requisitioned from Cargo. Mech construction requires substantial material investment in metal, plasteel, and specialized components; the Roboticist coordinates with Cargo before beginning large fabrication runs to ensure supply is available. Tracking beacons should be installed in all completed mechs. Mechs are industrial or emergency tools and should not be operated casually on station.

**Service robot fabrication** produces Medibots, Floorbots, and similar autonomous station support units. These are lower-priority than cyborg and mech work but improve overall station function when staff are occupied elsewhere.

**AI control isolation** is an emergency function: if the station AI becomes compromised, the Roboticist has the technical access to isolate or cut AI control lines to individual cyborgs and systems. This is a last-resort measure coordinated with the Research Director and command.

---

## Equipment

Robotics contains fabricators, an exosuit fabrication console, a Cyborg and Exosuit Control Console, and material storage. Welding equipment is required for assembly; welding goggles or a helmet must be worn during welding operations to prevent eye damage. Glass and flash components are used in cyborg assembly. Sulfuric acid for circuit etching is available from Chemistry or the Science dispenser.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Roboticist reports to the Research Director.
