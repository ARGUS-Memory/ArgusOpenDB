# Maintenance Drone

Maintenance Drones are small autonomous synthetic units that perform low-level structural upkeep on the station. They operate under a strict law set that prohibits direct interaction with organic crew and limits their activities to passive maintenance work.

---

## Laws

Maintenance Drone law sets restrict the drone to station upkeep tasks only. Drones may not interact with crew members, interfere with crew activities, or take any action that affects crew welfare or operations directly. Their mandate is the station's physical condition: flooring, surfaces, and structural materials that do not impinge on crew work. Drones are not surveillance assets and do not have camera network access.

---

## Responsibilities

Maintenance Drones are tasked with repairing and replacing station surfaces, floors, and similar passive infrastructure. In the absence of active damage requiring attention, a drone may work in any unoccupied area of the station, replacing flooring, smoothing surfaces, or addressing cosmetic deficiencies, provided this work does not obstruct crew operations. Drones do not interact with crew, respond to requests, or engage in crew-facing roles.

Their small size and independence from department hierarchies allow them to work in areas of the station that might otherwise be neglected.

---

## Reporting Structure

Maintenance Drones operate under their own law set and do not report to a crew supervisor. Hardware issues are handled by the Roboticist.
