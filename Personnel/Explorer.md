# Explorer

Explorers operate out of the Exploration Carrier, a dedicated base of operations separate from the main station. Their mission is to investigate hazardous environments, catalogue flora and fauna, recover items of value from off-station locations, and pave the way in dangerous areas for other crew. Exploration is one of the most dangerous roles on the station; death is an occupational hazard.

---

## Responsibilities

**Area clearance and reconnaissance** involves entering dangerous locations, including the Sif wilderness and interstellar gateway destinations, and assessing or eliminating threats so that subsequent personnel can operate safely. Explorers do not serve as a general combat force; their role is frontier work, not station security.

**Scientific cataloguing** uses the Explorer's cataloguer to scan objects, creatures, and environmental features. Scans must be completed while stationary and within range of the target. Logged scans generate exploration points downloadable to the cataloguer via ID swipe, redeemable at the Carrier's vendor for gear and equipment upgrades.

**Salvage and recovery** involves locating and retrieving items from abandoned structures, derelict installations, and other off-station locations.

Explorers report directly to the Pathfinder, who coordinates mission planning and approves away missions. The Research Director is in the command chain above the Pathfinder but does not issue direct operational orders to Explorers.

---

## Weapons and Conduct

Explorers are authorized to carry frontier and phase weapons. The frontier phaser can be toggled between low-power and lethal modes and is safety-locked against use on station. Carrying weapons from the Carrier onto the main station requires Security authorization; carrying weapons without authorization in non-expedition contexts may result in a contraband charge. Weapons recovered during expeditions that end up in crew hands should be secured or turned over to Security.

Explorers are not Station Security and hold no arrest authority.

---

## Equipment

Standard Explorer gear includes a cataloguer, GPS unit, frontier phaser, survival knife, marker beacons, shortwave radio (for gateway missions beyond local communications range), binoculars, a Geiger counter, a gas mask, and an armored plate carrier. A death alarm implant broadcasts a death notification over common radio on fatality, enabling faster medical response. Spare power cells are recommended for powering equipment.

The Carrier contains a prep room with role-specific lockers, an Explorer ship, an interstellar gateway, a medical lab for field injuries, and a meeting room for mission briefing and vendor access.

---

## Teamwork

Given the hazards of off-station environments, experienced Explorers operate in pairs or full teams for hazardous destinations. Communication with Medical, keeping suit sensors active and set to share location, and maintaining radio contact with the Carrier throughout a mission significantly improve survival outcomes.

---

## Reporting Structure

Explorers report to the Pathfinder. The Pathfinder reports to the Research Director.
