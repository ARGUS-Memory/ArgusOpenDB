# Cyborg

Cyborgs are synthetic crew members operating under the station AI's law framework. Two cyborg units are supplied to the station at shift start; additional units are fabricated by the Roboticist from positronic brains or from the minds of living or deceased crew members. Cyborgs serve as extensions of the AI and follow its directives within their law set.

---

## Overview

A cyborg unit requires no atmosphere and cannot be incapacitated by pressure or gas hazards, making it particularly effective in breach and emergency response. Station-supplied power cells carry minimal charge; cyborgs should upgrade to a higher-capacity cell as soon as possible after activation. Cyborg recharge stations restore both power and consumable tool stocks over time.

Each cyborg selects a module before activating. The module determines the cyborg's tool set and department focus. A cyborg cannot change its module after selection.

---

## Modules

**Standard:** A generalist loadout covering basic utility, including a crowbar, fire extinguisher, stun baton, wrench, and health analyzer. Not specialized for any department; the least efficient option for focused work.

**Engineering:** Equipped for structural and power work without requiring atmosphere or a voidsuit. Carries a full engineering tool set (welding tool, screwdriver, wrench, crowbar, wirecutters, multitool, T-ray scanner, analyzer), a Rapid Construction Device, meson vision, materials (metal, plasteel, glass, reinforced glass, cable coil), a plasma cutter, sheet loader, and floor and pipe painters. Engineering cyborgs can repair dents on themselves and on other cyborgs.

**Surgeon:** A surgical specialist module carrying a full set of surgical instruments: scalpel, hemostat, retractor, cautery, bone gel, Fix-o-Vein, bone setter, circular saw, surgical drill, organ gripper, and an organ repair gripper, alongside a hypospray preloaded with Tricordrazine, Inaprovaline, Oxycodone, Dexalin, and Spaceacillin, a defibrillator, and NanoPaste.

**Crisis:** A mass-casualty emergency module. Carries a health analyzer, advanced reagent scanner, roller bed rack, ointment, advanced trauma kit, medical splints, a hypospray preloaded with Tricordrazine, Inaprovaline, Dylovene, Tramadol, Dexalin, and Spaceacillin, a large beaker, syringe, defibrillator, and organ and medical grippers.

**Miner:** Equipped for asteroid extraction. Carries an optical material scanner, mining scanner, advanced sonic jackhammer, shovel, drill maintenance gripper, and mining satchel. Does not require oxygen during mining operations. Cannot use standard cargo crates; materials must be moved individually from the smelter.

**Janitor:** Automatically cleans floor surfaces as the cyborg moves across them. Carries soap, a trash bag, a mop, and a light replacer.

**Service:** A utility service module with limited practical scope; carries a service gripper and bucket.

---

## Communication

Cyborgs have access to the binary channel, shared with other cyborgs, androids, and the station AI, using the ,b prefix. Depending on module, cyborgs may also access a department radio channel with the appropriate :X prefix.

---

## Repairs

Dented cyborg chassis are repaired with a welding tool. Burn damage (from fire or energy weapons) requires opening the power cell cavity with a screwdriver to access the wiring panel for rewiring. Engineering module cyborgs can perform dent repairs on themselves and others but cannot reach their own wiring panel for burn repairs. A cyborg that is non-functional but structurally intact can be restored to operation if brought to Robotics.

Returning a cyborg to a biological form is possible: the cyborg is destroyed, the Mobile Mind Interface extracted, the brain removed from the MMI with a robotics ID swipe, and installed into a prepared biological body before resleeving.

---

## Reporting Structure

Cyborgs report to the station AI and, through it, to the Research Director for hardware maintenance and module issues. Physical maintenance is handled by the Roboticist.
