# Clown

The Clown is a licensed entertainer aboard the station. Their formal role is to provide amusement to the crew through performance, pranks, and general comedic disruption of daily routine. Clowns are recognized by their distinctive costume: oversized shoes, bright clothing, and a mask that, by long tradition, is never removed.

---

## Responsibilities

The Clown's duties are loosely defined, as entertainment resists strict procedure. A Clown in good standing keeps the crew amused, maintains a certain theatrical energy on the station, and absorbs the social pressure that might otherwise fall on more functionally critical personnel. Their presence is considered a morale asset during long and otherwise uneventful shifts.

**Performance** encompasses jokes, physical comedy, improvised theatre, and station-wide pranks. Banana peels, cream pies, and the bike horn are the traditional instruments of the Clown's craft. Pranks that cause property damage, injury, or significant operational disruption cross into conduct that Security takes an interest in.

**Clown tradition** holds that the Clown maintains their persona at all times. The mask stays on. The Clown operates according to an unspoken social contract that grants them license to behave in ways that would concern other crew, provided that behavior remains within recognizable bounds of entertainment.

---

## Equipment

Standard issue includes the Clown costume, oversized shoes (which carry a slip risk for others), banana peels, cream pies, and a bike horn. Additional tools acquired during the shift vary by individual.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Clown nominally reports to the Head of Personnel. In practice, the degree of supervisory oversight applied to the Clown position is limited.
