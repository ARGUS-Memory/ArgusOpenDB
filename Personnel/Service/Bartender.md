# Bartender

The Bartender operates the station bar and is responsible for preparing and serving drinks to crew members. The bar functions as a social gathering point, and the Bartender's role extends to maintaining its atmosphere and managing the occasional disorder that follows heavy alcohol consumption.

---

## Responsibilities

**Drink service** is the Bartender's primary function. The Booze-O-Mat stocks a range of spirits and glassware. The shaker, with a 120-unit reagent capacity, is the preferred mixing vessel for complex cocktails. A menu displayed at the bar helps patrons select from available options.

**Bar setup** at shift start includes activating the EFTPOS scanner for credit transactions, setting the bar sign via ID swipe, stocking the shaker and refrigerator, and ensuring the Booze-O-Mat is accessible. The backroom refrigerator contains beer kegs, a beer locker, and additional supplies. Bartenders who wish to renovate the bar space should coordinate with Engineering for any structural modifications.

**Order and safety** fall within the Bartender's responsibilities on their own premises. A shotgun is kept in the bar's backroom; it is a tool for breaking up brawls and self-defense within the bar area. Taking it outside the bar is a violation of station policy without specific security authorization. An armored vest provides additional protection during altercations.

---

## Equipment

The bar contains a Booze-O-Mat, shaker, bar sign, EFTPOS scanner, refrigerator with kegs and beer supplies, beanbag shells, formal clothing locker, and the backroom with the shotgun. A formal uniform, black shoes, and top hat are standard issue.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Bartender reports to the Colony Director through the service department hierarchy.
