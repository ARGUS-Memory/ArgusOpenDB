# Chaplain

Also designated Counselor, the Chaplain provides spiritual and pastoral support to the station's crew. The role encompasses religious services, funerary rites, psychological counseling, and the physical care of the chapel and crematorium. The Chaplain has access to the chapel, the confession booth, the crematorium, and the morgue; station access beyond these areas is limited.

---

## Responsibilities

**Funerary services** are the Chaplain's most visible function. A stock of wooden caskets in the chapel serves for wakes and burial-at-space. A casket functions as a locker; the body is placed inside, a eulogy is delivered (over the radio when no crew are physically present), the casket is moved to the mass driver, and the driver is fired to commit the deceased to space. The crematorium converts bodies to ash for those who prefer cremation; clothing and personal effects should be removed before cremation. Space burials via the mass driver and cremation are both recognized final rites on station.

**Pastoral counseling** includes confession, informal counseling sessions, and coordination with the Chemist for pharmaceutical support where appropriate. The confession booth provides a private setting: the Chaplain and the confessor each enter a separate compartment and activate the radio terminals. Crew members who are visibly troubled or who have recently experienced significant loss may benefit from pastoral outreach; the Chaplain does not wait passively but makes contact proactively. Prisoners awaiting sentencing or serving brig time are a frequent source of pastoral requests.

**Religious services** take the form of sermons, hymns, and ceremonial observance appropriate to the Chaplain's chosen tradition. The Chaplain selects their deity, religion, and holy book appearance at shift start. The holy book may be loaned or replaced through the Library. Candles can be placed anywhere in the chapel or elsewhere and lit for atmosphere; they burn down over time. Crayons allow the Chaplain to mark runes and letters on the floor for ritual purposes.

**Religious traditions** recognized in the contemporary era include Unitarianism, Deism, Buddhism, Shinto, Taoism and Confucianism, and a variety of Colonial Religious Systems developed in the colonies. Corporate-approved traditions also include Technologism and the Order of the Great Lady. Atheism is widespread and respected. Faiths that conflict with NanoTrasen's operating values are not permitted for public observance on station property.

**Other ceremonial functions** include the investiture of acolytes, the dedication of equipment and vessels, seances, and pilgrimages within the station. The Chaplain may also organize services for recurring events.

The **Null Rod** is an artifact issued to the Chaplain that reportedly dispels certain anomalous phenomena. It reveals and disrupts rune-based effects and may have use in security-adjacent situations involving unusual occurrences. The rod should be used judiciously; it is not a standard tool for routine duties.

---

## Equipment

The chapel contains a podium, pews, a confession booth with radio terminals, a mass driver, a stock of coffins, candles, crayons, the holy book, and the Null Rod. The crematorium is accessible through the chapel.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Chaplain reports to the Head of Personnel.
