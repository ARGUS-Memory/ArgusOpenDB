# Janitor

The Janitor maintains station cleanliness and handles minor upkeep tasks that fall outside Engineering's primary responsibilities. The role covers cleaning spills, removing litter, replacing damaged lights, and monitoring waste disposal systems.

---

## Responsibilities

**Cleaning duty** addresses spills and contaminated floor surfaces. For small isolated messes encountered during routine movement, the Space Cleaner spray bottle provides instant, residue-free cleaning without requiring the mop and bucket. For larger spills, the mop bucket is filled from the water tank via the blue bucket, a mop is wetted in the bucket, and the floor is cleaned tile by tile. Wet floor signs must be placed near the work area before cleaning begins and left in place for several minutes afterward to warn passersby of slip hazards. A radio advisory helps prevent Security from logging an incident against the Janitor for creating a workplace hazard. Galoshes are worn during mop cleaning to prevent the Janitor from slipping on their own work. Hazmat-grade spills, such as radium contamination from Chemistry, require the bio suit.

Crime scenes must not be cleaned until the Detective has completed their evidence sweep. Cleaning a scene before forensic analysis destroys the evidentiary record and obstructs any subsequent investigation.

**Litter collection** uses the trash bag carried on the belt during all patrols. Right-clicking the trash bag sets collection mode: single-item or all-items-on-tile. Common litter includes cigarette butts, packaging wrappers, and discarded pens. The bag is emptied into a disposals bin, which then clears its contents automatically.

**Light maintenance** covers broken light fixtures throughout the station. Replacement bulbs are stocked in the Janitor's closet. The light replacer tool loads replacement lights from a box in inventory and inserts them into fixtures more efficiently than by hand. Burned-out bulbs removed from fixtures should be taken to an Autolathe for recycling and new bulb production. During electrical storms, the volume of blown lights may be station-wide; the flashlight provides visibility in darkened areas during mass replacement.

**Waste management** involves the disposals system. The disposals conveyor runs from the central waste collection point through the Cargo Bay belt before reaching the disposal center at the south end of Arrivals, accessible through a maintenance tunnel. The conveyor lever is set to REVERSE to direct items to the mass driver; the blast door is opened, and the mass driver is activated when waste has accumulated on the pad. Cargo Technicians must have their belt running for the waste flow to function correctly.

The Janitor's janicart allows faster movement through corridors and can carry a trash bag. The cart is driven by holding the keys and clicking onto it; it is exited by clicking with an empty hand.

---

## Equipment

The Janitor's closet contains a mop, mop bucket, spare mop, water tank, blue bucket, galoshes, bio suit, Space Cleaner bottle, cleaner grenades (for extreme contamination scenarios), trash bag, replacement light boxes, flashlight, light replacer, and janicart keys.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Janitor reports to the Head of Personnel.
