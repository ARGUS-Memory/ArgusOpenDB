# Chef

The Chef prepares food for the station crew using the kitchen's equipment and ingredients supplied by Hydroponics, Cargo, and the kitchen's own stock. Feeding the crew is the role's core function; the kitchen is well-equipped to handle a wide variety of recipes.

---

## Responsibilities

**Food preparation** uses the kitchen's microwave ovens, blender, rolling pin, meat spikes, knife, and gibber. The microwave handles most cooked recipes; a dirty or broken microwave requires Space Cleaner or tool repair before it will function correctly. The blender processes food items into liquid reagent form, yielding nutriment, plant extracts, and other compounds that can be used as ingredients or isolated via the ChemMaster. The gibber processes animal carcasses into meat chunks; it accepts non-human animals under normal operating conditions. Livestock, including cows and chickens, can be ordered through Cargo or grown through Hydroponics' biogenerator. Cows are milked with a bucket and provide milk after feeding on wheat; chickens produce eggs under the same conditions.

**Ingredient coordination** with Hydroponics is the Chef's most important inter-departmental relationship. Botanists supply vegetables, fruit, and grain through the shared SmartFridge. The Chef communicates ingredient priorities to the Botanist, particularly high-demand staples like wheat, tomatoes, and potatoes. Where no Botanist is on shift, the Chef has direct access to a portion of the Hydroponics trays, four tray bays and the biogenerator, through the kitchen.

**Recipes** are catalogued in the Guide to Food and Drink. Strict recipe adherence is required for microwave cooking; improvised combinations produce inedible results. The blender and processor are more forgiving: the processor accepts only recognized inputs, and the blender will process any food item, though results vary.

---

## Equipment

The kitchen contains two microwave ovens, a blender, a rolling pin, a gibber, meat spikes, a knife, a tray and cup dispenser, a SmartFridge, a ChemMaster, a drinks dispenser with some ingredients, salt and pepper shakers, and a shuttered service window. Starting stock includes eggs, meat, flour, space milk, soy milk, and universal enzyme.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Chef reports to the Colony Director through the service department hierarchy.
