# Botanist

The Botanist grows crops in the Hydroponics facility to supply the kitchen and, in some cases, other departments. Their work sustains the station's food production pipeline and supports chemical and biological research through specialty plant cultivation.

---

## Responsibilities

**Crop production** is the Botanist's primary duty. Hydroponics trays require regular watering, fertilizing, and weeding to maintain plant health and yield. The Botanist coordinates with the Chef on what crops to prioritize; high-demand staples for the kitchen include wheat, tomatoes, and potatoes. Harvested produce is transferred to the shared SmartFridge for the Chef's use.

**Fertilizer selection** affects plant development. EZ-Grow is the standard fertilizer and maintains baseline plant health. Left-4-Zed significantly increases potency but extends harvest times considerably; it is not appropriate for staple food production where throughput matters. Robust Harvest increases yield volume. Ammonia and diethylamine, available from the Chemist, are more effective alternatives to EZ-Grow for general fertilization. Unstable mutagen induces genetic mutations in plants, producing variant strains with altered properties.

**Plant maintenance** uses a standard tool set. The mini hoe removes weeds from trays through repeated application. The Plant-B-Gone spray kills plants selectively and can be refilled with other chemicals. Buckets are used to water trays manually. Weeds and pests degrade plant health over time; regular attention to tray conditions prevents crop loss.

**Chemical enhancement and mutation** expand the Botanist's output beyond standard food crops. Injecting plants with reagents from Chemistry produces altered biochemical profiles, altered growth patterns, or new physical traits. The full system of plant genetics, gene families, and centrifuge-based gene transfer is documented in the Xenobotany guide.

**Hydroponics access** includes the biogenerator, which converts biomass into food or other organic products. This supplements kitchen supply when Cargo livestock orders are unavailable.

---

## Equipment

Hydroponics trays, a seed storage unit, fertilizer dispensers, a mini hoe, Plant-B-Gone spray, buckets, and a SmartFridge shared with the kitchen make up the standard Botanist workspace. A toxin medkit is advisable given the variety of plant-produced chemical compounds the Botanist may handle.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Botanist reports to the Colony Director through the service department hierarchy.
