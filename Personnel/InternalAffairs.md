# Internal Affairs Agent

The Internal Affairs Agent (IAA) monitors compliance with NanoTrasen Corporate Regulations and Standard Operating Procedure across the installation. They operate independently of the Security department and report through the Chain of Command rather than to the Head of Security.

---

## Responsibilities

The IAA's function is observation, documentation, and reporting. They investigate suspected breaches of procedure, collect evidence, interview personnel, and produce reports for appropriate command staff or Central Command. They do not arrest personnel, do not direct Security operations, and do not take enforcement action beyond their advisory capacity.

Investigations follow the Chain of Command: concerns are raised first with the relevant department head, then escalated to the Colony Director if unresolved, and only forwarded to Central Command if the Colony Director fails to act or is themselves the subject. Faxing CentCom directly without first contacting the Colony Director is appropriate only in cases where the Colony Director is implicated or clearly unable to address the matter.

The IAA is implanted with a loyalty implant at shift start and is expected to conduct all investigations impartially and factually. Reporting should focus on documented evidence of regulatory violations, not personal opinion or conjecture.

---

## Equipment

The IAA's office is equipped with a fax machine, photocopier, paper shredder, and employee record access. Personal equipment includes a universal recorder for interview documentation, a camera, a secure briefcase for evidence transport, and a flash for self-defense only. Sunglasses are standard kit.

---

## Access

The IAA holds security channel access and command channel access for coordination purposes. They are listed under Security in the station manifest but are not part of the Security chain of command. They do not hold armory access.

---

## Reporting Structure

The IAA does not report to the Head of Security. They report through the Colony Director for station-level matters and directly to Central Command for matters involving command-tier personnel or serious regulatory failures. Internal Affairs coordinates with Security and Command but operates autonomously.
