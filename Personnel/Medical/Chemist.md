# Chemist

The Chemist synthesizes and stocks pharmaceutical compounds for Medbay and, secondarily, fulfills chemical requests from other departments. They operate out of the Chemistry laboratory adjacent to Primary Storage, and their output directly determines what medications Medical Doctors and Paramedics have available in the field.

---

## Responsibilities

**Pharmaceutical production** is the Chemist's primary duty. Medical staff require a consistent supply of drugs covering the four major damage types: Bicaridine for brute damage, Dermaline for burns, Dexalin Plus for suffocation, and Dylovene for toxins. Beyond those, Inaprovaline stabilizes critical patients; Clonexadone replaces Cryoxadone in cryo tubes for improved clone damage treatment; Alkysine treats brain damage; Imidazoline treats eye damage; Spaceacillin addresses infections; Tramadol manages pain; Peridaxon, though time-consuming to produce, heals organ damage. Ethylredoxrazine clears alcohol poisoning. Medical records should be reviewed periodically for outstanding prescriptions.

**Delivery format** is chosen to match the application. Pills, produced via ChemMaster, hold up to 60 units per unit and allow extended dosing since ingested reagents metabolize more slowly than injected ones. Pills are best for single-serve treatments and prescriptions; standard therapeutic pills carry approximately 15 units. Bottles hold 30 units and are preferred by physicians using syringes, since injecting from a bottle allows precise dose control in 5-unit increments. Injecting through suit ports is possible but slower than direct injection. Autoinjectors carry 5 units per device and deliver medication quickly on contact; they are typically loaded with Inaprovaline or Dexalin Plus mixes for rapid field stabilization.

**Mixing** uses chemical dispensers as the primary input method. Correct reagent combinations produce the target compound. The ChemMaster separates, buffers, and packages finished products into pills or bottles. Direct mixing via syringe or dropper allows fine-grained control when small quantities of a specific reagent are needed.

**Grenades** are a specialized delivery mechanism: a grenade casing accepts two standard beakers, allowing up to 100 units of combined reagent dispersal. The igniter is assembled with a screwdriver, attached to the timer or trigger mechanism, and then affixed to the casing. Beakers are loaded, the casing is sealed, and the trigger is configured with the casing on the floor. Medical grenades may deliver beneficial compounds at scale; use of grenades in harmful ways falls under corporate misconduct and security jurisdiction.

**Interdepartmental requests** are a secondary responsibility. The Janitor requires Space Cleaner refills. Botanists and Xenobiology may request fertilizer compounds. The Kitchen may request food-safe chemical additives. Virology requires radium. Science maintains its own chemical dispenser and sulfuric acid supply.

---

## Equipment

The Chemistry laboratory contains three chemical dispensers, three ChemMasters, large beakers, a grinder, and phoron sheets. A SmartFridge connects directly to Primary Storage for medication transfer. Chemistry lockers hold attire and additional synthesis equipment.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Chemist reports to the Chief Medical Officer. Despite historical association with the Science department in some references, Chemistry is a Medical department function.
