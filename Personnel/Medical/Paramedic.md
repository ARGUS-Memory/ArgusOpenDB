# Paramedic

Paramedics are field-trained medical personnel specializing in reaching injured crew members outside Medbay, stabilizing them on-site, and transporting them to medical care. Unlike Medical Doctors, their expertise is triage and extraction rather than comprehensive treatment; they operate under time pressure and in conditions that preclude full diagnostic workups.

---

## Responsibilities

The Paramedic monitors suit sensors and radio traffic to identify crew in distress. On receiving a call or detecting a critical sensor reading, they respond to the scene equipped for immediate field stabilization. If the affected area is depressurized, a voidsuit is required before entry.

**Field assessment** begins with a health analyzer scan. This is the first step regardless of the patient's apparent condition. The scan identifies the dominant damage types and informs the treatment sequence.

**Critical patient stabilization** follows a fixed priority sequence. Inaprovaline is administered first to halt deterioration and prevent the patient's condition from worsening in transit. Active bleeding is addressed with trauma kit application to the affected area. Suffocation is countered with Dexalin. Burn kit application reduces infection risk from open wounds, particularly in patients already in poor condition. Remaining brute, burn, or toxin damage is treated with available kits, pills, or injections to bring the patient to a transportable state. Tramadol is given if pain symptoms are impairing communication or assessment. Fractures are splinted before the patient is loaded onto a roller bed.

**Non-critical patients** who are conscious and stable receive on-site diagnosis and medication, with transport to Medbay still recommended for thorough evaluation by a Medical Doctor.

**Transport** uses a roller bed for mobile patients. A stasis bag is used for critical patients to halt deterioration during transit. On arrival at Medbay, the Paramedic briefs the receiving physician on what was administered, the patient's symptoms, and the sequence of treatment already applied. Failure to brief creates overdose risk if the physician repeats treatments already given.

When no emergencies are active, Paramedics support Medbay operations: monitoring the reception area and suit sensors, assisting with patient transfers between wards, performing triage on waiting patients, and checking in on recovering patients in the sub-acute ward.

---

## Equipment

Core field equipment includes AR-M glasses for remote health assessment, a health analyzer, a syringe for drawing and administering medications, a medical belt for bottles, a roller bed (folded for carry), and a stasis bag. Standard medications include Inaprovaline and Dylovene bottles, and at minimum one advanced medical kit. A compact defibrillator fits on the belt and is available from the EMT Bay. Additional medications from the Chemistry SmartFridge (Bicaridine, KeloDerm or Dermaline, Dexalin Plus) improve field outcomes. A voidsuit with internals is required for response to breached or depressurized areas.

---

## Reporting Structure

Paramedics report to the Chief Medical Officer and coordinate closely with Medical Doctors on patient handoffs.
