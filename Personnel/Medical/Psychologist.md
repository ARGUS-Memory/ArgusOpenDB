# Psychologist

The Psychologist addresses the mental health and psychological wellbeing of the station's crew. Unlike Medical Doctors, the Psychologist's primary tools are assessment, therapeutic conversation, and pharmaceutical prescription rather than physical treatment. Their access is limited to the Psych Room and general Medbay areas; chemistry synthesis is handled by the Chemist on prescription.

---

## Responsibilities

**Mental health assessment** involves meeting with crew members who present concerns or are referred by other staff, identifying psychological conditions, and determining appropriate treatment. The Psychologist has the authority, with approval from the Chief Medical Officer, to formally assess a crew member as unfit for their duties on mental health grounds.

**Cognitive Behavioral Therapy** is the primary structured treatment framework. It proceeds in three phases. During orientation, the patient establishes commitment to treatment, crisis response plans are discussed, access to problematic substances is restricted where necessary, and a treatment record is established. During the skill focus phase, the patient develops coping strategies, practices their application, and refines them with guidance. The relapse prevention phase reinforces the patient's independent use of those strategies. CBT addresses depression, anxiety, PTSD, substance abuse, eating disorders, borderline personality disorder, OCD, and related conditions.

**Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing** is a structured trauma therapy. The patient describes their traumatic memory, then revisits it while the therapist introduces a bilateral distraction stimulus. The therapist assesses the patient's emotional state, physical response, and distress level across a scale of one to ten. A score of six or below indicates the patient has moved into a manageable range. No more than three full sets should be conducted in a single session; subsequent memories that surface during treatment are processed using the same method.

**Pharmaceutical prescriptions** are an available treatment modality. The Psychologist may prescribe antidepressants, sedatives, and other psychiatric medications, which are synthesized by the Chemist. Paroxetine and similar antidepressants carry hallucination risk and should be prescribed under monitoring. For acutely agitated or violent patients, soporific injection provides sedation; chloral hydrate is a stronger alternative for unmanageable situations, effective at low doses but with a narrow therapeutic window. The effects of chloral hydrate can be reversed with sugar and Dylovene following stabilization.

**Psychotic and violent patients** require an escalated response. The Psychologist's first goal is safety; ensuring that a hallucinating patient does not harm themselves or others takes precedence over treatment. Physical restraints including a straitjacket and muzzle are available for patients who present immediate danger. Coordination with Security is appropriate when a patient is also a security concern. The Psychologist advocates for the patient's welfare in those interactions and informs Security of any triggers, conditions, or vulnerabilities relevant to a safe de-escalation.

**Post-resleeve care** is a common referral source. Crew who have recently been resleeved may present with disorientation, memory irregularities, or psychological distress; the Psychologist provides assessment and support in coordination with Medical Doctors. See the Non-Contiguous Memory Disorder article for clinical reference.

## Confidentiality

Psychological records carry the strictest confidentiality protections in the medical department. No command authority, standing alert level, or security warrant overrides this protection. The Chief Medical Officer may authorize disclosure in exceptional circumstances. Security may be informed of a patient's condition where that condition poses a direct, active threat; they are not entitled to general psychological record access.

---

## Equipment

The Psych Room contains a couch and chair for consultation, a chemical closet for pharmaceutical stock, bookshelves, and therapy aids. The room includes window tinting for patient privacy. Restraints (straitjacket, muzzle, handcuffs) are available when needed. The Psychologist coordinates with the Chemist for pharmaceutical synthesis.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Psychologist reports to the Chief Medical Officer. The role is classified as medical, not clinical science, despite the specialized nature of the work.
