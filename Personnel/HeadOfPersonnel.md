# Head of Personnel

The Head of Personnel manages crew assignments, ID access, inter-departmental staffing balance, and civilian department oversight. They are the second-in-command of the station after the Colony Director and assume acting command in the Colony Director's absence, with the exception that they cannot substitute for the Head of Security's role.

---

## Responsibilities

The Head of Personnel's primary function is ensuring all departments are adequately staffed. This involves monitoring crew manifest coverage, facilitating job transfers, approving or denying access requests, and coordinating with department heads when personnel gaps arise. The Head of Personnel does not directly run other departments but can appoint personnel to fill temporary gaps.

Job transfers require the Head of Personnel to verify the applicant's qualifications, obtain sign-off from the relevant department head (documented on a transfer form), and update the crew ID accordingly. Transfers should be recorded. Access changes that are not documented increase security exposure.

The Head of Personnel also fields crew disputes and welfare concerns that do not warrant a security or Internal Affairs response. Acting as an accessible point of contact for crewmembers below the command tier is part of the role.

---

## Access and Equipment

The Head of Personnel holds basic access to all departments, plus full command access. Full access to individual departments beyond the standard command tier should not be taken without necessity. The ID modification console in the Head of Personnel's office handles all assignment and access changes.

The office also contains Ian, a companion animal. Station tradition requires evacuating Ian from any facility undergoing emergency transfer.

---

## Reporting Structure

The Head of Personnel reports to the Colony Director and assumes acting Colony Director authority in their absence. They coordinate with all Heads of Staff but do not outrank them in departmental matters. Internal Affairs agents are not subordinate to the Head of Personnel, though cooperation on compliance matters is expected.
